# Romans 1:1-2 — Prophecy and the Credentials of Christ

**Text**: Romans 1:1-2; Psalm 22
**Theme**: OT prophecy as credential for Christ; double-entendre model of prophecy
**Status**: Romans 10:9 and Deut 18:20-22 corrected to ESV; prophecy table reformatted
**Flags remaining**: 1 ⚠️ ("68% accuracy" stat for modern prophets — needs source) | Rogers "108" → "more than 300" ✓; Torrey "cataloged 300" → corrected to argued-from-prophecy ✓ March 2026

Did any of you guys watch the debate this week? I did, and then the analysis afterwards. I don't normally watch TV but I was up until 2am the night of the debate watching people reflect on, and dissect everything that the candidate say. One of our duties as an American is to vote – its an important responsibility. I tell my little sister that if she doesn't vote she can't complain about politics until the next election. I told her I don't care if she tells me who she votes for, I'd rather her vote for someone I don't particularly like – and have a good reason for it – than to see her not vote at all. Any way, while I was watching all of this coverage I was thinking about the elections and obviously who I should vote for and why… I'm not going to tell you all who to vote for, but I wanted to share a bit of how I come to my conclusion on the subject. First and foremost I look at their public relationship with the Lord. Some men have deep relationships – but are loath to share about it in the public sphere. Others claim to be Christians because they think it will help them get elected. We obviously want a true believer in Jesus Christ. We want someone who is actively yielding their life to Christ and living in submission. So in your decision process look for a man of integrity. Someone who does what he says – even when its not popular, because he believes it's what God wants him to do. Stability of position over time speaks to this. If someone… lets look at a candidates record without saying his name, if someone was against abortion, then worked to pass a law that was really weak on abortion, then says he's always been strongly against abortion, then takes a step back saying he only wants to end abortion in the second and third trimester, and then says "all abortion is wrong regardless of health of the mother, rape or incest" – you might possibly be dealing with a man who lacks integrity. It's a whole different story if someone was for abortion, becomes a believer and is now against abortion. That's a sign of growth, of their salvation being real, of being yielded to Christ! But when a candidate has had a half dozen opinions on something over the past half dozen years you have to wonder whose pulling the guys strings – how much does it cost to have your opinion become his opinion? IF the guy is yielded to the Lord the other social isues will be in line with scripture also – murdering babies is bad, God says Homosexuality is an abomination, and so forth you know the bibles stance on these issues. If they are truly following God, the bibles positions – God's positions will be their positions.

So first look at their walk with the Lord. Second look at how they treat others. If everything else is equal we want a guy who can intelligently stand up for what he believes in. One of the candidates seems to me to have a chip on his shoulder and goes around being negative towards the other candidates, and even the broadcasters. If he thinks everyones out to get him – that isn't going to serve him well In diplomatic situations. One can be a strong, decisive and firm leader without being abusive.

Finally and of least importance is the fiscal issues. I believe a devoted follower of God can hold to fiscal policy that doesn't make sense. As believers we know that our God shall supply all our needs – according to His riches and glory. If he takes care of the birds of the field, we know he's going to take care of us. While like the rest of you I worry about candidates that want to be overly kind on certain things and spend too much money trying to help with certain issues both within and outside of our country; this final criteria isn't a deal breaker in either direction. But I prefer a guy whose going to manage the country like one might manage their household or business checkbook. Its not a perfect analogy. But it fits in many ways. If you can't afford to go to the movies – then stay home. Save your money and pay your bills. If the business truck you have is still working just fine in all the ways that matter, and you can't afford the truck payment then don't get the truck. Likewise I appreciate our involvement with aids in Africa, feeding the poor in scores of countries, hurricane help for Haiti, and on and on and on. We are one of the most generous countries in the world. I respect and appreciate that. But if we want to continue doing these things we need to keep the check book balanced and not spend more than we can afford. It kind of gauls me that we take out loans from China to feed our enemies. So in short: Loves God with all His (or Her) heart soul mind and strength, balanced leader, and loves his neighbor as himself in a sustainable way. If you aren't already – we need to be praying for whomever is to lead our country because it is a tough job.

Ok – that was free. Ready to start? (pray)

Romans 1:1-2
As we read scripture sometimes we find things that reference something that may have been common knowledge 2000 years ago, but may not be today. Vs 2 (re-read) mentions something just exactly like that. We westerners know there are old testament references – prophesies regarding Christ. But how many of us (other than Pastor Bob) can name a passage that has a prophesy about Christ in it? You remember the last few months we've talked about a number of people that were reflections of Christ remember those? Can you name some?
There are a bunch of scriptures we could look at – Isaiah 53 for instance is chok full of prophesies about Christ. However tonight we are going to be looking at Psalms 22. While you are turning there I want to talk to you about prophesy for a minute. There are a lot of people that get overly wrapped up in prophesy at the expense of the rest of scripture. I know a guy whose spent the last year of his life making a website that lists every prophesy inside of and outside of scripture. He scours the news every day looking for something to be fulfilled. Then he updates his database. See the problem? His focus is on the news, and his database and not as much on the scriptures. His focus is on the "prophecies" of modern day people. Speaking of modern day prophets I was reading where the average modern prophet is only 68% accurate. *(Source needed — verify before preaching. Do not cite without attribution.)* Scripture says in Deuteronomy 18:20–22 that you can know a person is a prophet because everything they say happens. If it doesn't – not only are they not a prophet but they will surely die. PLUS the gift of prophesy was for the writing of the bible. When the Bible was concluded the gift of prophecy died out. Any time I hear someone say "I am a Prophet" I cringe because of the biblical criteria, and because they usually don't know the standard they just applied to themselves. Apart from God its an impossible standard. With God its simple. HOWEVER, as prophecy was for the creation of the Bible, its safe to say that most modern day quote unquote prophets are not prophets in the OT sense. I met a guy a while back at CVS who was telling me "God said the world is going to end on such and such a date. After dropping the date, he said "now I'm not one to set dates". But that was exactly what he'd just done. Needless to say that date came and went, and everyone is still here. The world didn't come to an end!

The point is when God is involved in prophecy it is always true. When God is involved it is never wrong.

That said as we read this passage of prophecy I want to explain how you read it. Out of necessity, the passages usually are talking about two separate things. Are you familiar with the phrase double entendre? Think of it in terms of a mountain range. There are little mountains up close, big mountains further away. You can see both mountains, and if you were asked to describe the scene you'd describe both right? Same with prophecy. I remember my parents traveled out west a number of years ago as part of a trip to Alaska, they were flying into this major airport and from the plane they saw all these huge mountains. But ONE mountain (St Helens) stood out bigger and wider, and taller than all the other mountains around it. Most of the time a traveler to an area doesn't know the name of the mountains we look at. When we visted Gatlinburg UNLESS someone specifically named a mountain I had no idea what they were called. But this one mountain that they saw from the plane was so big, so imposing, and with a huge scar, that its identity was instantly known – it had to be mount st helens. Its like that with Christ too. Sometimes you have no other choice but to see that a given passage is talking about Christ. This passage is like that. Look past the little mountains and look at Christ as we read. I identified 11 references to Christ in the passage we are about to read. Keep track of them as we go, and see how many you can find. Adrian Rogers – whom you may have heard of – who helped pull our denomination back from liberalism – believes there are more than 300 prophecies about Christ in the old testament. *(Corrected March 2026 — prior draft said "108." Verified: Rogers uses "more than 300" in multiple sermons including LWF #2299 "The Christ of the Old Testament." The figure "108" could not be found in any Rogers source.)* The venerable fundamentalist R. A. Torrey argued at length from fulfilled Old Testament prophecy that Jesus is the Christ — his book *The Bible and Its Christ* (1906) is one place he makes this case. *(Corrected March 2026 — prior draft said Torrey "cataloged" 300 prophecies "in his book." Researched: Torrey explicitly declines to enumerate in his known works, writing "we cannot attempt in these pages" to list them all. The "300" figure is a general apologetics convention, not Torrey's own count. "Cataloged" characterization removed.)* I've never counted, but the important part is God put references to His son in scripture so that we would have flashing neon signs RE when, and under what circumstances he would come, how he would live his life, and a number of other things. Lets not overlook the flashing neon sign in Romans. Read with me Psalms 22. Actually before we do that, I need some volunteers to read a couple passages elsewhere in the scriptures. Dad I need you in Psalms 22, but anyone else willing to read?

That's just a handful of 300!!! Prophesies about Christ, and these 11 are all from one single chapter in the old testament. So now lets re-read Romans: 1 Paul, a servant of Christ Jesus, called to be an apostle, set apart for the gospel of God, 2 which he promised beforehand through his prophets in the holy Scriptures, 3 concerning his Son, who was descended from David according to the flesh.

See what I mean? Can you feel the weight of those scriptures? 300 prophecies about Christ.

One more short thing, and then we'll start to close. After I finally turned the tv off, and quit watching the debate, and resumed reading scripture. It occurred to me that these politicians questioned everything they could to try and get some leverage over the other candidates. It occurred to me that no where in scripture do we see people doing that regarding Christ's claim to be descended from David. Its almost like someone today claiming to be descended from all of the signors of the declaration of independence, Lincoln, Washington, Kennedy, Reagan, Billy Graham, John Piper, Charles Spurgeon, and all the other people important in our faith, or countries history. It's a huge claim, and no one ever questions it. I thought maybe some of you might have the same question so I looked it up and as it turns out the temple kept records of genealogies' so as to help keep track of whose land was whose. Every years they had a year of jubilee. If you had to lease your house to someone – that was the year you would get it back. If you owed money the year of jubilee meant you were now debt free! This is an important year and there were probably a fair number of people that tried to steal using this custom and so the records were kept in the temple. Who owed what to whom. Whose property was whose and how much they were being paid for it.

Likewise in heaven there is a book – everyone who believes in Jesus has their name written inside. This is called the lambs book of life. If your name I written in there then you are signed sealed and delivered. God knows the number of your days and when your number is up, and you leave behind your physical body – if your name is in the book you're going to go to heaven. But if it isn't you'll have to spend the remainder of your days separated from God.

I heard a joke the other day about a man that died and was given his choice of where to go in the afterlife. In truth once we leave this body we have no choice – if we know Jesus, if our name is written in the book of life we will go to heaven. IF its not, if we don't know Jesus then we won't. Its that simple. But any way, this is supposed to be a joke, so let me get back to it. This man dies, and is given his choice of an afterlife. He opens the door to the first room and sees people waving flags and running around dancing, and laughing while the worship team is trying to sing. He decides that isn't for him. Too much chaos and plus he has the voice of an ox, and couldn't carry a tune in a bucket. So he went to the second door. Inside was what looked like the typical inside of a Baptist church. A little stained glass here and there, a big cross. A pulpit and stage, and on the stage were a bunch of people in choir robes singing hymns. There were a bunch of people but they all sat in the back. He didn't figure this was his group either so he left and went and tried door 3 – inside were a bunch of well dressed people drinking coffee and eating donuts. The man dashed out the door and quickly found peter. He told peter he chose door three, and Peter tried to make sure the man was certain, but before he could get the question out the man had ran back through. He quickly went back to the room behind door three only to find the coffee and donuts gone, the whole room engulfed in flames and all the inhabitances being dipped into the Lava that had consumed the floor. One guy was chained on the floor, and so the man walked over to him and said "hey what happened to the coffee and donuts". The devil growled and said that was our coffee break, we only get one every 30, million years.

In truth though, there are no coffee breaks in Hell.

My nephew Wyley asked someone about hell the other day, and they gave him the whole truth. Hell is hot, it feels like you're constantly falling but you never stop. No one talks, everyone is constantly in pain, crying and grinding their teeth. My theology prof said that hell is a kindness to these people because in order to end up in hell a person has to be so adamantly against God that they would be more miserable in heaven than they are in Hell. The worst part for me I think is where scripture talks about the worms. It says the worms are always consuming (you) and that the worms never die. Can you imagine being slowly eaten alive by worms, except your flesh grows back, so that the worms can eat once more, and then your flesh grows back, and the worms feast again!? Imagine that for an eternity! If heaven is a perfect place of peace, justice, holiness, and rest, Hell is the opposite. Scripture says Hell is what we deserve! But God chose to send his son to die upon the cross for our sins. So that we didn't have to spend an eternity in Hell. So that we could go to heaven and be with God. God is good! Going to heaven is easy – scripture says while we were yet sinners Christ died for us, that was the hard part. If you want to go to heaven Scripture says in Romans 10:9 that if you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved! Its that simple.

You know which door you've been living behind. And you know if tonight's the night to change that. If God is tugging at you right now – don't wait. Tell him you believe. Tell him Jesus is Lord. Mean it. He'll hear you. He already knows.

*(Draft ends here. Close with gospel invitation / prayer.)*
