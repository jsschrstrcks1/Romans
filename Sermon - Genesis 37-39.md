## 37:2–26

37:2-5, summarize the dream, vs8:b so they hated him even more, vs9, (summarize joseph went looking for his brothers, and a man told him they were in Dothan. Vs 18-26

So back to the scripture. Were catching up with Jacob. Joseph is 17 now, and seemingly full of himself. But God is already speaking to joseph through dreams. Not just any dreams, audacious dreams. Remember from the last few weeks we talked about birthrights, joseph was not in line for the birthright. He was basically in line for the leftovers. But yet he keeps having these dreams where all of his brothers are subservient to him. Bowing and scraping as they say to their brother Joseph. It would be like being a teenager, and having your younger teenage brother announce one morning at breakfast that he was going to be the president of the united states, and that he was going to make you the mens room janitor outside the oval office. You or I might have fallen over laughing, and make it into a big joke. Bu Josephs brothers took these dreams deadly serious. That made me wonder why, and scripture is largely silent. However when I looked up the Hebrew words for "coat of many colors" I found out that the description of the coat as having been made of many colors is actually inferred. The Hebrew says "a wristed coat". That is another way of saying that his father had made him a coat fit for nobility. A common man would wear a cloak that stopped above the knees. It kept you free to bend and kneel and work. Like wise the common mans cloak would have stopped above the elbows. But having a wristed cloak meant that you wore a cloak like what the nobility wore. Their cloaks went to their ankles and wrists. Their cloaks were indeed made of many colors. This cloak then was symbolic of Jacob recognizing Joseph's role prophetically. Further this would have exempted joseph from work. One wouldn't sheer a sheep in a tuxedo, you wouldn't mop floors in a wedding dress. Joseph wouldn't have been expected to work in the same way his brothers were. However I don't know about you, but that wouldn't have been enough to make me murder my brother. So I came to a couple of possible conclusions. Either the brothers had some serious respect for the one true God, and recognized that the dreams must be from Him. Or else Joseph had dreamed other dreams that had perhaps come true. I lean away from the first conclusion, because we know that there was a fair bit of idol worship going on in Jacobs house. Remember Rachel stole Labans house hold and fertility idols? In one of the earlier chapters Jacob rounds up all the idols and hides them under a tree. Its something that persisted, this idolatry that Rachel brought into Jacobs home. Jacob tolerated it for years. It was something ingrained into their ethos, into the culture of their group. It was part of their way of life at this point in time, or had recently ceased to be. So while I imagine over time they came to have this deep respect for the Lord, I'm not sure that this was true at this point. So I think in all likelihood these were probably not Josephs first dreams, and that the others had come true. I think that when they heard his dream, and his interpretation, when Jacob recognized and agreed with Josephs dreams and gave him the outer signs of nobility, that they were quaking in their boots. Why else jump directly to murder? They were certainly jealous, but have you ever been so jealous you were ready to murder?

Any way so they hatch their plan. They see their brother walking over to them, and decide enough is enough. They are going to act. One brother urges caution, and they toss him into a pit instead. Then they decide to have lunch, and while eating their sandwhich, they see a group of traders, of slavers off in the distance. They pull him out of the pit, and sell him for what amount to be about $4 profit each, and then they seek to deceive their father.

It struck me here – you remember that verse that talks about the sins of the father revisiting the sons? It struck me here that Jacob had deceived his father using a goatskin from a freshly slaughtered Goat. This time, it is Jacob that is deceived when a goat is slaughtered. I noticed too that the kids didn't really lie, they just kind of allowed Jacob to come to his own conclusions. They handed their father the robe, and Jacob said "a fierce animal must have killed him". Jacob was deceived like he had deceived his father. Both Jacob and Isaac were grieved by their sons actions. Isaac was grieved when he found out the truth, while Jacob was grieved until he found out the truth.

So we have Jacob not spending a lot of time with his kids, not loving his kids like he should, except Joseph and probably Benjamin.

## Joseph as a Picture of Christ

Which brings us to our final point. So the first point was about spending time with, and teaching your kids discipline. The second had to do with Jacobs sins revisiting him. Finally I want to look at Jacob, and how he is a picture of the coming messiah.

One thing I've hoped you picked up on during our time in genesis, is that God painted picture after picture after picture of his son. All throughout the old testament there is this point to, and anticipation of the coming of Christ.

We saw it with Isaac when he was to be sacrificed. We saw it with many of the people we've looked at. This is a theme that continues throughout the entire old testament. Everything is point to, and contrasting Christ. The life of Joseph is no different.

Toss out some similarities here. He was sold for a slaves price. In Josephs day that was 20 shekels of silver, and in Christs it was 30. Both were betrayed by someone they cared for. Both were mistreated. Christ of course much more so scourged with a cat of 9 tails, beaten, crown of thorns upon his brow, but Joseph was tossed in a well, and then sold as a slave. Both were recognized as royalty. We now know that Jesus christ is the son of the king of kings and lord of lords. He's the master, creater, sustainer and provider of all that is, and all that will be. Jesus is his son. Prince of princes. Joseph was recognized by his father as a noble, through the cloak, and God recognized him as such through Josephs dreams. Ultimately these dreams were born out in the chapters to come. They ultimately crucified christ, killed christ upon the cross. Joseph came close to death, but was ultimately saved by the slavers. Josephs death ultimately brought salvation to his hungry family, but the death of Christ ultimately brings salvation to a starving world. In the end, joseph spent the rest of his life in Egypt – or you might say paradise on earth compared to any other country of the day. Christ spent three days in the grave, and then was resurrected. He came back from the dead, and appeared to more than 500 people before he returned to spend eternity in heaven with God.
