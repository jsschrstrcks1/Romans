# Evangelism & Missions Content Survey

**Purpose**: Catalog all evangelism, missions, and Great Commission content across the Romans repository for reuse in upcoming sermons (especially Romans 15:14-33).

---

## Primary Evangelism Files

### 1. evangelism (romans 15, mt 28, and rev 7).md
**Status**: Early sermon — rough and conversational, but rich with raw material

**Reusable Content**:
- **Adoption story**: Friend with 6 children (biological + adopted from Kazakhstan and China). "Fulfilling the Great Commission one adoption at a time." Illustrates God's heart for the lost — adoption as microcosm of divine pursuit.
- **Justin Gamble, Anthony Cox, Chip Hall**: Men whose lives were so gospel-saturated that "to know them was to hear the gospel." Could illustrate what it means to be a priest of the gospel (15:16).
- **Panta ethne breakdown**: Detailed Greek teaching on Matthew 28:19 — *panta* (all without exception) + *ethnos* (people groups by tradition/kinship). Already developed and clear.
- **10/40 window statistics**: Most missionaries sent to comfortable, already-reached regions. The frontier gets the least. Directly parallels Paul's 15:20 ambition.
- **Relief organization analogy**: 100 families' worth of resources, 10 million families in need. Illustrates the missions resource allocation problem.
- **NYC elevator story**: Bob Leko and Dave witnessing to a homeless man despite overwhelming obstacles. Illustrates faithfulness in discomfort.
- **2 Corinthians 2:15 — aroma of Christ**: Extended illustration. Christians should "smell" of the gospel.
- **Worship = evangelism**: "Making much of God" — two sides of the same coin.
- **People Group**: Balija of India (~2 million; 0.01% Christian)

### 2. romans 15 (2).md
**Status**: Detailed sermon notes with some polished sections

**Reusable Content**:
- **Yazidi genocide account**: Devastating story of ISIL persecution — 5,000+ women murdered, tens of thousands enslaved. Demonstrates the darkness Paul was walking into.
- **Hmong Bua / Hmong Daw**: Animist group. Neighboring Hmong Daw saw 20,000 come to Christ since 1994 through gospel radio. Shows how the gospel crosses barriers Paul couldn't have imagined.
- **"No escape clause" in the Great Commission**: Doesn't say "until you're tired" or "until work gets busy."
- **Garlic uncle story**: Uncle worked for a man who ate too much garlic — the smell permeated everything. Applied: Christians should be that pervasive with the gospel.
- **"Checkbook of our lives"**: How we spend time reveals priorities. Could sharpen application in 15:14-33.
- **Historical Rome context**: 32,000+ prostitutes in Rome's capital. The moral environment Paul was writing into.
- **People Group**: Hmong Bua (animist, literacy barriers)

## Evangelism Content Embedded in Romans Series

### Romans 1 (2024)
- **People Group**: Balija of India (same as evangelism file)
- **Providence illustration**: Fine-tuning of the universe applied to individual salvation. If God cares about electron resonance, He cares about each person.

### Romans 10
- **Florence Chadwick swimming illustration**: 15 hours in the water, 1 mile from shore, couldn't see through the fog, gave up. Human effort cannot overcome the fog of sin.
- **"Give Me Jesus" spiritual**: 19th-century slave song. Gospel's power across cultures and suffering.
- **Five Solas structure**: Entire sermon built around Sola Scriptura, Sola Gratia, Sola Fide, Solus Christus, Soli Deo Gloria.

### Romans 11 — The Remnant of Grace
- **Navajo Code Talker's wife**: Left alone in the woods during WWII, survived through hunting dogs. Thought she kept herself alive; God kept her. Applied to spiritual preservation.
- **Gold bar in glass box**: Japanese game — hand fits through hole, grabs gold, but fist can't come back out. Proximity to truth ≠ possession of Christ.
- **Election applied to witness**: "God has many in this city who are My people." Don't measure mission by eyesight.
- **M'Cheyne quote**: "According to the purity and perfection of the instrument, will be the success. It is not great talents God blesses so much as likeness to Jesus."

### Romans 15a — The Welcome We've Been Given
- **People Group**: Burmese of Myanmar (~30 million; <0.1% evangelical)
- **Four OT quotations** proving Gentile inclusion was always God's plan (2 Sam 22:50, Deut 32:43, Ps 117:1, Isa 11:10)
- **"Every Sunday when we gather, we rehearse eternity"**: The church's worship as practice for Revelation 7:9.

### Sermon — Today You Will Be With Me (v2)
- **People Group**: Shaikh of Bangladesh (135+ million; <0.1% Christian)
- **"Your job is the one in front of you"**: The neighbor, the coworker, the friend who has never heard. God reaches the world one set of opened eyes at a time.

### Romans 15b — The Mission in the Early Light
- **People Group**: Kabyle Berber of Algeria (~6.7 million; 99.71% Sunni Muslim; <0.3% Christian)
- **Priestly service cluster**: Three Greek terms (leitourgos, hierourgounta, prosphora) — evangelism as temple worship
- **Justin Gamble / Anthony Cox / Chip Hall** illustration used for "priestly service of the gospel" (v. 16)
- **Frontier ambition** (v. 20) connected to 10/40 window missions crisis
- **Jerusalem collection** (vv. 25-27) as cross-ethnic gospel generosity
- **Synagonisasthai** (v. 30) — prayer as agonizing combat, not casual intercession
- **Paul's plans vs. God's sovereignty**: Paul planned Spain, arrived in Rome in chains — gospel still went

## Key Greek Terms Already Taught

- **Panta ethne** — all nations/peoples (Mt 28:19)
- **Lēstēs** — bandit/revolutionary (thief sermon)
- **Opheilomen** — "we owe" / obligation (Romans 15a)
- **Bastazein** — bear/carry burdens (Romans 15a)
- **Proslambanesthe** — welcome / receive fully (Romans 15a)

**Used in Romans 15b**:
- **Leitourgos** — priestly minister (v. 16)
- **Hierourgounta** — performing priestly service (v. 16)
- **Prosphora** — offering (v. 16)
- **Philotimeomai** — to make it one's ambition/point of honor (v. 20)
- **Synagonisasthai** — strive/agonize together (v. 30)
