## Verification Flags

### Confirmed Errors — Must Correct Before Preaching

- ❌ **"He only ever has two sons. One son Isaac, and another by his wife's handmaiden."** — Significantly wrong. Abraham had **eight sons** in total. Beyond Isaac (by Sarah) and Ishmael (by Hagar), Genesis 25:1–2 records that Abraham took another wife named Keturah, who bore him **six more sons**: Zimran, Jokshan, Medan, Midian, Ishbak, and Shuah. Genesis 25:5–6 notes Abraham gave all he had to Isaac but gave gifts to Keturah's sons and sent them east. If the point is theological — that only Isaac and Ishmael are the "significant" sons for the purposes of this promise — that is a defensible point, but it needs to be stated that way. As a factual claim that Abraham had only two sons, it is wrong and will be noticed by anyone who has read Genesis 25.

- ❌ **"Its probably ba'al, and his consorts"** (re: Terah's idols) — The midrash does not name Ba'al. The story of Abraham smashing the idols is a real and well-known Jewish tradition — its source is **Genesis Rabbah 38:13** (attributed to Rabbi Hiyya b. Abba), with parallels in the **Book of Jubilees** and the **Apocalypse of Abraham**. In the midrash, Terah leaves Abraham in charge of his idol shop; Abraham smashes them all and places the stick in the hand of the largest idol, then tells his father the large idol did it. None of the idols are named. Ba'al is not mentioned in any version of this tradition. Simply remove the Ba'al claim and describe it as his father's household idols.

- ❌ **"Filthy rags" attributed to Scripture (ESV context)** — The phrase "filthy rags" is from the **KJV** of Isaiah 64:6: *"all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags."* The **ESV** renders the same verse: *"all our righteous deeds are like a polluted garment."* The meaning is identical, but if this congregation uses ESV, say "polluted garment" or quote the verse explicitly. Don't mix translations silently. Either quote Isaiah 64:6 ESV accurately, or note you're using an older translation at that moment.

- ❌ **Romans 10:9 paraphrase — "confess with our mouth the Lord Jesus Christ"** — The ESV of Romans 10:9 reads: *"because, if you confess with your mouth that **Jesus is Lord** and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved."* The confession is the declaration **"Jesus is Lord"** — three words. "The Lord Jesus Christ" is a common Pauline phrase from other letters but it is not what Romans 10:9 says. This is the key verse in the gospel invitation — get the exact wording right. *"Confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord"* is what the text says.

### Needs Clarification Before Preaching

- ⚠️ **Midrash source worth naming** — The tradition is real (Genesis Rabbah 38:13), and noting it as *"a well-known rabbinic midrash, recorded in Genesis Rabbah"* would strengthen it. The congregation would benefit from knowing this is extra-biblical tradition, which the sermon already handles well by saying "these are outside of scripture." Good instinct — just name the source.

- ⚠️ **Sarah's age of 65 at Egypt** — Correct arithmetic, but not explicitly stated in Genesis 12. Sarah's age of 65 is inferred from Genesis 17:17, where Abraham (age 99) says Sarah is 90, placing her 10 years behind him. Working backward from Genesis 12:4 (Abraham age 75), Sarah would be 65. The inference is solid. Consider saying: *"Abraham was 75 — the text tells us that plainly. And Sarah was likely 65, ten years his junior as we learn later in chapter 17."*

- ⚠️ **Romans 15:27 — "owe it to the Jews"** — The reference is correct. Romans 15:27 (ESV): *"For they were pleased to do it, and indeed they owe it to them. For if the Gentiles have come to share in their spiritual blessings, they ought also to be of service to them in material blessings."* However, the original context is Paul collecting a **specific financial offering** from Gentile churches for poor Jewish believers in Jerusalem — not a general theological principle. The underlying logic Paul uses (Gentiles received spiritual blessing through the Jews; therefore they owe material blessing back) is sound and applicable more broadly, and the sermon's point holds. Just be aware of the specific context if anyone asks.

- ⚠️ **Zechariah 12:4 — "Judah will know the Lord reigns"** — The blindness of enemies is confirmed: *"I will strike every horse with panic, and its rider with madness. But for the sake of the house of Judah I will keep my eyes open, when I strike every horse of the peoples with blindness."* (Zechariah 12:4, ESV). The blindness falls on the horses/riders of opposing nations. The phrase *"so Judah will know the Lord God reigns"* is not in verse 4 — it is the sermon's interpretive conclusion. That conclusion is broadly consistent with Zechariah 12:5 (*"the clans of Judah shall say, 'The inhabitants of Jerusalem are my strength through the LORD of hosts, their God'"*), but should be presented as the point of the passage, not a direct quote from it.

- ⚠️ **Orange grafting — "fast-growing rootstock"** — The broader illustration is sound and horticulturally accurate. However, "fast-growing" is the least accurate detail: **trifoliate orange** (*Poncirus trifoliata*), one of the most common rootstocks in Florida, is actually among the **slowest-growing** rootstocks. **Sour orange** is more vigorous. The important accurate elements — thorniness, hardiness, disease resistance, bitter/bad fruit — all hold up. Consider dropping "fast-growing" and emphasizing what is unambiguously true: *"the rootstock is tough, thorny, and resistant to everything that would kill the sweet tree."* The spiritual parallel doesn't need the fast-growing element anyway.

- ⚠️ **Romans 3:23 — tense** — ESV uses present tense: *"for all have sinned and **fall** short of the glory of God"* — not "fallen short." Minor, but worth matching the ESV exactly if reading it aloud as a quote.

- ⚠️ **Romans 5:8 — "yet sinners" vs. "still sinners"** — ESV reads *"while we were **still** sinners, Christ died for us."* The sermon uses "yet," which is the KJV rendering. Minor, but again: pick a translation and stay with it.

### Substantially Accurate

- ✅ **Midrash exists and is widely recognized** — Real tradition, Genesis Rabbah 38:13. Correctly handled as extra-biblical.
- ✅ **Terah fell into idolatry** — Confirmed by Joshua 24:2 (ESV): *"Your fathers lived beyond the Euphrates, Terah, the father of Abraham and of Nahor; and they served other gods."*
- ✅ **Genesis 12:4 — Abraham was 75 when he left Haran** — Exact ESV text confirms this.
- ✅ **Abraham lived to 175** — Genesis 25:7 ESV: *"These are the days of the years of Abraham's life, 175 years."*
- ✅ **Ephesians 3:6 — Gentiles "fellow heirs"** — ESV: *"This mystery is that the Gentiles are fellow heirs, members of the same body, and partakers of the promise in Christ Jesus through the gospel."* Exact match.
- ✅ **Romans 15:27 — Gentiles "owe it"** — Correct reference. See caveat above about specific context.
- ✅ **Zechariah 12:4 — enemies struck with blindness** — Confirmed. See caveat above.
- ✅ **Isaiah 64:6 — "polluted garment" / "filthy rags"** — Correct source. ESV wording differs from KJV. See flag above.
- ✅ **Romans 5:8 — while we were sinners, Christ died for us** — Correct verse. ESV: "still" not "yet."
- ✅ **Psalm 50:10 — "cattle on a thousand hills"** — ESV: *"For every beast of the forest is mine, the cattle on a thousand hills."* Exact.
- ✅ **Christ descended from Abraham** — Confirmed in Matthew 1:1: *"The book of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham."*
- ✅ **Orange grafting broadly** — Accurate that virtually all commercial citrus is on grafted rootstock. Thorniness, hardiness, disease resistance, and bitter/bad fruit of rootstock all confirmed. See caveat on "fast-growing."
- ✅ **Genesis 12:2–3 content** — Confirmed: land, descendants, great name, blessing to those who bless, cursing those who curse, all families of the earth blessed.
