# Adrian Rogers — Sermons on Habakkuk

**Source:** Adrian Rogers, *Adrian Rogers Sermon Archive* (Signal Hill, CA: Rogers Family Trust, 2017).

Four sermons, all preached on Habakkuk 1-3 as a whole book. Rogers uses a consistent 3-point structure across all four, with slight variations in the headings. These are whole-book sermons, not expository on individual pericopes — useful as thematic reference, illustration source, and for Rogers' characteristic sub-point framework.

**Index:**
1. [Don't Let an Election Steal Your Song](#1) — November 10, 1996
2. [Giving Thanks in Dark Days (0930)](#2) — October 17, 1999
3. [Giving Thanks in Dark Days (1100)](#3) — October 17, 1999
4. [How to Stay Together When the World Comes Apart](#4) — August 26, 1984

**Rogers' master structure (recurring across all four sermons):**

| Chapter | Theme | Rogers' sub-points |
|---|---|---|
| Hab 1 | A Perplexing Problem | Heaven's Silence / Earth's Sin / Hell's Success |
| Hab 2 | A Proper Perspective | Reliability of Scripture / Resource of the Saint / Retribution of the Sinner / Reign of the Savior |
| Hab 3 | A Profound Praise | Remember God's Glory / Rejoice in God's Goodness / Rely on God's Grace |

**Key recurring aphorisms (Rogers):**
- "Faith doesn't live by explanations, but by promises."
- "Faith doesn't live by appearances, but by providence."
- "Faith doesn't live by circumstances, but by praise."
- "Peace is not the subtraction of problems from life; it is the addition of power to meet those problems."
- "Your standard of living may change, but not your standard of life."
- "We may never know that God is enough until God is all we have."
- "Sin that used to slink down back alleys now struts down main streets" (attributed by Rogers to Vance Havner).

**Key exegetical notes (Rogers):**
- Two different Hebrew words for "cry" in Hab 1:2 — first is a plea for help, second is a shout/scream
- "The law is slacked" (1:4) — the word means *paralyzed*
- "Shigionoth" (3:1) — "with deep feeling and emotion"
- "Selah" — "pause and think about it"

**Key quotations Rogers uses:**
- F.B. Meyer: "If any promise of God should fail, the heavens would clothe themselves with sackcloth; the sun, the moon and the stars would reel from their courses; the universe would rock, and a hollow wind would moan through a ruined creation the awful message that God can lie."
- Thomas Watson: "Where reason cannot wade, faith must swim."
- Henry Wadsworth Longfellow (attributed): "The mills of God grind slowly, but they grind exceeding fine."
- Isaac Watts hymn: "Jesus shall reign where'er the sun..."
- Unknown: "If the fields refuse their harvest..." poem (quoted twice)

**Notes on usability:**
- Rogers preaches the whole book in one sermon — useful as macro-frame for the series but not directly transferable to individual expository sermons
- His "Hell's Success" / "Babylonian captivity of America" / political application framing carries contemporary political weight that would violate the voice profile's political guardrail. Use Rogers' structural moves, not his political framings.
- His "we are bad but not as bad as they are" critique of American self-righteousness is strong and portable
- The two "Giving Thanks in Dark Days" sermons (0930 and 1100 same day) differ primarily in length and illustration density, not in substance

<a name="1"></a>
## Sermon 1: Don't Let an Election Steal Your Song

**Date:** November 10, 1996, 0930
**Main text:** Habakkuk 1-3
**Key verse:** Habakkuk 3:17-18

### Outline (Rogers)

- Introduction
- I. There is a Perplexing Problem
  - A. The Problem of Heaven's Silence
  - B. The Problem of Earth's Sin
  - C. The Problem of Hell's Success
- II. There is a Proper Perspective
  - A. The Reliability of the Scriptures
  - B. The Resources of the Saints
  - C. The Retribution of the Sinner
    - 1. Material Corruption
    - 2. Moral Corruption
    - 3. Spiritual Corruption
  - D. The Reign of the Saints
- III. There is a Profound Praise
- Conclusion

### Notable Rogers moves in this sermon

- Opens with post-election discouragement as the entry point (November 1996, post-Clinton-re-election)
- Notes Habakkuk is addressed "to the chief singer on my stringed instruments" (3:19) — calls the book "written for the minister of music"
- "We live in a day when we have anarchy in the nations, apostasy in the churches and, God help us, apathy in the streets"
- On Hab 1:2 — the two words for "cry": plea-for-help + shout/scream
- On Jer 5:30-31: "the prophets prophesy falsely, and the priests bear rule by their means; and my people love to have it so"
- "The problem is not in the White House. The problem is in the church house."
- "This last election was not a referendum on one man's character. It was a referendum on the character of this nation."
- On Jer 8:12: "a generation of unblushables"
- "The law is slacked — the word slacked literally means, 'paralyzed.'"
- Argues the Church is "in a Babylonian captivity" by the world, flesh, and devil
- "God doesn't give Habakkuk, really, an explanation at all. He just says, 'Habakkuk, I'm going to do something; but it wouldn't do Me any good to explain it to you, because you couldn't understand it.'"
- On Hab 2:1 watchtower: "He'd been doing too much talking and not enough listening"
- F.B. Meyer quote on God's promises failing
- On Hab 2:4: "the reliability of the Scriptures, that's fact. The resource of the saint, that's faith. What you have to do is to put your faith in God's facts."
- Argues the five woes = material corruption (v.6), moral corruption (vv.12, 15), spiritual corruption (vv.18-19)
- On Hab 2:6 "woe to him that increaseth that which is not his" — applies against gambling: "Gambling is an effort to get what belongs to somebody else into your possession... All true business is win-win. All gambling is win-lose."
- On Hab 2:14: "For the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover the sea" — eschatological hope
- On Hab 2:20: "God said, 'I'm right up here, Habakkuk. I'm in My holy temple. There's no panic. The Holy Trinity is not meeting in emergency session.'"
- Closes: "Habakkuk began this book by asking, 'Why? Why, Lord? Why?' God never did answer why. He just said, 'I'm gonna reveal who: Me.'"

## Sermon 2: Giving Thanks in Dark Days (0930 service)

**Date:** October 17, 1999, 0930
**Main text:** Habakkuk 1-3
**Key verse:** Habakkuk 3:17-18

### Outline (Rogers)

- Introduction
- I. Faith Doesn't Live by Explanations, but by Promises
  - A. The Problem of Heaven's Silence
  - B. The Problem of Earth's Sin
  - C. The Problem of Hell's Success
- II. Faith Doesn't Live by Appearance, but by Providence
  - A. The Reliability of the Scripture
  - B. The Resource of the Saints
  - C. The Retribution of the Sinner
    - 1. Material Corruption
    - 2. Moral Corruption
    - 3. Spiritual Corruption
  - D. The Reign of the Savior
- III. Faith Doesn't Live by Circumstances, but by Praise
  - A. Remember God's Glory
  - B. Rejoice in God's Goodness
  - C. Rely on God's Grace
- Conclusion

### Notable Rogers moves in this sermon

- Written ahead of Y2K (the Y2K scare is the framing catastrophe)
- "Peace is not the subtraction of problems from life; it is the addition of power to meet those problems"
- On Hab 2:4 quoted three times in NT (Romans, Galatians, Hebrews) — "The just shall live by faith"
- On Habakkuk's progression: "First, he starts out sighing; then, he's seeing; and finally, he ends up singing"
- "We don't live by explanations — we live by promises"
- "If God did explain it to us, we'd say, 'Now, Lord, let me tell you a better way.' Who's going to counsel God?"
- Quotes Romans 1:24-32 as contemporary commentary on moral decay
- "The most powerful force on earth is faith in the gospel of Jesus Christ. The government can't make us good; only Jesus can do that. And changed lives are the only hope for America."
- On the five woes — same material as sermon 1
- On Hab 3:17-18: "What if everything goes caflooey with Y2K? That's what he's talking about right here."
- "How do you know you're not getting your joy from your job? Take away your job, and let's see if you have your joy."
- "We may never know that God is enough until God is all we have. And when we find out that God is all we have, we're going to find out, like Habakkuk found out, that God is enough."
- Closes with full "If the fields refuse their harvest..." poem

## Sermon 3: Giving Thanks in Dark Days (1100 service)

**Date:** October 17, 1999, 1100
**Main text:** Habakkuk 1-3
**Key verse:** Habakkuk 1:4

### Outline (Rogers)

- Introduction
- I. A Perplexing Problem
- II. A Proper Perspective
  - A. The Reliability of Scripture
  - B. The Resource of the Saint
  - C. The Retribution of the Sinner
  - D. The Reign of the Savior
- III. A Profound Praise
  - A. Remember God's Glory
  - B. Rejoice in God's Goodness
- Conclusion

### Notable Rogers moves in this sermon

- Later-service version of sermon 2 — more compressed, same substance
- Note from Rogers: "Faith is not smoke and mirrors. Faith has spiritual steel and concrete in it"
- "You need something rock solid. You don't need to be walking around on Jell-O and eggshells."
- On Hab 1:5 — God's "you won't believe it if I tell you" answer: "My ways are above your ways, My thoughts are above your thoughts" (Isa 55:9)
- Thomas Watson quote: "Where reason cannot wade, faith must swim"
- "If God did explain it to you, you'd say, 'Now Lord, let me tell you a better way.' Who's going to counsel God? Who's going to tell God what to do, or how to do it?"
- Romans 11:33-34 as the landing point for divine mystery
- "Get a grip on the Word of God. What has happened today is we have gone off in subjectivism, and emotional feelings, and ecstasies, and thoughts, and philosophies, rather than standing on the Word of God."
- "There used to be a time when preachers would say, 'The Bible says…' Then they started saying, 'The church says…' And now they just scratch their head and say, 'Well, it seems to me.'"
- On Hab 3:17-18 and the "yet": "Your standard of living may change, but not your standard of life"
- "If your joy is rooted in anything that can be touched or tampered with, you have an unstable joy"
- "I refuse to be an irrational optimist. I refuse to be a morose pessimist. I stand on the Word of God, and I will live by faith."

## Sermon 4: How to Stay Together When the World Comes Apart

**Date:** August 26, 1984
**Main text:** Habakkuk 1-3
**Key verse:** Habakkuk 3:17-18

### Outline (Rogers)

- Introduction
- I. A Perplexing Problem
  - A. God's Seeming Indifference
  - B. God's Seeming Inactivity
  - C. God's Seeming Inconsistency
- II. A Proper Perspective
  - A. The Reliability of the Scriptures
  - B. The Retribution of the Sinner
  - C. The Reign of the Savior
- III. A Profound Praise
  - A. Praise is Rooted in Revelation
  - B. Praise Recognizes Reality
  - C. Praise Results in Rejoicing
- Conclusion

### Notable Rogers moves in this sermon

- Earliest of the four (1984) — pre-dates Rogers' later refinements to the structure
- Framing: "Coming events, many times, cast a shadow ahead of time" — connects Christian tribulation awareness to Habakkuk's moment
- "We all know financial problems. We all know a heartache... we all have our own private tribulations"
- On Hab 1 — frames it as three problems: God's Indifference, Inactivity, Inconsistency (more compact than the later Heaven's Silence / Earth's Sin / Hell's Success)
- "America has been cursed with blessings. One of these days, perhaps, we will be blessed with cursings."
- "God is not necessarily protecting the American economy and the American way of life. God is in the business of getting glory to Himself."
- Preemptively anticipates objection: "You say, 'Preacher, I don't like that kind of preaching.' I don't like it, either."
- Notes that "The LORD is in His holy temple" (2:20) means "The Babylonians may invade the temple down here; they can't invade the one up there."
- "Quit arguing with God. Quit trying to tell God how to run His universe. God is in control."
- Three Selahs in chapter 3 — Rogers distinguishes them:
  - v.3 Selah: God's majesty (Teman, Paran, Sinai — where the Law was given)
  - v.9 Selah: God's mercy (the rainbow)
  - v.13 Selah: God's might (going forth for the salvation of His anointed)
- On praise and reality: "A Christian who praises is not Pollyanna. A Christian is a realist. He is not some sentimental optimist; nor is he a morose pessimist. He sees it exactly as it is."
- On Hab 3:16: "I am scared stiff. When I think about what's going to happen, my stomach churns, my lips tremble, my bones turn to water"
- On Hab 3:17-19: "If my standard of living changes, my standard of life is not going to change"
- "You make my feet like hinds' feet" — the sure-footed gazelle on the mountain peak "shouting 'Glory!' all the way"
