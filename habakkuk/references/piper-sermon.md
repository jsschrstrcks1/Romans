# John Piper — How Does the Gospel Save Believers? Part 3

**Source:** John Piper, *Sermons from John Piper (1990–1999)* (Minneapolis, MN: Desiring God, 2007).
**Date:** August 23, 1998
**Text:** Romans 1:16-17 (with extended treatment of the Habakkuk 2:4 quotation)

A Romans-series sermon by Piper in which the entire second half is exposition of Habakkuk 2:4 in its original context. The most theologically dense Habakkuk-2:4 treatment in the reference set. Directly load-bearing for sermon 4.

---

## Quick reference: load-bearing material for **sermon 4 (Hab 2:2-5)**

This is the highest-value single piece of source material we have for sermon 4. Piper does what the other sermons gesture at: he reads Habakkuk 2:4 in its own context (not just as a Pauline proof-text) and shows how the verse functions for Habakkuk *before* Paul ever picks it up.

### Piper's central observation on Hab 2:4 in context

> *"In the context of the book of Habakkuk the meaning of this sentence is just what it is here. The unrighteous nations are in great peril before the judgment of God... But Judah herself is not exempt from judgment. The wickedness and idolatry of some is about to be tried in the wrath of the Babylonian invasion. The crucial question here is: How can a person be saved? How can they gain their lives and not be destroyed in God's judgment?"*

Then Piper extracts two crucial truths from Hab 2:4:

**(1) Rescue depends on faith.** *"That's what Habakkuk 2:4 promises: 'the righteous will live by his faith.' That is, it's by faith that you gain your life. By faith you will not be swept away utterly in the wrath of God."*

**(2) Life is gained by faith, by a righteous person.** *"Habakkuk 2:4 doesn't say in so many words that we get God's righteousness by faith, which is what Paul teaches in this book and in Romans 1:17. But Habakkuk does link the righteous person and faith. The least we can say — and maybe we should say more — is that the thing about righteous people that brings them to life and salvation is their faith."*

### Piper's logic for why Habakkuk anticipates the doctrine of imputed righteousness

> *"If it is by faith that we escape God's judgment, and if, as Habakkuk 1:13 says, '[God, your] eyes are too pure to approve evil, and You cannot look on wickedness with favor,' then faith must somehow be counted by God as righteousness, because otherwise we could never be rescued by him and given life by him, because we are all sinners and he could never look on us with favor. So, if God can't look on any evil with approval (Habakkuk 1:13) and yet saves us and gives us life by our faith (Habakkuk 2:4), then our righteousness that has a standing with this holy God must be a righteousness by faith and a gift from him."*

This is a tight, two-text Old Testament argument that justification by faith is anticipated within Habakkuk itself. Hab 1:13 + Hab 2:4 = the doctrine of imputed righteousness in seed form. **This is portable directly into sermon 4.** It lets you say: *Paul didn't invent this in Romans 1:17. He read it in Habakkuk. The seed of justification by faith is in the prophet's own logic — God is too pure to look on evil (1:13), and yet the righteous live by faith (2:4). The only way to reconcile those two sentences is the gift Paul will later name.*

### Piper on "from faith to faith" (Rom 1:17 / Hab 2:4)

Piper offers two readings of *"from faith to faith"*:

**Reading 1:** Parallel to 2 Corinthians 2:15-16 (*"from death to death... from life to life"*). When the gospel meets faith in the soul, that initial faith leads to ongoing faith. *"Faith is the initial window of the soul that lets the light of the revelation of righteousness in. And when the light of God's gift of righteousness comes in by faith, it powerfully works to awaken and sustain and engender more and more faith for the years to come."*

**Reading 2:** The gospel saves believers by keeping believers believing. *"What saves is persevering faith (Mark 13:13; Colossians 1:23). If that's true, now it makes clear sense why verse 17 explains how God saves believers by saying that in the gospel God reveals a righteousness for us that is first perceived and embraced by faith, and then has the effect of awakening all the necessary future faith that we need in order to be saved. The gospel saves believers because the gospel keeps believers believing."*

### Piper's pastoral landing

> *"So every time the Bible demands you to do something do not think, 'I must do this to take away my guilt or to get forgiveness or to get a right standing with God.' Rather think, 'I will do this because my guilt is already removed, I am already forgiven, I already have the gift of God's righteousness, and so I know that God is for me and will help me. So I will trust him and obey him and display by my radical, risk-taking obedience the glory of God's grace.'"*

## What carries forward to sermon 4

1. **The Hab 1:13 + Hab 2:4 logic.** Piper's argument that the doctrine of imputed righteousness is in seed form within Habakkuk itself is the strongest single move in the reference set for sermon 4. The Reformed reading of Hab 2:4 isn't Paul reading something *into* Habakkuk — it's Paul reading something *out of* Habakkuk that was already there. Use this in sermon 4 to anchor the move from OT prophet to NT gospel.

2. **The "saved from God's wrath" framing of "live."** Piper makes the case that "the righteous shall *live*" in Hab 2:4 is not a generic moral life but specifically rescue from divine judgment. Cross-references: Hab 2:18, 3:16, 18-19, 3:13. Strengthens the pastoral force of the verse — Habakkuk is not making a general statement about religious living. He is naming the only ground on which any person survives the day of judgment.

3. **The "from faith to faith" reading.** Sermon 4 already plans to walk through the three NT quotations of Hab 2:4 (Romans 1:17 emphasis on *life*; Galatians 3:11 emphasis on *justification*; Hebrews 10:38 emphasis on *perseverance*). Piper's reading of "from faith to faith" as initial faith awakening sustained faith ties those three uses together into one coherent doctrine: faith starts you, faith keeps you, faith finishes you.

4. **Pastoral landing on how to read commands.** Piper's *"do this because my guilt is already removed"* framing is portable. The congregation hearing sermon 4 should leave knowing that obedience flows from imputed righteousness, not toward it.

---

## Filter issues

- **Christian Hedonism.** Piper opens this sermon with his characteristic *"two ultimate concerns: how to display God in our lives and how to be happy in God forever."* That's a Piperian frame that is broadly compatible with Reformed theology but should not be borrowed wholesale — it's his fingerprint, not yours.
- **The "Henry VIII / Bill Clinton / Genghis Khan" digression** in the opening is dated and culturally specific. Skip it.
- **Romans 8:30 ("those whom He justified, He also glorified") as the architecture.** Piper treats this as the structural backbone of Romans 1:16-17. Useful theology, but for sermon 4 (which is on Habakkuk, not Romans), don't let the Romans framing eclipse the Habakkuk frame.

## Verified quotations from this sermon

These are the most quotable lines, preserved verbatim and attributable to this specific source:

> *"All our hope for salvation hangs on beholding and believing the revelation of God's righteousness as a gift to us."*

> *"Justification (God's righteousness completely imputed to us) is the foundation of glorification (God's righteousness completely imparted to us)."*

> *"The gospel saves believers because the gospel keeps believers believing."*

> *"From God's side the key to our being saved is that he goes on revealing to us the righteousness of God in the gospel, month after month and year after year."*

All of these are usable in sermon 4 with attribution to "Piper, Romans 1:16-17 sermon, 1998."
