## Why is the Rapture Worth Studying?

The rapture is important because it is a distinct possibility that it is something we will experience. We should know of the rapture because God thought it important enough to mention. We should study the rapture because there will invariably be Christians that are curious about the passages in question – and its important that we be prepared to answer the tougher questions. Questions regarding the Eschaton generally are more difficult. As with any topic there are a wide variety of views on the subject. Wider than the scope of this paper, so in the interest of brevity and succinctness we will pre-suppose a pre-millennial position, and only explore the mainstream rapture positions within this eschatological framework. There are at least four, though we will focus on three: Pre-Wrath (sometimes conflated with Mid-Tribulation, but a distinct position — developed by Robert Van Kampen and popularized by Marvin Rosenthal in *The Pre-Wrath Rapture of the Church*, 1990; places the rapture near the 6th seal, later than the midpoint), Pre-Tribulation, and Post Tribulation. As is the case with anyone who writes on a question of theology this author has certain pre-conceptions that he will try to set aside in an effort to earnestly and honestly evaluate the three positions previously mentioned in light of scripture, and then having read the scripture we will examine the writings of a number of great minds who have studied this deeply and scoured the scriptures for clues as to the timing of this event.

- **"Pre-Wrath (previously called Mid-Tribulation)"** *(needs clarification)*: Pre-Wrath and Mid-Tribulation are related but distinct positions and should not be treated as the same view:
  - **Mid-Tribulation**: Places the rapture at the literal 3.5-year midpoint of the tribulation. Associated with Norman Harrison (*The End*, 1941) and G.H. Lang.
  - **Pre-Wrath**: Developed by Robert Van Kampen in the late 1980s and popularized by Marvin Rosenthal (*The Pre-Wrath Rapture of the Church*, 1990). Places the rapture not at the midpoint but later — near the cosmic signs of the 6th seal — arguing that God's wrath does not begin until that point, and the church is raptured just before it. The two positions share a rejection of pre-trib and post-trib but differ meaningfully in timing and in their reading of the seal judgments. Revise the parenthetical before publishing.
