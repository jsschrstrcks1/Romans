# Hebrews 3–4 — Christ Is Superior to Moses (Raw Early Draft)

*Raw transcript, likely early 2010s (Glenn Beck reference, Grace as puppy in training). Covers Hebrews 3–4 broadly: Christ's superiority to Moses and Christ's rest. Distinct from the other Hebrews 3:12–4:16 sermon (`Heb 3.md` / `Sermon - Rest in the Lord.md`), which focuses on accountability and rest with different illustrations. Several Scripture reference errors and historical imprecisions flagged below.*

## Introduction

Christ is Superior to Moses.

The jews tended to get hung up on individuals from their history. They even talked about God through the lens of their forebearers. The God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob.

We tend to do the same thing don't we? How many of us spend more time listening to Glenn Beck in the average week than talking to God or reading His word?

I like to think of Christians as wind up toys. You remember those old metal toys, you wind them up, and let them go? Then having exhausted their wind, they would stop, and you'd have to wind them up again? We likewise are wound up by spending time with the Lord, by reading his word, by going to church. Were like a hot coal, if you set it on the hearth it will eventually cool off.

If you're only having your spring wound once weekly, don't be surprised when you struggle to follow the Lord through the week. We need to have our springs wound constantly. We should always be praying.

Pray without ceasing says 1 Thess 5:16-18, rejoice in everything, because this is God's will for your life! ⚠️ **Paraphrase, not ESV. ESV order: "Rejoice always" (v.16), "pray without ceasing" (v.17), "give thanks in all circumstances; for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus for you" (v.18).**

I heard a guy we all know well preach one time that a Christian is like a flashlight. Take out the batteries, and the Christian emits no light! Same principal.

If you are not constantly connected to Christ you're like the toy that needs winding, the flashlight with no batteries, and the coal left to cool on their hearth. God in his sovereignty can use you any way, but you will have to struggle a lot more in the process. Like Jonah who ran from God. God used him any way, but I bet in retrospect Jonah would have rather skipped the fish ride.

By the way (update on Grace). Were back to actively training. She is still very much the mess puppy. But she's bright enough to be able to predict what were going to do next. She's also independent enough to decide whether she wants to do that or not. I walk two houses down to my parents a couple times a day. Its good for her I think to get some exercise in, to socialize with the other dogs, and so forth. However she's getting to where she would rather stay in their yard sometimes. Once she refused to go through the gate. I had to get a leash and pull her through. Now I frequently keep her on a leash when we go back and forth. The same was true of Jonah, and will be true of us. God's will WILL happen. But whether we get to be a willing partner, or cause God to deal more harshly with us is a whole different story.

God rewards obedience and punishes rebellion.

Gideon (as we talked about briefly a while back) was an example of obedience.

Moses was disobedient to God. ~~Moses was supposed to touch the stone with his staff. Instead in his anger he struck the stone.~~ ⚠️ **Numbers 20:8-12: God told Moses to speak to the rock, not touch it with his staff. Moses struck the rock twice in anger. The error is "touch/stone" vs. "speak to the rock."** Because of his disobedience he was allowed to see the promised land, but barred from entry.

## Christ Is Superior to Moses

This section is basically comparing Christianity to Judaism. Moses being the figurehead of Judaism, and Christ of Christianity. The author seems to be saying "How can you go back to Judaism when Christ is so much better!"

- He does most every miracle God did throughout the OT (Chapter 1) ⚠️ **Imprecise — Hebrews 1 is about Christ's divine nature and superiority to angels, not about miracles**
- He is better than the angels (Chapter 2)
- And now He's better than Moses (Chapter 3)

Christ was sent while Moses was called.
Christ the High Priest, Moses was not.
Christ's ministry related to a person's eternity, Moses' ministry related to a nation's earthly future.
Moses was an apostle of the Law, while Christ was the author — the *archēgos* — of salvation by grace. ⚠️ **ἀρχηγός (archēgos) in Hebrews 2:10; ESV translates as "founder" of salvation, not "author"**
Moses gave his life getting his people to the Promised Land, but was barred from entry. Christ gave his life getting his people to heaven, where we will be with him for eternity.

## Christ's Ministry Is Superior

This would have rocked their world. It would be as mind boggling and borderline offensive as though someone walked in here and declared communism was better than democracy.

Moses was a servant while Christ was the Son.

Old Testament ministry was one of bondage and servitude.
- Daniel — served his way to the top
- Joseph — served his way to the top
- Moses — floated his way down the Nile, clear to the top

~~Paul calls the Law (in Galatians 2:4) a yoke of bondage.~~ ⚠️ **"Yoke of slavery" is Galatians 5:1** ("For freedom Christ has set us free; stand firm therefore, and do not submit again to a yoke of slavery." ESV). **Galatians 2:4 mentions "slavery" in the context of false brothers, but the "yoke" phrase is 5:1.**

Jesus came from the top, and set us free from the law, and from sin, death and Satan. Jesus' ministry was one of grace and reconciliation. Moses' ministry pointed to Christ.

## Christ's Rest Is Superior

Rest is a major theme in chapter 4. Lots of allegory in this chapter.

Jews were in bondage in Egypt like sinners are in bondage in the world. God redeemed Israel by the blood of the spotless lamb. God redeemed sinners with the blood of Christ his perfect Son.

Because of Israel's rejection of the "minority report" —

*Movie illustration: Minority Report (2002, Spielberg). Three precogs (children of drug addicts whose parents' drug use gave them precognitive abilities) were kept in a semi-conscious state. Their visions were used to predict murders before they happened. But the three didn't always agree — one sometimes had a different picture, and because the other two disagreed, that report was set aside.* ⚠️ **Minor details: the precogs were kept semi-conscious in a pool, not fully "unconscious"; "computers can read thoughts now" is an overstatement — brain-computer interfaces exist but cannot read thoughts in the film's sense.**

It was like that with Israel's spies. 10 said one thing, 2 said "trust God, we can do this." Israel disobeyed and had a 40 year wander in a desert that they should have been able to cross in a week and a half. ⚠️ **Deuteronomy 1:2 says "It is eleven days' journey from Horeb by the way of Mount Seir to Kadesh-barnea" — "week and a half" is consistent.** Had they obeyed God, they would have been in the promised land in days, and owners of it probably within the month. But they doubted God, and rebelled. So instead of rest they got punished.

Jonah did the same thing — instead of obeying God and going to the dirty Ninevites, he rebelled, and God had to pick him up in a one of a kind sushi taxi. It only took him three days to preach the Gospel, after which he could have had rest, but instead he probably doubled the amount of time, and likely would have had bleached skin for the rest of his life. His rebellion ultimately cost him. ⚠️ **Bleached skin is popular sermon tradition, not from Scripture. Speculative.**

But we're not under the Law any more. We're under grace because of Christ. He took the worst of our punishments.

Christ doesn't ask us to go through anything worse than he went through. Christ asks us to go through less than we deserve.

Freedom from sin doesn't always mean freedom from the scars sin leaves behind.
- They can be seen in our kids' actions
- In their choice of spouse
- They can be seen in our own lives in terms of our flesh being drawn towards pet sins

Sometimes people addicted to drugs accept Christ and never have an interest in drugs again, however more often God gives us the opportunity to do his will in spite of our weakness.

Even Paul had something he struggled with — "that which I want to do I do not do, that which I hate I do." ⚠️ **Paraphrase of Romans 7:15. ESV: "For I do not do what I want, but I do what I hate."**

But Paul said ~~shall we continue in Sin that grace may abound, may it never be!~~ ⚠️ **Romans 6:1-2. "May it never be" is NASB. ESV: "By no means!"**

> No temptation has overtaken you that is not common to man. God is faithful, and he will not let you be tempted beyond your ability, but with the temptation he will also provide the way of escape, that you may be able to endure it.
> — 1 Corinthians 10:13 (ESV) ✓

Rest then is found in the context of a relationship with Jesus. Run to him, rejoice in him, cling to him. When you feel down, and discouraged, or when you feel tempted, or whenever anything comes along that you don't think you can bear — run to Jesus.

## Closing: Five Disciplines

Whether we are in the midst of trouble or not, we should be participating in a relationship with God daily through these things:

**Reading our Bible** — God's word will keep you from sin, or sin will keep you from God's word.

**Praying** —

> Rejoice always, pray without ceasing, give thanks in all circumstances; for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus for you.
> — 1 Thessalonians 5:16-18 (ESV) ✓

**Worship** —

> Finally, brothers, whatever is true, whatever is honorable, whatever is just, whatever is pure, whatever is lovely, whatever is commendable, if there is any excellence, if there is anything worthy of praise, think about these things. What you have learned and received and heard and seen in me — practice these things, and the God of peace will be with you.
> — Philippians 4:8-9 (ESV) ✓

**Fellowship with other believers** — Go to church.

**Evangelism** —

> Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you. And behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age.
> — Matthew 28:19-20 (ESV) ✓

If you're doing these things, you are keeping the battery in the flashlight, you're keeping the little metal toy wound up, and you're keeping the hot coal near enough to the fire that it stays hot. What use is a flashlight without batteries? What use is coal without fire?

Maybe you are that coal that's been apart from the Lord and had a chance to cool off. In a few moments the praise team is going to sing a song — if that's you, come up and talk to me.

Maybe you don't yet know Christ — if so, come talk to me.

Maybe you want to join the church — if so, won't you come?

Maybe you've never met Jesus Christ as your Lord and savior. If that's you, come talk to me! Jesus has a gift for you — he has peace, and rest.

Maybe you just need some prayer. Won't you come?

The sermon prescribes five generic disciplines without acknowledging the text's own answer to its own problem.

### 14. Surgical Fixes (5)

**Fix 1 — Integrate the integrity log's corrections into the sermon body before the next use.**
The Hebrews 1 mischaracterization, the Galatians citation error, and the Moses/rock imprecision should be corrected in the preaching text itself. The log has done its work; now let the sermon reflect it. Do not preach the annotated version — preach the corrected version.

**Fix 2 — Rebuild the sermon body around Hebrews 3:7–19.**
The section headings suggest two sermon topics; the actual textual work should begin at verse 7 rather than stopping there. Read Psalm 95:7–11 aloud in the sermon. Explain what "Today" means — the urgency of the present moment before the heart calcifies. This is the passage's center of gravity.

**Fix 3 — Replace "He does most every miracle God did throughout the OT" (line 39) with a two-sentence accurate summary of Hebrews 1.**
Hebrews 1 argues that Christ is the divine Son through whom God spoke, who sustains all things by his word, who is superior to the angels in nature and name. That is the argument. It can be stated accurately in one sentence.

**Fix 4 — Develop the "Christ's Rest Is Superior" section from Hebrews 4:1–11 rather than from the Minority Report.**
The text itself makes the argument: the rest that remained after Joshua's conquest was not the ultimate rest, because Psalm 95 still says "Today" — meaning rest is still available, and still at risk of being missed. The Sabbath rest of God (4:9–10) is the sermon's theological destination. This is better than the film illustration and requires no scaffolding.

**Fix 5 — Replace the five generic disciplines with Hebrews 3:13 as the specific congregational application.**
"But exhort one another every day, as long as it is called 'today,' that none of you may be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin." This verse does exactly what the five disciplines are trying to do — it provides a concrete, daily, communal practice — but it is native to the text, carries divine authority, and addresses the sermon's own stated concern about spiritual drift. The disciplines can remain as illustrations of what daily exhortation might look like; Hebrews 3:13 should be the command they illustrate.
