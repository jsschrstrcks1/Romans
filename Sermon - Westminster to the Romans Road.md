I love a lot of the people and things that Tozer quotes in this chapter. One of which is the Westminster Confession of Faith. You know how we as Baptists have the Baptist Faith and Message? Ours was written as the bare minimum necessary to cooperate as churches, where the Presbyterians wrote theirs as "believe this or else you're not a Presbyterian." Some forms of Presbyterianism have wandered away from the truths of scripture, and are allowing women, gays, and lesbians to be pastors — among other things — because they have unpinned themselves from both the Westminster and from scripture. They no longer see scripture as wholly without error. So as their view of scripture became polluted by their own folly, they began to see Westminster differently also, and backed away from both. Scripture is better than Westminster, for sure. But these standards — like the Baptist Faith and Message — help hold us to scripture, and so are important. Westminster is a great one. The 2nd London Baptist Confession is one of the best ones our denomination has produced, way back in 1689.

In Presbyterian churches that haven't wandered too far, they still teach the Westminster to kids and make them memorize it as part of their education. I think it's a sad thing that we have largely lost that as Baptists.

I won't say I agree 100% with Westminster. They advocate for baptizing babies because it brings them into the covenant family, they say. Which doesn't save kids. But they think it makes it easier for kids later to be saved. If that were true, wouldn't we see some evidence of this in their denomination? If baptizing kids made the kids more likely to be saved — and I don't think it does — wouldn't we see more people in Presbyterian churches, and not less? Yet in the last twenty years, the largest and most liberal of the Presbyterian denominations has shrunk from roughly 3 million people to less than 1.5 million. They are failing to hold onto their kids worse than we as Baptists are. On the Southern Baptist rolls we have about 14–15 million members, making us the largest Protestant denomination. Yet even we are shrinking — as a denomination we have shrunk by about 1% over the last year.

Why do you think that is?

One of the keys I think is that the year prior we were growing. The white Anglo-Saxon Protestant demographic shrank two years running, but the Black segment grew in 2017 and not in 2018. One of the big things that happened was the introduction of a side issue to the gospel in those communities. They are focused on this idea of being "woke." What is it to be a Black church? But it's gone further than that and become focused more on race than on Christ, as far as I can tell. Meanwhile the white segment has been shrinking for a long time. The problem of being sidetracked is the same, but the issue is different.

I watched a livestream of the Southern Baptist Convention a few years back. One guy got up and preached his heart out, kept me enthralled for a solid hour, and at the end gave a passionate plea for missionaries in the US and abroad. People were talking throughout — you could hear the murmur of voices. At the end there was a smattering of light applause. The next speaker was a politician coming to curry favor with Christians. Rah rah America, let's make money, and the cheers were deafening — a standing ovation. The pastors in the room were more concerned with politics than with the gospel.

Maybe it's not always like that. But if you knew your kid was going to die tomorrow, would it be more important to share the gospel with them, or to talk politics? Which one has eternal significance for your loved one?

I know where I would rather spend my time, and it's not on politics and politicians. Those things are important. But not as important as where our loved ones will spend eternity. I almost don't talk politics with people anymore, things are so charged. What if in speaking our politics we are killing our witness? This is true in many other countries. In France, the Republican Party of America is looked down upon by the church because they believe we are neglecting the more powerful avenues of helping the downtrodden. They believe that as Republicans we prioritize money over people and over the gospel. England is little different. In fact an Englishman told me — and a Google search verified — that the average Christian, as a whole, spends more money on dog food than on the gospel. *(Statistic widely circulated; original verified source unknown — see source notes.)* Dog food is important, and we need to take care of our animals. But it gives you an idea of just how little emphasis the church in America is putting on the gospel.

Once people are saved and being discipled, they will be taught what the Bible says about homosexuality, about being fiscally responsible, about what a church does to take care of people that need help — those that need a hand up, a little benevolence.

The most important thing we can do with our lives is share the gospel. So all of this seemed like a natural springboard into a time of talking about the sharing of the gospel.

I use a modified Romans Road approach. I don't share all of the verses that are traditionally included — not because they aren't worthwhile, but because there are a lot of styles floating around in my head, and parts of this style or that style come out alongside the framework of the Romans Road.

**Verses I use:**
- Romans 3:10
- Romans 3:23
- Romans 5:12
- Romans 6:23
- John 3:16
- Ephesians 2:8–9
- Romans 10:9–10
- Romans 10:13 *(listed as 10:12 in source — see source notes)*

There are other places you can go, but that's a good start. I found a sample presentation that someone wrote that is pretty good *(unattributed — source unknown; see source notes)*, and I'm including it here along with a Romans Road bookmark for you to hang onto as a cheat sheet.

When it comes to the end, people get worried about the sinner's prayer. They are worried about making sure people say all the right things. In truth, it's not the prayer that saves them. If God has already been working in them and they are willing to pray that prayer, then they are saved before they pray it. Romans 10:9 says confess with your mouth and believe in your heart. If they aren't believing in their heart then it doesn't matter what they say — there is nothing adequate they could say that would save them. But if God is working in their heart, confessing him as savior doesn't even have to happen in prayer. If they believe already then "Jesus is Lord" is enough. However, when I walk someone through the sinner's prayer, I like to have them repeat the whole thing back to me: *"I know I'm a sinner. I know sin separates me from God. I know that there is nothing I can do to fix this on my own, and so I thank you, Jesus, for dying on the cross to pay the price for my sins so that I could spend eternity in heaven with you. Thank you for being my Lord."* Something like that.

### Romans Road Presentation
*(Unattributed — source unknown. Scripture verified except where noted below.)*

**Romans 3:23 NKJV** — "for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."

**Romans 3:10 NKJV** — "As it is written: 'There is none righteous, no, not one.'"

**Romans 5:12 NKJV** — "Therefore, just as through one man sin entered the world, and death through sin, and thus death spread to all men, because all sinned—"

The creator of the universe is holy. God is light, and in him is no darkness at all (1 John 1:5 KJV). He has set laws in place for his creation to obey. What are these laws? They begin with the Ten Commandments. While you might consider yourself "good" compared to most people, how do you measure up against God's law? Have you ever told the smallest lie? Then you are a liar. "For whoever keeps the whole law and yet stumbles at just one point is guilty of breaking all of it" (James 2:10 NIV). Disobedience to God is sin.

*Is sin really THAT big of a deal? If it is… what HOPE is there?*

**Romans 6:23 NKJV** — "For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."

Disobedience to an infinite eternal God deserves an infinite eternal consequence. God shows us his character and his laws in his creation, the world around us. He has also written his laws in our hearts — on our conscience. He has also given us his Word, the Bible. No good judge would let the guilty go free, the criminal go unpunished. Neither can a holy righteous God allow sinful man to go unpunished. But God loves man, whom he has made in his image, and he has provided a way of escape by sending his only Son to die in our place.

*After what I've done to grieve God… HOW could He willingly die in my place?*

**Romans 5:8 NKJV** — "But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."

"For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life" (John 3:16 NKJV). While he died on the cross, he was mocked, spit upon, and cursed. Yet Jesus cried out, "Father forgive them for they know not what they do" (Luke 23:34 KJV). With such love, Jesus died in your place — knowing every evil act, word, and thought you would commit. What powerful love! He loves you unconditionally, even to the point of death, when you are at your worst.

*HOW can I be saved?*

**Romans 10:9–10 NKJV** — "that if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved. For with the heart one believes unto righteousness, and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation."

You can never be saved by trying to be a good person, nor can you be saved through any amount of good works. Ephesians 2:8–9 says: "For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast" (NKJV). *(The presentation labels this KJV — that label is inaccurate; see source notes.)* You and I can be saved only by confessing our sins and placing our faith in God's Son, Jesus Christ, who died and paid for our sins on the cross. We must also surrender our lives to his lordship, placing him in charge of every area of life because we now belong to him. Both John the Baptist and Jesus himself began their preaching with the word "Repent." "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand" (Matthew 4:17 NKJV). To repent means to change one's mind — to turn, to go in another direction. How can we be led to repentance? The first step is true sorrow for what we've done wrong. "For godly sorrow produces repentance leading to salvation, not to be regretted; but the sorrow of the world produces death" (2 Corinthians 7:10 NKJV). Worldly sorrow is more like the regret of a criminal who's just been caught, whereas godly sorrow is the deep remorse that produces a change in direction. Have you ever felt convicted after doing something wrong? The Bible says that the Holy Spirit is the one who convicts us of sin (John 16:7–8).

*Did God HEAR me? Did He accept me?*

**Romans 10:13 NKJV** — "For 'whoever calls on the name of the LORD shall be saved.'"

God promises that "to all who received him, to those who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God" (John 1:12 NIV). Yes, God does hear and accept all who come to put their faith in him. There is no need to fear death any longer, because Jesus broke the power of sin and death on the cross with his own shed blood. The price for your sin has been paid in full, and it is God's promise to receive all who come to him by placing their faith in Jesus Christ as their Lord and Savior. As a believer, you have a new life in Christ. Jesus did not remain in the grave — he rose from the dead after three days (Mark 16). "Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new" (2 Corinthians 5:17 KJV).

*What do I do now?*

**Romans 10:17 NKJV** — "So then faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God."

Your journey as a child of the King of Kings has just begun. You must begin by spending time daily in prayer and in God's Word, the Bible. You must also find fellowship with other believers in a church that teaches and preaches the Bible. You should also now confess Jesus your Savior before men — share about your new faith in Christ with your family, coworkers, and others whom God has placed on your daily path.
