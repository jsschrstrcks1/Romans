# Genesis 4:1-16 — Cain and Abel

## Opening — Fat and Flavor

I was thinking about lean vs fat meat this week. Perhaps it was a by product of thinking about sacrificing sheep, but it seems to me that lean meat doesn't have as much flavor. The flavor of a good meat is in the fat. A nice juicy prime rib has a little bit of fat marbled throughout. Where as a lean meat — like rabbit — not only lacks fat, but doesn't have as much flavor and is dry and tough. Which is why when we cook rabbit we sear it in olive oil, wrap it in bacon, and then in foil before we cook it on the grill. So that all that fat, that flavor, that moisture permeates the meat. Taking something tough, bland and dry and giving it a depth of flavor a level of moisture and a tenderness that makes it a much more tasty experience. In Adams time and now, the fatty meats are prized above others. Hams — loaded with fat. This is relevant, and you will see why in a minute.

## The Mark of Cain — Correcting a Dangerous Misreading

The second thing I want to talk to you about before we read our passage tonight, is that there is a common misconception about tonight's passage. A century and a half ago in an attempt to justify acts of evil otherwise good men took this passage and tried to claim it as a justification for slavery. They argued that the mark of Cain was a certain skin color. Whatever the mark of Cain, and we don't know what it was, there is no indication that anyone other than Cain had or would ever have it. Regardless of what the mark of Cain actually IS, this theory was the result of men trying to justify their sinful deplorable evil practice of owning other men as slaves. It is a classic example of what happens when people start with an assumption and try to find a place in scripture to prove it. However what we need to do when studying scripture is to actually hear what the word of God has to say and then allow it to shape and mold our opinions. One brings honor to God, the other is used to justify slavery and other evil acts. One results in people coming to know the Lord, the other results in people like Jim Jones leading people to a commune in another country and causing them to commit mass suicide. Always ALWAYS start with scripture, and let scripture shape you and your opinions.

In the Hebrew it says in the fullness of time. Someone had to have told them something. Someone had to have given them a date at the very LEAST, and if God were to do that, then why would he not also have defined the other things he expected? Secondly H.A. Ironside argued that Romans 10:17 — So then faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God. Obviously Abel had faith. If Abel had faith, he must have heard the word of God. There had to have been some behind the scenes instructions given that we don't know and haven't encountered.

## Why Wasn't Cain's Offering Accepted?

### 1. Improper Type of Offering

I think its likely that the type of offering was wrong. If we move forward in scripture a bit we find that the appropriate sacrifice for the covering of sin is one of blood. Blood had to be shed to cover Adam and Eve's nakedness when God knit them clothes of fur. Blood would have to continue to be shed to keep their sin covered, and shielded from the eyes of God. Yet what did Cain bring to the altar? He brought fruit.

### 2. The Quality of the Offering

Abel brought the fattest of his first born sheep. In other words when he gave, he gave the best he had at the appointed time. The fattest, the best tasting, the juiciest, most succulent tender young lamb he had, the most perfect lamb in his flock he gave in sacrifice. I think this moved God's heart a little as it was symbolic of what he knew his Son would have to do in time. It certainly reflected what would have to happen. But the point is scripture goes out of its way to say that Abel gave the absolute best he had at the appointed time. Cain by contrast, scripture says in the NET and some of the other translations that Cain gave some of his fruit. We aren't told it was his best. It was either just average, the same as had always been done, or else it may have been the bruised, or dinged and damaged fruit.

My grandmother always loved the damaged tomato bins at the fruit stands. She would buy up every single damaged tomato she could get her hands on, wash them, and can them. There was never a fruit so far gone she couldn't find a use for it, and I loved that about her. She did the same at Winn Dixie — she had a route she would walk, a circuit you could say around the store going from discount bin to discount bin and she'd buy up anything that was a decent value.

But what God was asking for was not their leftovers. God wanted to and deserves to be first! How often do we give God our leftovers? I have been making a concerted effort to keep my Saturdays open in the mornings and afternoons in part because I like to go back over the sermon and think about it again, but also because if I fill Saturday up with junk that can be done on other days, whats left for God when I get to church? I plan my week around Saturday being a day of rest to the extent that is possible. I mean obviously if my ox falls in a well, or if my truck breaks I'm going to do what I need to do to get it fixed. But I pattern my life in such a way that when I get to church, God gets the best of what I have to offer at the appointed time.

Abel gave God his best at the appointed time, and Cain gave God just some of his fruit. The quality of the offering was improper.

### 3. A Heart Problem

We know that sacrifices offered with an unrepentant heart are viewed poorly by God. Even today, when we take the Lord's Supper. Scripture says if you have anything you need to deal with with your brother do so before you take the Lords supper. In fact it says Stop now, and Go! Likewise we've discussed that its not good for a non-believer to take the Lords Supper. In a similar way if Cain had something against another, or another had something against him then God would have had a dim view of Cain's offering. Frankly Cain's sin could have been disobedience to God. Could have offered something other than what God had commanded. His offering done in disobedience is I think worse than not giving an offering at all. By making this offering in the way he did he may as well have been daring God "if you exist, do your worst." Its like the kid that doesn't get his one chore done, vs the kid that makes a bigger mess and gets mouthy about it.

## God's Warning — Genesis 4:6-7

Even after all that, look with me at Genesis 4:6-7 — "And Cain was very angry, and his countenance fell. So the Lord said to Cain, 'Why are you angry? And why has your countenance fallen? If you do well, will you not be accepted? And if you do not do well, sin lies at the door. And its desire is for you, but you should rule over it.'"

Cain was even defiant after a gentle correction from God. Cain grew angry. Maybe even grew to hate his brother, and God warned him that sin lies at the door, and that Cain should rule over the sin, and not be ruled over by sin. So what does Cain do? He immediately goes and commits murder.

But don't we do the same thing? I knew a young couple. They dated for years, both grew up in the church. They eventually married, and would use the other as an excuse not to go to church. They didn't make a habit of reading their bibles, or praying together, or worshipping. God was NOT a part of their marriage. A few years later, he was getting physically violent, and then had an affair. Today they are divorced with a child. Years later BOTH are mad at God for the failure of their marriage. They pushed God away at every turn, and then when God allowed their own efforts to fail, they got angry. They allowed sin to rule over them. They simply went through the motions of living for God. They were even pretty convincing for a while, but in the end I'd argue that the truth wasn't in them.

## Comparison — The Sin of Pride

Cain compared himself to Abel. That is always a huge mistake. Because if you win, you despise the other, they are in some measure looked down upon by virtue of having won. However if you lose, then you're forever looking up in anger. Either scenario leads you to sin, and thus you have lost.

Pride is one of those wicked and pernicious sins that no matter how well you think you're doing you're fooling yourself. If you think you have beaten pride, you're being proud and thus it has won. It is sneaky, and pervasive in our culture, and it is winning.

C.S. Lewis said, "Pride gets no pleasure out of having something, only out of having more of it than the next man. We say people are proud of being rich, or clever, or good-looking, but they are not. They are proud of being richer, or cleverer, or better-looking than others."
