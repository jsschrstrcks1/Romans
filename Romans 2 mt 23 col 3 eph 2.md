**Supporting Texts:** Matthew 23:27–33; Ephesians 2:4–10; Colossians 3:1–6
**Key Themes:** Hypocrisy; religious self-deception; outward conformity without inward transformation; God's righteous judgment; resurrection not renovation; political idolatry

## Opening — The Cup

I was at someone's house one time, and I was looking for a cup. I knew where the cups normally were — I was a regular guest — so I checked there first. But the cabinet was empty. Thinking they probably just hadn't run the dishwasher yet, I popped it open, and success. I found a cup. Looked great on the outside. Clean, white. The other dishes in the dishwasher appeared clean also, so I took my cup over to their fridge and dropped some ice cubes in there and started filling it with water. Only to be smacked in the face by a smell. You know the one. Rotting food. It was at this point that I looked inside the cup and saw a bone stuck to the inside — kind of moldy looking, may have been barbecue at some point, but now it was all rancid, nasty, stinking, dead, moldy bone. I was really glad I noticed before taking a sip.

That cup looked good on the outside. But on the inside it was absolutely vile. Sinful, you might say.

---

## Clean Outside, Dead Inside

I read a story about a man that believed he was safe because his people had been given a special name by God — Jew. He was proud of having the right monotheism — he wasn't a Jehovah's Witness, Mormon, or Muslim. He could discern between good and evil. He had even been educated in Old Testament law.

Let me ask you, and this is not a trick question. Is he safe from hell based on those few things? Such a person would be like my cup — clean on the outside and sinful on the inside.

Today people have multiple Bibles, are called Christians, know the difference between moral evil and moral good. They are monotheistic — they don't worship multiple gods, and they don't worship the pope, or a saint, or Islam, or Krishna. They have received a Sunday school education through the Old and New Testament.

One day, will that be enough for them to get to heaven? They too are like my cup. They may look nice for church, they may play nice in front of their pastor, but at the end of the day, where it matters, they are like the inside of my cup. Moldy, nasty, stinky, dead.

---

## Whitewashed Tombs

Jewish leaders saw themselves as guides of the blind, but Jesus says they were blind leading the blind. They had spent their lives studying the Old Testament — God's Word — and were proud of that. They traveled around looking for religious converts, and yet after Christ, were they saving these people? Or enslaving them to the law? They were the blind leading the blind.

This was meant to be on the edge of offensive to the Jews of the day. The Jewish leaders especially considered themselves to be enlightened, considered themselves to be the standard of holiness and godliness and to have reached the pinnacle of knowledge. And yet in Matthew 23 Jesus calls those same people whitewashed tombs. Made to look alive on the outside, but dead inside. Like my cup. Looked fine in the dishwasher — it had clearly been run. But a dishwasher can only do so much, and that wedged-in bone, covered in vile filth, was more than any human endeavor could have made clean.

I talked to my friend later, and he said they had to throw out the cup. They got the bone out, they got the mold out. But the smell persisted and was so awful it made anyone who tried to use it feel like they were going to vomit. The cup was irredeemable and disgusting. And is now destroyed.

## Scripture Reading — Romans 2:1–11

Let's read our passage this morning.

> Therefore you have no excuse, O man, every one of you who judges. For in passing judgment on another you condemn yourself, because you, the judge, practice the very same things. We know that the judgment of God rightly falls on those who practice such things. Do you suppose, O man — you who judge those who practice such things and yet do them yourself — that you will escape the judgment of God? Or do you presume on the riches of his kindness and forbearance and patience, not knowing that God's kindness is meant to lead you to repentance? But because of your hard and impenitent heart you are storing up wrath for yourself on the day of wrath when God's righteous judgment will be revealed.
>
> He will render to each one according to his works: to those who by patience in well-doing seek for glory and honor and immortality, he will give eternal life; but for those who are self-seeking and do not obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, there will be wrath and fury. There will be tribulation and distress for every human being who does evil, the Jew first and also the Greek, but glory and honor and peace for everyone who does good, the Jew first and also the Greek. For God shows no partiality.

## We Need to Be Our Own Harshest Critics

I can't help but think of Matthew 23 when I read this passage. We need to be our own harshest critics. The rabbis and Jewish leadership were taking a new convert and forcing outward compliance to a greater degree than the rabbis and Jewish leaders were willing to follow themselves.

It would be like today — 1 Corinthians 11 talks about how a man dishonors his head if he prays or prophesies with his head covered, and then lays out the reasoning for it. Likewise, 1 Corinthians says a wife who prays or prophesies with her head uncovered dishonors her head (1 Corinthians 11:4–5). So if I were to preach on that, and then next Sunday come to church wearing a hat, I'd be doing basically what they were doing — only they were doing these things in secret.

They said you can swear by the temple and not follow through, but if you swear by the gold in the temple, then you really mean it. Or you can swear by the altar and not follow through, but if you swear by the meat on the altar, then you'd better follow through. These rabbis were teaching these sorts of things. They were making people jump through a million often contradictory hoops, and in the end they were being religious without the relationship with Jesus Christ.

Jesus responded and said what purpose is the gold without the temple, and the meat without the altar — it is the temple and the altar that are the important parts (Matthew 23:16–22). He goes on to call them whitewashed tombs. They were nice looking on the outside, and in public, but in the privacy of their own homes they were dead inside. Rotten stinking corpses. Part of the crowd faking it. Trying to look good on Sunday, but smell like hellfire the rest of the week.

Sort of like spraying perfume on a corpse.

## The Perfumed Corpse

I knew someone that had a cat growing up, and it had escaped — they thought. They looked everywhere for that cat. A few days turned into a week, turned into a month, and at that point the mom found the cat, because of the smell. But her husband didn't believe it was their cat. He had to see it for himself. She didn't want to leave it outside, because then the stink would get worse, and it was already so bad that it was hard to stay in the same room with it. So she made a coffin — a cardboard boot box, a hand towel or two, and then the cat. But oh, the smell was so bad. So she went and got Febreze out of the pantry and she sprayed the cat with that.

People, Febreze can cover a lot of smells, but not the nauseating stench of something that's been dead for a while. But she tried. Then she sprayed Lysol. No help. So she went into her bedroom and brought out some of the Dolce & Gabbana perfume she had been given for Christmas. Even that didn't make it better. So she put the lid on the box, and tried to seal it inside a plastic garbage bag. Even then, she could still smell the stink of death — through the bag, over the perfume and Lysol and Febreze. It didn't matter what she did. She didn't have anything that smelled strongly enough to cover the stink of death.

So it is for those who live their week for themselves and try to live Sundays for the Lord.

## God's Righteous Judgment

Paul writes in verse 3:

> Do you suppose, O man — you who judge those who practice such things and yet do them yourself — that you will escape the judgment of God? Or do you presume on the riches of his kindness and forbearance and patience, not knowing that God's kindness is meant to lead you to repentance? But because of your hard and impenitent heart you are storing up wrath for yourself on the day of wrath when God's righteous judgment will be revealed. He will render to each one according to his works: to those who by patience in well-doing seek for glory and honor and immortality, he will give eternal life; but for those who are self-seeking and do not obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness, there will be wrath and fury. There will be tribulation and distress for every human being who does evil, the Jew first and also the Greek.
> — Romans 2:3–9, ESV

You know what Jesus said about such people? About these whitewashed tombs, about these people like my cup — clean on the outside but full of filth and rot and decay on the inside? People like that cat laid out all pretty in a boot box, with little towels, sprayed with expensive perfume but reeking of death?

Jesus says in Matthew 23:33 — "You serpents, you brood of vipers, how are you to escape being sentenced to hell?"

Such people reek of hellfire. You know the ones I mean — those that come to church on Sunday, and on Monday they go back to cursing like sailors. They dress nice on Sunday, but on Monday they go back to having sex outside of marriage. They may smell of nice perfume on Sunday, but on Monday they live for themselves. They seek out whatever earthly pleasure the lusts of their heart desire. On Sunday, they wear nice shoes; and on Monday they worship their true slavemaster — the devil. But boy, their hair looks nice on Sunday.

There is a group of people out there that believe if they can just fool the pastor on Sundays then maybe they will be able to fool God. People, if I can see it, God can see it. But if I can't see it, God definitely still can.

## The Difference Is Christ

In truth, the difference between these people we've been talking about — the whitewashed tombs, the perfumed dead cats, the cups clean on the outside and full of rot inside — is Jesus Christ.

Someone who yields their life to Christ, who becomes a believer in Jesus Christ, has that filth scrubbed out of their cup. Where it was nasty and gross and full of mold and stink, Jesus comes in like Mr. Clean and scrubs all the dirt and stink away. Even the dirt on the outside that you and I can't see — He takes care of that too.

That cat, dead and stinking of too much perfume and death. He doesn't just take the stink away. He resurrects the cat.

Ephesians 2 says:

> But God, being rich in mercy, because of the great love with which he loved us, even when we were dead in our trespasses, made us alive together with Christ — by grace you have been saved — and raised us up with him and seated us with him in the heavenly places in Christ Jesus, so that in the coming ages he might show the immeasurable riches of his grace in kindness toward us in Christ Jesus. For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast. For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand, that we should walk in them.
> — Ephesians 2:4–10, ESV

In short, that person inside the whitewashed tomb — Jesus doesn't help them do better at hiding their mess. He resurrects the corpse. He doesn't spray it with perfume. He gives the dead guy a new body, a new nature.

## Set Your Minds on Things Above

What then should we do, those of us who have been made new? We've been resurrected as believers. We're no longer dead, no longer a corpse inside that tomb. Should we then crawl back inside the tomb to sleep? Certainly not. Should we go back to sinning? Certainly not. We need to set our minds on things above.

Colossians 3 says:

> If then you have been raised with Christ, seek the things that are above, where Christ is, seated at the right hand of God. Set your minds on things that are above, not on things that are on earth. For you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God.
> — Colossians 3:1–3, ESV

Because our old nature is gone. We have a new nature, and that new nature is hidden in Christ.

> Put to death therefore what is earthly in you: sexual immorality, impurity, passion, evil desire, and covetousness, which is idolatry. On account of these the wrath of God is coming.
> — Colossians 3:5–6, ESV

## God's Justice

I was talking with another pastor this week. Up in Georgia, they were able to save 39 kids who had been kidnapped to be sold into sexual slavery. 39 American kids. They caught the guys. I felt conflicted. Not because they caught bad guys — for that I was happy. But because on the one hand I know vengeance is the Lord's, and on the other hand I was hoping the people who would do that to kids would get the death penalty.

It occurred to me through the course of this conversation that all earthly justice can do is take the man's life. They have already lost their freedom. They are already in bondage. Confining them to a cell, while appropriate, doesn't change their captivity. And secondly, God's justice will come for these people, and all of the ones that are never caught. God's justice is both more severe and more perfectly fitting the crime than anything we could ever dream up.

Growing up I heard someone say God has a special place in hell for those that hurt children. I think that quote belongs firmly in the book of first speculations. However, we do know Scripture says it would be better for a man to have a great millstone fastened around his neck and to be drowned in the depth of the sea than to cause one of these little ones to sin (Matthew 18:6). It's something He takes special interest in, and His vengeance will be perfect against the monsters that took and abused those 39 children.

Jonathan Edwards — who is regarded as America's most important theologian and philosopher — titled his most famous sermon "Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God," drawing from Hebrews 10:31: "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God" (ESV). The sexually immoral, impure, who desire evil, and covet, and are idolatrous — they are heading for just that. The hands of the living God.

## The Idolatry We Don't See

Idolatry is something we have to be very careful of in this country. We don't generally grab a stick, carve a face in it, and worship the stick. We know better than that. Where we get in trouble is in other ways that seem more sneaky, but that are no less gross than worshipping a stick.

We slowly give our hearts over to things that don't matter. We elevate our own comfort, or security, or pleasures above God's desires for us. We buy into the notion that we don't have to share the gospel if only we can elect a godly man in the White House. I agree that we should try and elect godly people into the nation's positions of power. But I disagree that this is a cure-all. The problem is sin, not who's running the country.

I read something that an author acquaintance of mine named Barnabas Piper wrote. He said, "Christian, if you feel politically homeless you're probably right where God wants you. This world is not our home, we serve a greater ruler, and we are to represent his kingdom here. And it looks nothing, not a bit, like our present political power structures and parties."

He continues, "Christian, if you feel at ease in either major American political party, you're in a bad place. You likely have placed the values of your own party, of power, of man-made structures over the words, values, economy and reality of Christ's kingdom that you are called to represent."

I won't say that's always true. But I think it's mostly true. Especially in our denomination, folks have a tendency to worship politics at the expense of the gospel. At the expense of Christ.

Think back over your conversations this week — or maybe better, look at your Facebook profile. If someone were to find you on Facebook, would it be easier for them to know your politics? Or know your Savior? If they can't find your Savior on your page, then perhaps you have an issue in your heart. Perhaps, Christian, maybe — just maybe — you're flirting with political idolatry.
