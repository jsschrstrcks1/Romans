## Thus-Says-the-Lord — Sermon Evaluation

### Verdict: "A Devastating Refrain Undone by Bad Math and a Missing Gospel"

**One-Sentence Summary**: A raw Psalm 106 sermon with a genuinely powerful rhetorical device — interleaving abortion statistics with Israel's downward spiral — fatally undermined by multiple mathematical errors, a missing gospel presentation, and an abrupt ending that abandons the congregation at the moment they most need the cross.

### Berean Gate: CONDITIONAL PASS

**Scopus Test**: The thesis — modern abortion is the same child-sacrifice sin of Psalm 106, and the same God who showed mercy to Israel offers mercy to us — demonstrably arises from Psalm 106:37-39 in context. **Pass.**

**Authority Test**: The preacher submits to the text's horrifying claims. He does not soften the passage. However, Psalm 106 is treated as a framework for the abortion statistics rather than exposited on its own terms. **Pass, with reservation.**

### Fatal Flaw Flags

**FLAG 1: NO CLEAR GOSPEL PRESENTATION — SCORE CAPPED AT 69**

Line 89-91: "But God being rich in mercy, and because of his great love for us sent his son to die for us – the harlot. The gospel." This is a placeholder, not a proclamation. No sin explained (beyond abortion), no wrath, no cross, no resurrection, no repentance, no faith. After 87 lines of crushing conviction, the gospel MUST be proclaimed. It is not.

**FLAG 2: NO ACTUAL EXHORTATION**

The sermon never tells the congregation what to believe, repent of, do, or pursue. "Shame on us as a people" is conviction, not exhortation.

### Core Override Questions

1. **Does this sermon make me think more of Christ — or myself?** Neither. It makes me think about dead babies. Christ is nearly absent.
2. **Did I encounter God, or just ideas about Him?** God's mercy is briefly encountered in the quoted verses (79-83). But secondhand, through quotation, not through preaching.
3. **Was I comforted, convicted, or merely informed?** Deeply convicted. But conviction without gospel resolution is pastoral malpractice.

### Strengths (Top 3)

1. **The interleaving refrain** (lines 17-85): "We as a people have..." between each episode of Israel's rebellion forces the congregation to hold ancient sin and modern sin simultaneously. Arises from the text's own catalogue structure.
2. **The Piper four-point framework** (lines 56-69): Gives the climax theological precision. The preacher's own addition — "at least they were sacrificing to something higher; we sacrifice to ourselves" — is sharper than Piper's original.
3. **The mercy thread** (lines 77-83): Quoting vv. 8, 10, 23, 30, 43-46 in sequence mirrors the psalm's rebellion-mercy alternation. Textually faithful and pastorally necessary.

### Critical Weaknesses (Top 3)

1. **Gospel absent** (lines 89-91): Placeholder, not proclamation. After 87 lines of crushing conviction, the cross must be proclaimed. It is not.
2. **Five of eleven math comparisons are wrong** (lines 9, 17, 20, 36, 44, 58): Lake Okeechobee off by ~300×, Sphinx off by ~100×, National Mall off by ~60×, WTC off by ~4×, internal volume inconsistency. Destroys credibility.
3. **Evolution tangent** (lines 24-26): Eisegesis. "From everlasting to everlasting" in Psalm 106 is about covenant faithfulness, not cosmological origins. Breaks the sermon's momentum.

### Blind Spots

- **Post-abortive mother**: Someone in that room has had an abortion. The corporate "we" gives them no specific word of grace.
- **Roe v. Wade date**: 1973, not 1970. "Ten years" should be "seven years."
- **Gold dust medical claims**: Speculative; metallic gold is largely bioinert.
- **Translation inconsistency**: Bookend verses (106:1, 48) are NIV; body quotations are ESV.

### Illusion of Strength

The volume of statistics creates the illusion of meticulous research. The wrong comparisons, once discovered, make a listener doubt all the numbers.

### Missed Opportunity

**Psalm 106:23**: "Moses, his chosen one, stood in the breach before him, to turn away his wrath." This is the natural Christological bridge. Moses stands between God's wrath and God's people — exactly as Christ does on the cross. The sermon quotes v. 23 but doesn't develop it. A gospel close built from v. 23 — "Who stands in the breach for us? Christ. The one who absorbed the wrath we deserved for the blood we have poured out" — would be organic, text-driven, and devastating.

**Psalm 106:45**: "He remembered his covenant, and relented according to the abundance of his steadfast love." The basis of mercy is covenant, not sentiment. This is where Reformed theology shines — and where the sermon could bridge to the New Covenant in Christ's blood.

### Surgical Fixes

1. **Build the gospel close from Psalm 106:23 and 106:45** — Moses in the breach → Christ on the cross; covenant faithfulness → New Covenant. Give the congregation: the specific sin (innocent blood poured out), the specific wrath (God ready to destroy), the specific mercy (Christ in the breach), the specific call (repent and believe). Address the post-abortive person directly.
2. **Fix or remove broken math comparisons** — Keep: White House, Statue of Liberty, Ice Palace, Raymond James, stadium. Remove or recalculate: Lake Okeechobee, WTC, Sphinx, National Mall. Fix internal inconsistency (200M → 300M). Fix Roe v. Wade date.
3. **Remove evolution tangent** — Replace with exposition of v. 48's actual point: God's eternal covenant faithfulness as the frame for Israel's transient rebellion.
4. **Add exhortation** — Pray, support crisis pregnancy centers, consider adoption/foster care, speak truth, and if you've been party to abortion: come forward, receive grace.
5. **Fix Piper quote** — Restore original wording ("millions," not "billions"; "send to heaven / make murderers on earth"). Fix "make kill" typo.
6. **Remove duplicate Hoover Dam line** (lines 41-42).

### Dead Orthodoxy Flag: NOT TRIGGERED

This sermon is not bloodless. The fire is present; the gospel resolution is not.

### Weight of Glory Assessment

The sermon carries weight — the subject demands it, and the refrain delivers it. But crushing the conscience without proclaiming the gospel has wielded the hammer without offering the balm.

### Rubric Fit Note

Psalm 106 is a historical recitation psalm. The rubric's verse-by-verse exposition expectations bend here — narrative arc serves this genre better than sequential exegesis. However, gospel centrality requirements are fully applicable: the psalm's own mercy thread demands gospel resolution.
