# Thus Says the Lord — Evaluation
## `Christian and works - eph 2.md`
### Ephesians 2:8–10 / John 15:1–5
*Evaluated March 2026*

---

## Verdict Title
**"A Doctrinally Sound Sermon That Doesn't Arrive at the Cross"**

## One-Sentence Summary
A faithful opening of Ephesians 2:10 — the neglected half of the most memorized passage in the church — that correctly identifies works as fruit of salvation and wisely deploys the Heidelberg, while stopping short of the cross, leaving the gospel close as a placeholder, and never fully pressing the congregation on what works-avoidance reveals about the state of the heart.

---

## Berean Gate

**Scopus Test:** *Thesis:* "Works are the fruit of salvation, not the cause of it — every believer is created in Christ Jesus for good works, which means we are called to walk in them." Demonstrably arises from Eph 2:9–10's own argument. **PASS.**

**Authority Test:** The preacher does not soften or evade the text's demands. Works/grace distinction handled honestly; moralism given no back door. **PASS.**

**Berean Gate: PASS**

---

## Fatal Flaw Flags

**⚠️ GOSPEL CLOSE IS A MANUSCRIPT PLACEHOLDER.** The sermon ends with the single word: *"Gospel."* As a raw transcript the pastor presumably preached the gospel verbally — but the manuscript as it stands cannot be preached or republished without completing this section. This is not a score cap trigger for the raw transcript, but it is a **hard stop on reuse** until the gospel close is written in full with penal substitution, the cross, and a genuine invitation.

No other fatal flaw flags triggered. Score is **not capped.**

## Interpretation Band
**61 — Concerning Gaps. The sermon explains but doesn't fully preach.**

---

## Core Override Questions

1. *Does this sermon make me think more of Christ — or myself?* Primarily myself — what I should do.
2. *Did I encounter God, or just ideas about Him?* Ideas about Him, with pastoral warmth.
3. *Was I comforted, convicted, or merely informed?* Encouraged and informed. Not convicted.

---

## Top 3 Strengths

**1. The Neglected Verse**
Opening with "Who here has memorized Eph 2:8-9? Almost everyone. Who stopped there?" is an excellent hook that immediately creates productive dissonance. The congregation recognizes their own gap before the sermon begins. This is the right starting point.

**2. The Grape/Reaper Illustration**
Native to John 15, visually specific, and carries real theological weight: fruit identifies the vine. The Carolina Reaper is the right choice — nobody accidentally eats one. *"You wouldn't accidentally eat a reaper, thinking you were about to bite a juicy yummy grape."* This image will stay with the congregation.

**3. Heidelberg Q&A 91**
Reading the Catechism aloud and applying it as a three-part diagnostic (true faith / conforming to God's law / for God's glory) is the sermon's doctrinal anchor. It distinguishes genuine works from JW performance-religion with precision rather than sentiment. The Isaiah 29:13 application immediately after lands the negative example.

---

## Top 3 Critical Weaknesses

**1. ποίημα and προητοίμασεν are both left on the table.**
The text says we are God's *ποίημα* — masterpiece, poem — the word appears only twice in the NT. It transforms v.10 from a duty text to a grace text. *"Prepared beforehand"* (προητοίμασεν) means the good works were ordained in eternity, connecting directly to Eph 1's election language and grounding works as God's work through us, not self-generated performance. The sermon quotes v.10 but doesn't preach it. The richest words in the passage are untouched.

**2. The gospel close is one word.**
*"Gospel."* The sermon builds toward a moment of invitation and deposits a placeholder. Whatever was said verbally in 2019 is now inaccessible. Before this sermon is reused, the gospel must be written in full — with penal substitution named, the cross bleeding on the page, and an actual invitation issued. The manuscript as it stands cannot be preached.

**3. The imperative isn't grounded in the indicative long enough.**
The sermon moves quickly from "Christ died for us, we were made alive" to "so here's what we do." The Puritans' *uses* pattern — doctrine → conviction → direction → consolation — is compressed here into doctrine → direction. The conviction step that makes the direction feel necessary rather than dutiful is missing. The result: the sermon feels more like a ministry training session than a proclamation of grace producing obedience.

---

## Blind Spots

The preacher is clearly comfortable with Reformed soteriology — perhaps too comfortable. Because works/grace is theologically settled for him, he moves past it quickly. But for half the congregation, the relationship between grace and obedience is not settled. The sermon assumes more doctrinal stability than many listeners likely have.

The sermon also doesn't press its own implicit question: *What does it reveal about you if you produce no fruit?* This is the homiletical nerve of John 15 — and it goes untouched. John 15:6 is right there.

---

## Illusion of Strength

The EGR section (Extra Grace Required) is warm, observed, and gets the most air time in the sermon's second half. It *feels* like pastoral depth. But it is the loosest connection to Eph 2:10, and it crowds out the cross. "Love difficult people" is a valid application of Galatians 5 but has drifted from the primary text. The EGR section is likeable — and that likeability has consumed time that belongs to the cross and to John 15:6.

---

## Missed Opportunity

**John 15:6 exists.** *"If anyone does not abide in me he is thrown away like a branch and withers; and the branches are gathered, thrown into the fire, and burned."* The grape/pepper illustration sets this up perfectly — and then the sermon refuses to press it. A man who produces jalapenos instead of grapes is not only doctrinally interesting — he is in danger. The text provides the warning. The illustration earns it. But the warning is never spoken.

---

## Surgical Fixes

**Fix 1 — Unpack ποίημα at v.10.**
One sentence: *"We are his workmanship — ποίημα, the Greek word — God's poem. His masterpiece. You were not an accident and you are not a disappointment. You are God's artwork, created in Christ Jesus."* This transforms v.10 from duty to grace before the imperative arrives.

**Fix 2 — Preach προητοίμασεν.**
*"Which God prepared beforehand that we should walk in them. Before you were born, God had works prepared for you. You are not generating these on your own. You are walking into a path He built."* Sovereign grace applied to works — already in the text.

**Fix 3 — Add John 15:6 after the grape/pepper illustration.**
The illustration earns the warning. *"And Jesus is not making a botanical observation. He says 'if anyone does not abide in me he is thrown away like a branch and withers... and burned.' The stakes of what kind of fruit you produce are not small."*

**Fix 4 — Write the gospel close in full.**
Full penal substitution. Specific invitation. The cross must bleed on this page before this sermon is reused. The placeholder must go.

**Fix 5 — Tighten the Promise Keepers section.**
The statistics are unverified (flagged separately by careful-not-clever). Cut them or source them. Keep the quoted description — *"30,000 guys ready to charge hell with a small bucket of water but no one to show them the way"* — that line is vivid and real. Drop the unsourced percentages.

**Fix 6 — Trim the EGR section.**
The EGR material is genuinely good but it currently dominates the sermon's second half when the cross and John 15:6 should. Trim to one tight paragraph. Use the recovered time for the cross.

## Dead Orthodoxy Flag
Not triggered. The sermon is warm and pastoral throughout. The fire is low but present — the EGR neighbor section and the pandemic urgency close show genuine pastoral heat.

## Weight of Glory
The pandemic urgency close — *"There are people alive today that will be gone tomorrow because of this virus... A lot of the works vary based on your spiritual gifts, but sharing the gospel is not one of those"* — carries genuine eternal gravity. That urgency is right. It just arrives too late and without the cross under it.
