# Voice Audit — New Sermons
## `christmas 2019.md` and `Christian and works - eph 2.md`
*Corpus baseline: Romans 15a, Romans 14a, Sermon - Today You Will Be With Me v2*
*Audited March 2026*

---

## Preliminary Note: Raw Transcript vs. Polished Manuscript

Both files are raw transcripts — spoken ministry captured in text, not edited prose. The machine tell scan is calibrated accordingly. Many features that would flag in a polished manuscript are natural features of oral speech. The audit's purpose here shifts: not *was this AI-generated?* (clearly no) but *what voice is present, what needs to be preserved in any editing pass, and where does the spoken voice diverge from the author's polished form?*

---

## SERMON 1: `christmas 2019.md`

### Machine Tell Scan

**Result: NONE flagged.**

- No uniform sentence length — naturally variable throughout
- No transition tells — pivots are conversational: *"Any way,"* *"One more passage,"* *"Its interesting"*
- No AI vocabulary — theological terms sharp: *debt, wrath, humiliation, sin, humiliated, wrath, died, raised*
- No vague moral swaps — *"brokenness"* never substitutes for sin; *"debt"* and *"wrath"* used plainly
- Hedge language is appropriate theological caution: *"I don't believe the trinity was shattered by the cross... but we have to be careful lest we stray into heresy"* — a real preacher being careful with a hard text, not an AI softening a claim
- No artificial seams — voice is consistent throughout

### Voice Continuity Check

**Present (matches polished corpus):**
- ✅ Compressed declarative chains: *"Defeated sin. Defeated hell, defeated Satan."*
- ✅ Antithetical parallelism: *"We owed a debt we could not pay, he paid a debt he did not owe"*
- ✅ Direct pastoral address: *"He knows Paula, what it is to lose a dearly beloved child"* — the most pastorally specific line in either sermon; irreplaceable; could not be AI-generated
- ✅ Pause-and-pivot: *"Its interesting, you guys know I have sheep"* — natural spoken pivot after theological weight
- ✅ Sharp doctrinal vocabulary throughout

**Absent — transcript artifact only (will emerge in editing):**
- ⬜ Explicit gear-shift markers (*"Now —"* / *"Hear me:"*)
- ⬜ The "Night Is Far Gone" refrain — pre-series 2019 sermon; refrain not yet established
- ⬜ Labeled section structure

**Absent — substantive:**
- ⬜ *"Some of you who..."* direct address beyond Paula — systematic Perkins-category address not present; Paula is a single excellent instance

### Conviction Check

1. **Sin confronted:** Idolatry — the security blanket returned to. Named: *"self interests, our idols, our sources of security found outside of Christ... our own sufficiency, or our own intelligence, or wealth, or health."* ✅
2. **Clear call:** *"We like Linus, should respond to Christ by laying down our wants, and desires at the foot of another tree. At the foot of the cross."* ✅
3. **Direct address:** Paula ✅ — single instance
4. **Conditional voice:** Minimal and appropriate — theological hedging only
5. **Comfort before conviction:** NO — idol/blanket conviction precedes the comfort; humiliation and wrath come before resurrection ✅

### Cadence Check

**Hottest point:** Humiliation/penal substitution section.

- ✅ Compressed declarative chain at climax: *"Defeated sin. Defeated hell, defeated Satan."*
- ✅ Three-part anaphora present
- ⚠️ Surrounding paragraph is very dense — the compression arrives late inside a wall of text; in live preaching the speaker would pause naturally; on the page it needs editorial breaks
- ⬜ Pause-and-pivot after the peak is absent — rolls directly into *"Being saved, starts with the gospel"* and ends

### Authenticity Risk Assessment

**Authenticity Risk: LOW**

This is a real man's voice, unmistakably. The borrowed tomb line (*"A tomb would have been a poor investment any way. He only needed it for three days"*) could not be AI-generated. The Paula moment is local knowledge no machine has. The rough grammar and conversational texture are not weaknesses — they are the texture of someone speaking to people they love on a Sunday morning in December.

**The gap between this and the polished corpus is editorial, not authorial.** The gear-shifts, labeled sections, and *"Some of you"* movements emerge in the editing pass — and the voice that emerges is the same voice, sharpened.

## SERMON 2: `Christian and works - eph 2.md`

### Machine Tell Scan

**Result: NONE flagged.**

- Variable sentence length throughout
- No AI transition tells — *"So Maybe its because,"* *"But first lets read,"* *"What I'm trying to get at here"* — conversational
- No AI vocabulary — *dead, trespasses, sin, alive, workmanship, fruit, gospel* — sharp throughout
- No vague moral swaps

**One partial note:** The EGR section (Rick Warren, *Purpose Driven Life*) reads slightly more polished than the surrounding transcript — accurately so, because the preacher is quoting a published book. This is not an AI tell; it is a preacher working from a read source. The surrounding pastoral material (*"I happen to have some neighbors"*) is native voice.

**No seams detected** — consistent voice throughout.

### Voice Continuity Check

**Present:**
- ✅ Antithetical parallelism: *"Our faith, our salvation doesn't come from works. Works come from our salvation."* — the most cleanly antithetical line in either sermon
- ✅ Text-first orbit — returns to Eph 2 regularly; John 15 brought in and returned to
- ✅ Sharp doctrinal vocabulary: *dead, alive, corpse, trespasses, sin, grace, works, gospel*
- ✅ Compressed statement: *"Works don't save us. We can never be good enough to save ourselves, just like a dead man can't resurrect himself."*
- ✅ The *"He just said Go"* compression at the close — pure author; native cadence emerging even in raw form

**Absent — transcript artifact:**
- ⬜ Gear-shift markers in written form
- ⬜ Labeled section structure
- ⬜ "Night Is Far Gone" refrain

**Absent — substantive:**
- ⬜ Shrinking sentences at climax — the gospel-sharing exhortation doesn't compress further
- ⬜ *"Some of you who..."* categorical direct address — stays in *"we"* for most of its length; EGR section has *"I happen to have some neighbors"* but no systematic differentiated address
- ⬜ Pause-and-pivot after intensity — EGR section builds and then rolls into practical examples without pulling the room close

### Conviction Check

1. **Sin confronted:** Fruitlessness and directionless post-conversion living. Named through the PK illustration. But the specific sin of *not doing works* is described more than it is pressed. The question *"What does it reveal about you if you produce no fruit?"* is implied, never asked.
2. **Clear call:** *"He just said Go."* ✅
3. **Direct address:** *"I happen to have some neighbors"* — pastoral self-disclosure ✅; the germaphobe food pantry volunteer ✅; but no cornering of a specific hearer
4. **Conditional voice:** Minimal — *"I don't agree with everything Warren has written"* is appropriate qualification
5. **Comfort before conviction:** PARTIAL FLAG. The sermon is predominantly encouraging in register. The conviction that *no works = serious spiritual warning* is implied but never landed. The fruitless hearer is not cornered.

### Cadence Check

**Hottest point should be:** The closing gospel-sharing exhortation.

- ✅ *"He just said Go"* is a native cadence compression — three words, declarative, final
- ⬜ Anaphora not built around it — *"Not 'go if you're gifted.' Not 'go if you're ready.' He just said Go."* is what this voice would produce; it's one step away
- ⬜ Pause-and-pivot absent — exhortation moves directly into the gospel close placeholder
- Breath test: Most paragraphs pass; EGR section breathes naturally as spoken ministry

### Authenticity Risk Assessment

**Authenticity Risk: LOW**

The same conclusion as the Christmas sermon. This is genuine oral ministry. The grape/pepper illustration is locally observed (pastor knows someone with a grape orchard and hot peppers). The food pantry volunteer story is local knowledge. *"He just said Go"* is a native cadence move that will anchor the edited version of this sermon.

## Minimal Restoration Edits (for any future editing pass)

These are not rewrites. They are prompts — places where the author's own voice, already present in the raw text, should be amplified.

### Christmas 2019 — 3 edits

**Edit 1 — Add a second *"Some of you"* beat before the lost sheep section:**
Before Matthew 18:10-14, add: *"Some of you came in here this morning already knowing you're the lost sheep. You've been wandering a long time. You're not sure how you got here today. This part is for you."* The raw material is already there — this just names it.

**Edit 2 — Compress the humiliation paragraph into a chain:**
Somewhere inside line 43 lives: *"He deserved a palace. He got a cattle stall. He deserved the honors of heaven. He got the reproaches of Psalm 22. He deserved a tomb worth owning. He only needed it for three days."* The borrowed tomb line is already there — the chain just needs to be pulled out and set apart.

**Edit 3 — Add one gear-shift before Psalm 22:**
*"Now — open to Psalm 22:1."* That's it. One word. The spoken pivot is already there; it just needs its written form.

### Christian and works — 2 edits

**Edit 1 — Build anaphora around *"He just said Go"*:**
*"Not 'go if you're gifted.' Not 'go if you're ready.' Not 'go if you have the words.' He just said Go."* Three lines pulled from the existing argument, compressed around the line that's already there.

**Edit 2 — Add direct address to the fruitless before the gospel close:**
*"And some of you know — if you're honest — that there's been no fruit. No giving. No praying. No telling. Just receiving. That's not a lifestyle. That's a warning sign. And Christ is the only answer to it."* This is the John 15:6 application the sermon currently avoids.
