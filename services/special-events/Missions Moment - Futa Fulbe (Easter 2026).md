# Missions Moment: The Futa Fulbe

**Easter Sunday — April 5, 2026**

---

Our people group of the week is the Futa Fulbe — that's FOOL-bay FOO-tah.

The Fulbe are a West African people whose children grow up in age-sets — groups of all the children born within a three or four year span. Those children go to school together, work together, and eventually help each other pay bride prices when it comes time to marry. Their villages are often scattered across the landscape, but each one centers around a court and a mosque. Islam is the organizing reality of Fulbe life. It is not an add-on. It is their identity, their community structure, their answer to every question about meaning and duty and what happens after death.

And that last part matters this morning. Because the Fulbe answer to what happens after death is the same answer Islam gives everywhere: it depends on whether your good deeds outweigh your bad ones. Salvation by the scale. Mercy measured out on a balance.

Efforts to bring the gospel to the Fulbe have produced only a handful of believers. They are deeply devoted to their faith and see it as sufficient — as the fulfillment of everything they need.

But here is what we are about to celebrate at this table and in this sermon: Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep. That changes everything. The resurrection is not advice. It is not a system of weights and measures. It is an announcement — death has been defeated, and every man and woman who belongs to Christ will be raised. Not because the scale tipped in their favor, but because the last enemy has already lost.

The Fulbe need to hear that. A people who organize their whole lives around duty and community and obligation — they need to hear that there is a King who has already done what no amount of obligation can accomplish. He has swallowed up death in victory.

Let's pray for them.

Father, we thank you that the tomb is empty and that death does not get the last word. We pray now for the Futa Fulbe. Open their eyes to see that salvation built on works will always leave them uncertain, but that Christ's resurrection leaves nothing uncertain. Raise up workers — full-time missionaries who will go and live among the Fulbe and love them and tell them plainly what happened on the third day. Give boldness to the small number of Fulbe believers. Start something among them, Lord — not just scattered conversions but a movement, a church, a Christ-shaped community that the scattered villages can see and be drawn to. We ask you to do what only you can do: take a people who believe they already have the answer, and show them the Answer they never expected. In Christ's name, amen.

Who would like to pray for the Futa Fulbe?
