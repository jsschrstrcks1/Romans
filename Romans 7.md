# Romans 7:7-12
## Raw Sermon Draft

---

The law is not it self evil; that is the law on its own is not enough to separate us from God. The law is static, firm, part of God's word and as such is written on our hearts. The law is kind of like a light, it exists to shine in the darkness. It doesn't cause darkness, but it does create an un-ignorable contrast.

Have you ever been in perfect darkness? We like to go down into caverns every now and then, and especially with the nieces and nephew. We went down in one while we were in Gatlinburg. It was cool in that it's the only cavern I've ever been into where the bottom of the cave was probably at about the same level as the bottom of the mountain. You had to drive to the top of this mountain, go inside the cave, and then walk a bit… Any way, the striking thing about caves is that they are one of the only places where you can experience true and perfect darkness. They have you walk into this big chamber, grab hold of your children, and then they turn the lights out. The darkness is so thick it feels like you could probably cut it with a knife. You can put your hand directly in front of your face and still not see it. Its kind of cool, and a little un-nerving or maybe disorienting. They leave the lights off long enough that if your eyes could adjust to that level of darkness they would. But they can't, they weren't designed to work with out some amount of light. Even a small amount of light allows you to see. Like at night, the light of the stars and what not causes you to be able to see somewhat. You can see trees, and other things shadows even that help keep you from walking into things. But in the cavern there is none of that. The inky blackness is all consuming. That's the power of sin, and the law came so that we could see the difference. You maybe have heard that you couldn't appreciate mountains without valleys, or the good times in your life without the bad. Likewise, without something to show holiness, with out the light of the law, we wouldn't be able to recognize evil – and so that is the role that the Law plays now. It is a way for us to see and know right from wrong. Good from evil. When Christ died, he came and fulfilled the law, he completed it.

As MacArthur has taught, the God-ordained role of the law in a fallen world is to reveal the nature of human sin. Christ in his life, death, burial and resurrection was the ultimate consummation of that. What was the most important commandment? Love the Lord your God with all your heart soul mind and strength, and love your neighbor as yourself – all of the law and the prophets rests on this. Think about this for a moment and lets look at Christ's life – just through the lens of the greatest and most important commandment. Did he love God? Of course he does, but lets look at how he lived that out. In Jn 14:15 Jesus said "if you love me you will keep my commandments". At no point in time did Jesus ever disobey a commandment of God. God told him to set aside his divinity and humiliate himself and be born in a feed trough in an animal stable. What did Jesus do? Just as he was told. But I think most importantly, and the story that first came to mind when I thought about the obedience of Christ was the passage luke 22 about Jesus in the garden of gethsemane – you guys remember that story? Who can tell me what happened the night Jesus was betrayed? He went to the garden to pray, and the words of his prayer are really what came to mind here. Jesus said in Luke 22:42: "Father, if you are willing, remove this cup from me. Nevertheless, not my will, but yours, be done." Paul says of Christ in Philippians 2:8 that "he humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even death on a cross." I think its clear that Christ love the Lord God with all his heart, soul, mind and strength. We know he was unfairly tried in a kangaroo court. I The second part of the greatest commandment was to love your neighbor as your self. This too is best demonstrated in his final week and culminating at the cross. He loved us so much, and his father so much that he didn't call upon the angels of heaven to force the man who would scourge him with a cat of nine tails to stop. He didn't cause lightning to rain down from the sky upon those who mocked him. He could have caused all those who didn't believe in him to lose molecular cohesion. Are you familiar with that phrase? There are a number of forces involved in holding everything together on a molecular level. The strong force, the weak force, magnetic forces, and so forth, there are positive and negative charges and all sorts of things like that involved, but science hasn't really figure out just why things stick together the way they do. However a century ago a brilliant individual who would ultimately lose his sanity figured out among other things that sound, and light, and energy travels in waves. He designed the electric motors that we still use to this day, there has been very little variation if any on his design. In fact he took one of his little electric motors and made it spin a little off balance. He attached it to the wall of his building and – over the course of an hour or so his little hand held device caused the building he was in to shimmy and shake, the neighbors thought there was an earthquake, everyone thought the building was coming down and had to beg Tesla to turn his device off. His research eventually showed that molecules – when given the right stimulus (such as sound, or vibration in the case of his building) were relative easy to tear apart. Mythbusters the TV show tackled this myth. They took a little one pound electric motor and battery, attached it to a suspension bridge in California (with permission of course), and in just a few minutes the entirety of this huge suspension bridge was wiggling up and down by something like 4 inches. At the right frequency the molecules lose their ability to stick together, and everything turns to vapor. Tesla had planned to make cellular phones, the internet, television, radio, and electricity all wirelessly instantly available to the entire world, all based on this same set of principles that Christ could have used to cause everyone who tried to hurt him to turn to dust. If Christ had wanted to, the man and his whip could have been vaporized. Anyone who lied about him at trial could have been vaporized. It's easy to win a trial when youre innocent, and those who seek to do you harm turn to dust. But Christ was obedient unto death. He sat through the trial, he allowed himself to be beaten. He allowed the people to mock him. He obediently carried the cross up calvary, why? Because John 15 12-17. 13Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends. 14You are my friends if you do what I command you. 15No longer do I call you servants, for the servant does not know what his master is doing; but I have called you friends, for all that I have heard from my Father I have made known to you. 16 You did not choose me, but I chose you and appointed you that you should go and bear fruit and that your fruit should abide, so that whatever you ask the Father in my name, he may give it to you. 17These things I command you, so that you will love one another. Vs 12 says "this is my commandment, that you love one another as I have loved you". He did all of that because he Loved God with all his heart, with all his soul, with all his might, and he loved his neighbor as himself. Christ completed the law. Where the law was a flashlight in a cave, Christ is a great beacon, a lighthouse of hope. Because of Christ we who believe in him are dead to sin, we are dead to the consequence of the Law, we are new creations, the old has passed away behold all things have been made new! As a Christian you are free from the consequences of the Law, but should still view the moral law as the standard we should strive for.

So the first point was freedom Second point is that our life should be a reflection of this. Who of us can say we live that kind of love? I doubt any of us do all the time, and probably most of us don't most of the time. I don't know about you guys, but I'm still human, and I still fall short in this area. Sometimes I put what I want, and feel like doing ahead of what others need. I can't think of a time Christ did that. He fed people, healed them, saved them from storms and the storms of life. He gave and he submitted to his father, and he was obedient every step of the way until we killed him for it. How about you, looking at your life this week, can you say you've done /everything/ that the Lord has commanded you? I'm not sure I can say that. I've been irritable about helping someone this week, and let my feelings get in the way of doing what was right. So often we let the great "I" get in the way of obedience to Christ. I don't feel like picking so and so up, that means I'd have to leave 20 minutes earlier than I do now, I don't feel like going to the nursing home because I'm tired today. I don't feel like going to church because the kids or grandkids have been crazy today and I'm weary. What was the common word there? I. I. I. I want, I like, I need, I feel. Aren't you glad Christ didn't say any of those things as he carried the cross up the hill to his death? I know I am. We are so unworthy of what God has done for us, we didn't deserve it, we couldn't earn it, we couldn't have been saved by any other means than by the intervention of a great God. Isn't God Good? (all the time) All the Time (God is good).

Finally – and were going to be a little short tonight, because I want to spend some time in prayer as a group again tonight before communion. Finally our third point tonight is this; Even Paul wasn't perfect. Re-read 21-25. Paul says "wretched man that I am". I think we can all identify with that on some level. None of us are perfect, we all strive to be like Christ, but our will just isn't strong enough. One commentator I read said its like when most people go on a diet. My dads been on a diet, and he's been doing really good. How many pounds are you up to? Isn't that great? But any way, this one commentator said that resisting sin in your own will power is like how most Americans diet. He said statistically 3 in 5 people that go on diets gain wait because they lack the will power. Something I don't want you to just read and miss, is that Lost people, people that don't know Jesus don't really care too much about sin. Sin makes you numb. It desensitizes you to things. I was reading an article the other day talking about the sin of pornography and how it affects the way people see people of the opposite sex. It turns them almost like a commodity. This isn't something people consciously do, but it's a byproduct of sin. Porn fuels sex slavery and these horrible things we've been hearing about little girls getting kidnapped, or forced to perform sex acts at highschool football games. Granny talked about this a while back. Where is the outrage of society? Its dulled by the creep of sin in the lives of our country. Those little girls are drugged up, and used in making pornography in soviet block countries, and other places were the rules are lax, or the enforcement of such laws is impossible. Paul is very sensitive to whatever it is that's going on in his life. This is a testament to the work of the Holy Spirit in his life. He cares that he's not doing the things God wants him to. He cares that vs 23 that he's being made captive to the law of sin dwelling in his flesh. He never says what it was specifically that afflicted him, but isn't it re-assuring to know that Paul, the man God used to write half the new testament had something he struggles with? Shall we continue in sin, may it never be Paul says. We need to try to be holy, but we need to know that our strength will only get us so far. We need to wait on the Lord, because he will renew our strength. Last story. Then were going to pray as a group for a few minutes, and then take communion. There was once a man that lived in a small house with only one door. He lived in a valley in the mountains. One morning he wakes up, and opens the door to go out side and finds a huge stone has rolled in front of the door. He pushes on the rock tries to roll it away, and fails. This makes him despondent, hows he going to eat, to drink? He's surely dead. At that moment God sent an angel to deliver a message to him. The angel said "Jesus requests that you push on the rock" The man says sure, but what about my food? What about drink? How am I to live? The angel says don't worry, my God will supply your needs, but Jesus asks you to push on the rock. So he pushes on the rock, minutes roll by, hours roll by. God provides his food, and the man keeps pushing on the rock. Days go by this way, the man wakes up, and pushes on the rock with everything he's got. Days turn into weeks, weeks turn into months and months turn into years. Every day the man is diligent, and every day God provides him food. After a number of years the man is sitting their eating and it occurs to him that he's pushed on this rock for years and years now, and the rock hasn't moved. God's been faithful, but still the rock is there, and he wonders if he hasn't perhaps failed God in some way. So he prays to God. Every morning he starts praying to God, help me move the rock, help me do your will. Every night he goes to bed tired. Another year goes by and still the man is struggling with this rock. Finally one night he got angry with God, and decided to quit pushing on the rock. He prayed to God in his anguish and pleaded with God to move the rock. He said "God I've pushed and I've pushed and I've pushed. I've done so every day and to the best of my ability but still there is this rock. Please let me out of this trap! When he had finished praying and opened his eyes the angel was standing there. The angel said "look at your self in the mirror, you've slimmed down, your muscles are toned, you're much more fit to live as you've been in this valley. Before you were the only one, now look – other people are moving into the valley, and most importantly like your body, your relationship with the Lord has deepend as well. In the same way you're biceps have grown your faith has grown, your triceps have grown true, but so has your obedience. God has big plans for you, he just needed you to push on the rock for a while. It was never ever about moving the rock, it was always about the work God was doing in you through the Rock. At that moment, the angel waved his hand and the rock rolled away.

I think so often we are like that man. Sometimes God just takes struggles away from people true, but sometimes he lets us have, and I think in truth if were all honest with each other each of us has a rock covering our door. Something we are asked to push against. It may be emotional baggage, or a sin God's helping you deal with, it may be arrogance. So many people struggle with that one. Ultimately every sin I can think of comes back to one letter in the alphabet "I". Take heart though, God has plans for you plans to prosper you and not to harm you. So keep pushing on the rock. Keep struggling through that baggage towards the Lords goal for you, and in his timing, he'll move it for us.

Maybe you are sitting there tonight and have never asked Jesus into your heart, and through the service tonight, or any of the other nights you've been here you realize that there are things you do, things in your life that are sinful. You realize that you don't want to get what you deserve for your sin, you want the Free gift that God has for you instead through his son Jesus. The good news is that God chose you, remember Jn 15:16? It says God chose you. Scripture says He knew you before the foundation of the earth, that he knit you together in your mothers womb, that he loves you, has a plan for you, a purpose – and it all starts with accepting a free gift. If that's you tonight, when the praise team comes up and sings, come talk to you.

Maybe you're a Christian, but you've been wandering and are feeling numb to some things you know you shouldn't be. If so, come talk to me.

Maybe you've never been baptized, if so come talk to me.
Maybe you're just looking for a church home, and would like to become a member and have a say in the business meetings, and direction of the church. If so, come talk to me.

I love you guys! Don't forget that!
