MAIN TEXT: ROMANS 8:12–17 (ESV)

"So then, brothers, we are debtors, not to the flesh, to live according to the flesh. For if you
live according to the flesh you will die, but if by the Spirit you put to death the deeds of the
body, you will live. For all who are led by the Spirit of God are sons of God. For you did not
receive the spirit of slavery to fall back into fear, but you have received the Spirit of adoption
as sons, by whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!' The Spirit himself bears witness with our spirit that we
are God's children, and if children, then heirs — heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ,
provided we suffer with him in order that we may also be glorified with him."

INTRODUCTION: MINI-GOLF IN JAPAN

The illustration is a personal story: a relative by marriage borrowed several million dollars
to open a mini-golf course in Japan. After three years the business failed — the Japanese public
had no interest in mini-golf. He returned to the US broke, in debt beyond recovery, having also
spent his personal savings. He moved in with his father while getting back on his feet.

The core point: he owed a debt he could never hope to repay — parallel to humanity's debt before
God.

[Note: Cultural context included — Japan's emphasis on miniaturization (smartwatches, capsule
hotels) as backdrop to explain why mini-golf seemed like an opportunity. The capsule hotel
description is broadly accurate. The smartwatch/computer comment is illustrative hyperbole.]

ROMANS 8:12–13 — DEBTORS TO THE FLESH

Before Christ, we were debtors to the flesh.

Romans 3:23 — referenced: "because of that sin, we deserved death."

[FLAG: Romans 3:23 (ESV) reads: "for all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." It does
not reference death as the penalty; that comes from Romans 6:23. The pastor appears to be
combining both verses (3:23 and 6:23) in the same breath, which many preachers do in an
evangelistic sequence. When presenting this in print or as a formal teaching, the references
should be kept distinct: Romans 3:23 establishes universal sin; Romans 6:23 establishes death as
sin's wage.]

Romans 6:23 — quoted directly and accurately:
"The wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."
(ESV — confirmed.)

Romans 8:13 — accurately paraphrased: if you live by the flesh, you will die. The pastor
clarifies this is eternal death (separation from God), not only physical death.

ILLUSTRATION: Adam and Eve; God created a perfect world, but humanity rejected it through sin.
Used to answer the objection: "If God is good and all-powerful, why is the world so terrible?"
Answer: God created it perfect. Humanity, through the Fall and through every sin, rejected that
world and chose this one.

ILLUSTRATION: The pastor's cousin who tried to trade unfairly — good baseball cards for junk,
doing chores for video game time, claiming it was for the pastor's benefit. Compared to Satan's
deception of Adam and Eve: traded a perfect world for a fallen one. Vivid trading comparisons:
a Porsche for a Yugo; a Grand Master Dressage horse for an untrained Shetland pony; a 747 for
a soggy paper airplane.

ILLUSTRATION: A "friend" who repeatedly hurt the pastor and then feigned repentance, ultimately
stealing the pastor's parents' identity and having utilities disconnected. This is compared to
humanity's pattern of returning to sin after repentance — a slap in the face of Christ.

Caveat: For believers, no sin is so severe as to lose salvation — but it is still a rejection of
Christ. For unbelievers, ongoing sin leads to exactly what is deserved.

Monopoly analogy: the "Go Directly to Jail" chance card vs. Boardwalk and Park Place — every
person living by the flesh has chosen the jail card.

Romans 5:8 — quoted:

[FLAG: ESV reads "while we were STILL sinners" not "yet sinners." Minor paraphrase. If quoting

(Second instance) "if you confess with your mouth and believe in your heart that Jesus is Lord

ESV (Romans 10:9): "because, if you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in

[FLAG: The first instance ("confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus Christ") differs from ESV

ILLUSTRATION RETURN: The son from the mini-golf story returns to his father, confesses total
failure and debt. The father says: "Don't fret. I have a plan." The next evening, after preparing
dinner for his father, the son learns his father has paid every debt — the US bank, the Japanese
bank, the credit cards. Everything. "You've been redeemed." Parallel to Christ paying our debt
in full.

Romans 8:15 — accurately referenced:
"You have received the Spirit of adoption as sons, by whom we cry, 'Abba! Father!'" (ESV.)
We are made heirs to God's kingdom — heirs with Christ.

ELECTION — ROMANS 8:28–30

Romans 8:28 — referenced accurately:
"And we know that for those who love God all things work together for good, for those who are
called according to his purpose." (ESV.)

Romans 8:29–30 — referenced but not quoted in transcript (noted as "read verses" — pastor read
from the Bible):
"For those whom he foreknew he also predestined to be conformed to the image of his Son, in
order that he might be the firstborn among many brothers. And those whom he predestined he also
called, and those whom he called he also justified, and those whom he justified he also
glorified." (ESV.)

Point: Just as adoptive parents choose a specific child, God chooses us. The doctrine of election.

"The king of Kings, Lord of lords, ruler of all rules. Omnipotent God of the universe who uses
the heavens as a footstool, who knows you — flaws and all — has chosen you."

[FLAG: "Uses the heavens as a footstool" is a conflation. Isaiah 66:1 (ESV): "Thus says the
Lord: 'Heaven is my throne, and the earth is my footstool.'" Heaven is God's throne; the earth
is his footstool — not the heavens as the footstool. The intended point (God's vastness) is
correct; the specific image should be "uses the earth as a footstool" to match Scripture.]

Ephesians 2:8–9 — referenced accurately:
"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift
of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast." (ESV.)

God chose the named congregation member Kurt not because of anything special in Kurt, but by
grace alone. A personal, congregational illustration of election.
