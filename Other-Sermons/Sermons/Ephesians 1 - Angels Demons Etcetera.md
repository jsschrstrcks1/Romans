## Opening — The Week from Hell

Context: Week of breakdowns — four of five vehicles down, bugs, banking problems, sick kids and adults, broken lawn equipment, dad's back, sheep escaping fence.

## I. Cultural Obsession with Angels

Chuck Hastings quote: angels on coffee mugs, t-shirts, checkbooks, license plates, soap dispensers, Bubble Angel Barbie. Our culture has made angels into decorations.

TV preacher with mountain of credit card statements claiming to bind "demon of debt" with made-up words.

## II. Pseudepigrapha and False Teaching

Three groups of ancient books:
1. **Canon** — the 66 books of Scripture
2. **Apocrypha** — accepted by Catholics, rejected by Protestants
3. **Pseudepigrapha** — claim to be scripture but are verifiably false

**How we know they're fake**: Language dates documents. "Groovy" = 1960s. "Bad" meaning good = 1980s. "Booyah," "psych," "fly" = 1990s. "Bae," "lit," "snowflake" = 2010s. If a document claims Paul wrote it but uses language from centuries later — it's a forgery.

**Pseudepigraphal examples**:
- Sarah married seven times, each husband killed by a demon on the wedding night. A blind man and the angel Raphael burn fish liver and heart to scare the demon away. This is the root of the practice of mumbling nonsense to "bind" demons.
- Solomon's ring to bind demons. Interrogating demons for their names and weakness words — Orniskelos causes sore throats, flees from Raphael; Hellorath causes gas, flees if you mutter "aiarhea." The ring was broken when Solomon dallied with female demons.

This is where the modern charismatic obsession with "binding" demons and learning their names comes from — not from Scripture, but from pseudepigraphal folklore.

## III. What Scripture Actually Teaches

> "Little children, you are from God and have overcome them, for he who is in you is greater than he who is in the world." — 1 John 4:4 (ESV)

> "He has delivered us from the domain of darkness and transferred us to the kingdom of his beloved Son, in whom we have redemption, the forgiveness of sins." — Colossians 1:13-14 (ESV)

Ephesians 1 teaches that believers are **sealed with the Holy Spirit** — believers cannot be demon-possessed. Angels are sentient tools in God's hands — the hero is God, not the angels.

**Cargo cult analogy**: A tribe worships cargo ships instead of the God who sent them. Don't worship the boat.

## IV. The Dualism Heresy

Treating everything as a galactic fight between angels and demons fails by leaving out the sovereign God. The battle is already won. We are sealed and protected by the Holy Spirit.

This worldview is also used to remove individual responsibility for sin. "The devil made me do it" — this excuse has its origins in the Garden:
- Eve: "The serpent deceived me"
- Adam: "The woman *you* gave me"

**Personal application**: The devil didn't break the lawn mower, the trucks, the Buick, the AC, or the Hyundai brakes. He didn't give you gas or make you sign up for an irresponsible credit card. He didn't make you embrace a life of sin. "You chose that. You are responsible for that."

## V. The Riches of God (from Ephesians 1)

Article on lottery winners: winning was the worst thing that happened to most of them — spending, shysters, debt, dying alone.

God's riches are different. Every euro, pound, yen, dollar belongs to God. Your house is "God's house He lets you live in."

Beyond material riches: perfect goodness, grace, patience — all treasures, all costly to learn. "You only want to ask for patience if you're ready to encounter situations that make you impatient."

God lavishes on us "in accordance with His wisdom."
