This will be our last week in this first chapter of Peter. 

As a quick refresher, we as believers in Jesus Christ are the elect exiles. Chosen of God but still exiled in this world for the duration of God’s plan for us. Being chosen is a wonderful thing. A gift we could never hope to deserve when we look at God’s standard, vs how we have lived our life. It’s a gift that he KNOWS us deeply and truly to our core, and chooses to love us any way. 

Chose to let his son die for us any way. 

Its important to remember that Peter is writing this likely from rome. Who was the roman emperor at this point? Nero. 

Nero who starting as a pre-teen, began trying to murder his mother, who succeeded in 56 in doing so. Nero who the historian Tacitus claimed had rome burned down, and the hired groups of thugs to assault anyone who tried to stop their home from burning. Nero who took great joy in having Christians crucified. Nero who would force Christians to fight against lions bare handed. Nero who lit the streets of rome by setting Christians on fire.  Nero who tradition holds ended the lives of both Paul and Peter. Is the one on the throne. The fires of ad64 may have just happened. Remember the line that Nero fiddled while rome burned? Nero may have even started the fires as a way to build his fancy new city. Nero as you may have guessed was not a nice man. 

In fact Tacitus writes: a vast multitude were convicted, not so much on the charge of burning the city, as of "hating the human race."

In their very deaths they were made the subjects of sport: for they were covered with the hides of wild beasts, and worried to death by dogs, or nailed to crosses, or set fire to, and when the day waned, burned to serve for the evening lights. Nero offered his own garden players for the spectacle, and exhibited a Circensian game, indiscriminately mingling with the common people in the dress of a charioteer, or else standing in his chariot. For this cause a feeling of compassion arose towards the sufferers, though guilty and deserving of exemplary capital punishment, because they seemed not to be cut off for the public good, but were victims of the ferocity of one man.” 

Circensian means the circus. They were doing these things to Christians as part of their regular circus events. Come to see the elephants perform, and see a half hundred Christians wrapped in animal skins and ripped apart by dogs. Or set on fire, or used as target practice. Or crucified. Or burned for street lighting. 

Can you even imagine? 

The early stages of this Neronian persecution are the “troubles” Peter wrote about. 

Nero wasn’t just bad to other people, he was ate up with sin. Anything sinful and he was wrapped up in it. Every kind of sexual deviancy he’s reported to have indulged in. Indulge being a kind word for what he would have done. 

Ultimately after 13 years of terror, and disruption, rape, murder, and fire Nero’s reign came to an end when the senate deposed him. Shortly after he committed suicide. 

This is why Peter wrote “In this you rejoice, though now for a little while, if necessary, you have been grieved by various trials, 7 so that the tested genuineness of your faith—more precious than gold that perishes though it is tested by fire—may be found to result in praise and glory and honor at the revelation of Jesus Christ.”

Trials for a little while indeed. 

RC Sproul and Peter Remind us that in the midst of all that, that we as Christians hold a  “privileged and secure position as the objects of God’s sovereign, gracious, eternal choice to be His people in and through Jesus Christ”. 

He won’t let his people be wasted needlessly, but he will task them with accomplishing hard things for his glory, and to bring people into his kingdom. 

It strikes me that at this point in the letter, Peter  could begin to speak out against the autrocities in rome. He could, and maybe would have been right to condemn Nero’s sexual deviancy. He would have been right to speak out against the idolatry. Against the murder of Christians for no reason. He could have used his pulpit to call for the Senate to remove Nero as emperor. The senate was already concerned about him. They may not have listened, but Paul could have tried. He could have stood on his political soap box, and decried the evils and excesses of rome. Or even of the individual freedoms lost by Christians being impaled on stakes, soaked in oil, and set afire to ignite rome. 

But we don’t see that do we? I want you to watch for that as we read this chapter, and as we work through the rest of the book. Watch for places where Paul gets political, and addresses Rome, or Nero, and the senate. Tell me when you see it, if you see it. 

Spoiler – you won’t. Instead what Does Peter call us to do? 

Lets read.  We will read the whole chapter. But pay attention to what Peter says we are to do next. 

It isn’t picketing. It isn’t marching on the capital, demanding someone else be president. It isn’t riots, and burning rome. Peter calls us to something that is even more important. 

Lets read. 

Did you see it? Peter commanding us to rebel, and go to war against rome? Against any and all who would dare impinge upon my rights? 

Me either. Instead his instructions are clear, and start around vs 13. Prepare for action. Be soberminded. 

Sober minded here includes both alcohol and things like recreational use of  Marijuana. Keep your mind clear, be ready for action. Things are coming for a time, that we have to be ready for. He doesn’t call for civil disobedience in our passage, he calls for radical obedience. A child like obedience; and then commands us to be holy. Not when you can be. Just Be Holy, for I am Holy vs 16. Almost the entire rest of the chapter is about being holy. Its such a central theme that most of the bibles I checked say “be holy” as a header, as a description for the contents of that whole section of scripture. Be Holy. 

Kevin De Young writes “There’s no question holiness is one of the central themes in the Bible. The word “holy” occurs more than 600 times in the Bible, more than 700 when you include derivative words like holiness, sanctify, and sanctification. You can’t make sense of the Bible without understanding that God is holy and that this holy God is intent on making a holy people to live with him forever in a holy heaven.
The whole system of Israel’s worship revolves around holiness. That’s why you have holy people (the priests), with holy clothes, in a holy land (Canaan), at a holy place (tabernacle/ temple), using holy utensils and holy objects, celebrating holy days, living by a holy law, so that they might be a kingdom of priests and a holy nation.
Be Holy. 

We live in a new testament context, under the new testament rules. Freed from the old testament rules that have been satisfied in Christ. I read a story about an animal that lives In the forests of northern Europe and Asia lives little animal called the ermine, known for his snow-white fur in winter. He instinctively protects his white coat against anything that would soil it. Fur hunters take advantage of this unusual trait of the ermine. They don’t set a snare to catch him, but instead they find his home, which is usually a cleft in a rock or a hollow in an old tree. They smear the entrance and interior with grime. Then the hunters set their dogs loose to find and chase the ermine. The frightened animal flees toward home but doesn’t enter because of the filth. Rather than soil his white coat, he is trapped by the dogs and captured while preserving his purity. For the ermine, his purity – his Holiness lets say, is more precious than life. 

We have no holiness of our own right? Our best works are as filfthy rags scripture says. Whatever holiness we have belongs to Jesus. 

Missionary Lou Nichols wrote about it in terms of canning. Any of you ladies can? My mother and grandmother both do. Any way, Lou wrote this: I grew up on a farm and my mother used to make jam, I would watch her pour wax over the top of the preserves until it was at the brim of the jar. She would then carefully wipe some wax around the rim itself, and place the rubber lined lid on tight. The wax and the vacuum caused by the cooling preserves would seal that jar so tight that it could have sat on a basement shelf for years, and the jam would have been good when finally opened. Sealed, preserved, protected. The Word of God was written under the inspiration of the Holy Spirit of God, and after listening to the message of truth, the good news of our salvation, and having believed, we were sealed in Him (Christ) by the Holy Spirit of promise”. A seal of God’ ownership was placed over our lives, and unlike Pilate’s seal, no power in Heaven or earth can break that seal, a seal that means a great deal to God. He shut us up tightly. We are set aside for His use, for His eternal fellowship. Preserved, protected until the day of the redemption of our bodies (or glorification). 

Part of being sealed by God, is receiving Jesus Holiness. 

What exactly are you doing with that fine ermine coat he has granted you? Are you conformed to the passions of your former ignorance? Or are you taking holiness seriously? We’ve been sealed. We’ve been given a precious coat that cost Christ everything. 

Are you living like that matters? 

Because it does. The world around us is watching. Your kids, and grandkids are watching. Your neighbor is watching, and what do they learn about your Jesus from the way you live your life? 

D. L. Moody once said “A holy life will make the deepest impression. Lighthouses blow no horns, they just shine.” 

Are you out there shining? Being holy?

Salt is useful because it preserves meat. Light protects. We are called to be salt and light. To preserve and protect. 

But does that mean crowing about our politics on facebook 24/7? 

Or does it mean that we should be holy. That we should be sharing the gospel, and teaching others to be holy. To avoid those things God has shown us are sinful In our lives. To forsake the passions of our former ignorance. Instead put our efforts into things that matter. 

We have been ransomed from those old things at a high cost. They don’t have a hold on us any more. They don’t have to define us. God knew us, and extended to us his holiness any way.

"For the time is coming when people will not endure sound teaching, but will accumulate for themselves teachers to suit their own passions, and will turn away from listening to the truth and wander off into myths" (2 Timothy 4:3-4).

If you are going to listen to God you must listen to ALL He has to say. We don’t’ get to pick and choose what feels good. 

If God tells us to be soberminded, we should be sober minded. 

If God tells us to go to the missions field, then we should go to the missions field. 

God says whoever gives up their life for my sake will find it. 

That’s the truth. That’s the promise. It costs us everything we have to gain more than we could have ever hoped for. Freedom, and joy, and peace eternal. Grace and forgiveness, mercy and healing. He’ll meet you wherever you are. The glorious, and painful, and dangerous, and wonderful part is that he loves you too much to leave you where he finds you. 

God chastises those he loves. 

He works in us to squeeze the sin from our lives. To purify us, and make us worthy of the price that was paid for us. 

A lot of preachers get up here and say that if you just accept Jesus everything will be candy and roses. Happy, healthy, wealthy, and secure. Following Jesus has a cost. It trades all the bad in you, for all the good in Christ. It’s a glorious and beautiful trade. But it means you’ll have days you are unhappy. When you’re unhappy, God’s there supporting you, holding you up. Like the footprints in the sand poem. It means there are days you’ll be unhealthy. But God is the great physician, and one day when we stand in his presence these old bodies that let us down won’t be decrepit any more. No more diabetes, or heart disease, or liver problems in eternity. No cancer. Or addiction. All those problems stay here. No depression in heaven. No sadness. Only happy tears, and Jesus is right there to wipe away every tear. 

No mental health struggles. Mental health issues is just one more way our body can let us down, like diabetes or heart disease. One day mental health issues will be gone too. And best of all we will be standing in the presence of our savior. 

But all that starts with the gospel.
