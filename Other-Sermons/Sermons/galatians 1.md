The backstory behind this text revolves around a group of people called the judaizers. Have you ever heard of them? They were a group of people that were trying to add things on top of salvation. In these Galatian churches, the judaizers were trying to enforce elements of the old testament law in these new testament churches. Circumcision, and dietary laws were two of the big hot button issues. The men of the judaizers would pull Christian men out and into a room and then proceed to check him to make sure he was circumcised. When it came to food, Paul and Peter had huge fight over whether or not it was Ok to violate the old testament law in light of Christ’s fulfillment of the law. Paul wrote a whole chapter chastising Peter, and at the end of the day, after God gave peter a dream, Peter agreed Paul was right. So these are just some of the things the judaizers were arguing for. Basically they were adding requirements to salvation. Adding or taking away from scripture is the source of every heresy and false religion tied to Christianity that exists. These five things became the pillars of the reformation and even though the reformers like Luther didn’t express them so succinctly if you were to boil down what they had to say to their essentials, they would be arguing for the same five things – in latin these were sola scriptura, or through scripture alone, sola gratia, or through grace alone, sola fide, or through faith alone, sola christus, through Christ alone, and fifthly sola deo Gloria or glory to God alone.
