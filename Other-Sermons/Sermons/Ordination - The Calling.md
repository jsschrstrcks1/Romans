The lesson here isn't just for Kenny, but for each one of us. When we ask for God to prepare us to be a sanctuary, sometimes we envision a team of maids coming in to prepare our sanctuary by polish the windows and dusting the knick-knacks, or a message from the pulpit so stirring, so moving that without us really having to do anything, God makes a change
In reality,
We experience rejection that we learn how to love others and make them feel accepted
We are afflicted so that we learn how to understand that God is in control and learn how to have joy in the midst of suffering
We go through troubles (sometimes it doesn't feel like we ever get "through") so that we learn to have peace in the midst of troubles
We go hungry so we understand how to be generous
We go without so we understand how to be thankful for what we have,
We are humiliated so that we understand how to be humble
We have people in our life that aggravate us so that we learn self control

The service
What we envision
Billy Graham preaching in front of millions, 10's of thousands coming to Christ, On tv, admired, quoted, emulated
In the worst case giving our life for Christ in a spectacular martyrdom that is recorded in history and is the cause of many people turning to Christ

The reality
You may spend your entire life toiling faithfully and never seen God "come through" for you like Abraham who spent his whole life waiting for what God promised (descendants like sand on the sea shore) but died an old man with only two sons and one of them illegitimate.
You may be like the shy man who agreed to teach the boys Sunday school class. He felt like such a failure, there was even one kid who kept falling asleep during Sunday school. So one day he worked up his courage and went to the shoe store when the kid worked to speak to him about Jesus one on one. He went away feeling depressed, that he had been ineffective.
If we could look ahead and see that it wouldn't be until you were in your 90's that you would have a child and see what God did through that child faith would be easy
If we could look ahead and see that God took the seed that had been planted in that kid, and through that SS teachers faithful efforts used that kid to draw 10's of millions of people to his son, and hundreds of millions would hear the gospel as a result…faith would be easy
But that's not often how it works-
Faith is trusting gods sovereignty even when we don't understand, even when we don't like the path we are on.
And lean not on your own understanding and acknowledge him in all your ways

Son, my charge to you is;
continue pressing on in the face of discouragement
running with endurance the race set before you, even when your strength is gone and you just don't have anything left
set a standard for your life that can be measured with the bible
Don't lift yourself up, but always hide behind the cross so that you don't obstruct peoples view of God
Live a life worthy of the calling you have received.
