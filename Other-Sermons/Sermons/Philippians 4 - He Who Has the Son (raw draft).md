## Introduction

Imagine you go on a trip for 80-90 days to somewhere in Europe. Airlines in Europe don't let you carry much home with you, in fact once you account for your clothes, there is no room left to take anything else besides your clothing. In fact while you're here you have a job, and the only souvenir you can take is whatever cash you can deposit in your bank account. Under those circumstances, who would fill their room with trinkets? Buying furniture, memorabilia? And who would save their money in their bank account. It's like that for us now! We are only here on earth for a short time – 80-90 days on average. Anything we accumulate here, can't go with us in the end.

## Unity — Philippians 4:1-3

Paul really loved this church. The Macedonian call in Acts. Church was founded by a river by women. The benefactor of this church was a woman. Church met in a woman's house. What an honor to be called his crown! No other church given this honor.

Division in the ranks. Two women having a disagreement. In eastern culture, the norm was not to address concerns directly. This would have been shocking, but less shocking to them than leaving their names out entirely. Polemical literature.

Find a mediator. Plead your case. Relationship is restored.

Paul calls these two women out, and speaks to them directly. They were his friends. They were most likely in leadership – they served along side Paul (vs 3). Reminds me of Facebook or dealing with two siblings in a squabble. Both probably had written to Paul pleading their case. Paul doesn't side with either of them. Tells them to find unity, to be at peace, to be of the same mind in Christ Jesus.

Considering the previous chapter was about being wary of false teachers, its possibly that they were arguing over how to best deal with heretics in the community. Paul's answer? Point them to Christ.

When we encounter frustrations that cause lack of unity, what should we do?

Look to Christ – "Rejoice in the Lord always; again I will say, rejoice!" (Phil 4:4) *(See flags — corrected to ESV.)*

Prayer brings our requests before the Lord (vs 6).

Worship helps re-align our emotional state (vs 7).

Approach directly, and in love. "Be angry and do not sin; do not let the sun go down on your anger." (Eph 4:26) *(See flags — corrected to ESV.)*

## Worship — Philippians 4:8

Whatever is true. God is a God of truth. He doesn't lie, deceive, or manipulate with half truths or whole lies. If God says it, you can believe it. It's a check you can take to the bank. 2 Timothy 3:16 says all Scripture is breathed out by God. The bible, is the word of God. It is true, and a letter of love from our creator and sustainer of the entire universe.

Whatever is noble. God is the king of kings and Lord of Lords. Noble has a second meaning. Websters says it means to be of high morality. Noble can also mean a coin that was remarkable for being of the purest gold they could make at the time.

Whatever is right and pure. This builds upon the last two. Because HE is truth, because he is noble in every sense of the word, then he is also right. His ways are the moral high ways, his ways are truth. His ways are right. He is the one true God most remarkable for being the most pure. He is fully righteous.

If it is lovely, excellent, admirable, or praiseworthy, then think on these things.

What else besides God the father, the Son, and Holy Spirit meet all of these requirements?

So often we become so consumed with things that are of a lower focus. How I feel. What I want. What I like. What I "think" I need. I want my house clean, I want a new car, I want to go on vacation, me me me I I I. None of those are inherently bad. But when we are more focused on them, than on others, and especially when we are more focused on these things than on our Lord and savior, then it is wickedly sinful!

This shows our priorities in life! Are you your first priority? Or is God?

You can't out give God. (Haiti Missions Trip).

A friend wrote a song about how he didn't want to waste his life. He wrote how money doesn't matter, possessions, pets, and the so called "Good life" are all fun, but you're still basically wasting your life.

So much of our life is spent pursuing things that don't matter. So much conversation is wasted on things that don't matter.

Instead of talking to people about the weather, maybe you should tell them about the impact Christ has made in your life? Instead of talking to people about your fantasy football team, maybe you should talk to them about how you're letting Jesus call the shots in your own life?

In 10000 years will it matter to you what Tom Brady's completion stats are? Will it matter whether your kids have heard the Gospel?

If those worldly things are all you achieve in life, you've missed out on the best parts, both here and hereafter.

Store up your treasures in heaven.

### Hay and the Stubble

Collected a list of things without being given an explanation. Each was labeled, and tossed into a fire. The useless things were gone! Bed times, disagreements, politics, addictions burn away. Selfish wants, and desires. All burned. All except, knowing Christ, the fruits of the spirit, and other things like evangelism.

## Gospel Close — He Who Has the Son

As we begin to draw the service to a close, I want you to think about one more thing in our passage today. In vs 3 it talks about people having their name written in the book of life.

"Whoever has the Son has life." (1 John 5:12) *(See flags — corrected to ESV.)*

A young man served in Vietnam. Saw things that tore him up. He came back to the states traumatized and barely able to cope with the things he had seen and done. He was unmarried, in his twenties. Perhaps the same age as some of your kids or grand kids. He moved back in with his father and lived on in depression. His father, a collector of art, encouraged him to take this course at the local community center on dealing with pain through art. So the son did. In 16 weeks he painted a self portrait. He poured every bit of grief, anger, and confusion he felt into this painting. However he still felt empty, confused, hurt and angry, and a short time later took his own life. He couldn't cope on his own with the things he had seen over seas.

The father, a world renowned art collector quit buying art after all these things had transpired. As it happened the last piece of art he added to his massive collection was this self portrait of his son. He hung the picture (which he called "the son") on the mantle over the fireplace. It was the first thing you would see upon entering his home.

A number of years later this man passed away, and his massive art collection went up for auction. He had no heirs you see, and so he designated that whatever money the sale of his art would bring be donated to an organization that reaches out to these veterans. On the day of the auction the media was present, scores of collectors, basically the hoi polloi of Los Angeles turned out hoping to bring home a Degas, Monet, Dali, or any of the more than 200 famous pieces of art owned by the father.

As the auction was starting a man walked in off the street. He was a construction worker on break, and decided to see what all the fuss was about over his lunch break. At that moment the auctioneer came out and unveiled the first painting. It was the father's favorite, the one his son had painted before committing suicide. The auctioneer began calling, asking for bids to start at 100 million dollars. The crowd chuckled and yelled bring out the masters! The auctioneer ignored them and pleaded with them to buy "the son." Explaining how this painting was the owner's favorite! The crowd continued to jeer and jape.

But the auctioneer kept calling "million million million, do I hear a million, hundred hundred hundred thousand, do I hear a hundred thousand." On and on this went until the auctioneer got down to a hundred dollars. Finally he asked for offers. The construction worker on his break was moved by the man's love of the painting, and wanting to be a part of this day counted out what he had in his pocket, raised his hand and shouted six dollars! The auctioneer fell silent for a moment, shocked, and began calling "7 7 7 do I hear 7$." The crowd was silent. Hoping that perhaps a Van Gogh would come out next. Eventually the auctioneer closed the bidding on "the Son," thanked everyone for coming out and began to close down the auction.

Everyone was shocked. But what about the rest they cried?! The auctioneer went on to explain that the father had left very specific instructions: he who gets the son, gets it all.

*(See flags — this illustration is an apocryphal parable, not a true story. Traced to Ivor Powell, Bible Windows, 1954. The Vietnam details are later embellishments.)*

How true that is for us today. So many people go to church, or take communion, or try to live good lives thinking that will be enough. But those same people one day will be standing in front of our Lord and savior Jesus Christ and he will say to them: "I do not know where you come from. Depart from me, all you workers of evil!" (Luke 13:27) *(See flags — corrected to ESV; original blended Luke 13 and Matthew 7:23.)*

When we stand in front of the gates of heaven. It won't matter what kind of car you drove here on earth. The size of your house won't matter. Your accumulation of earthly wealth, power and prestige will all be gone. The ONLY thing that will matter is whether or not you have accepted Jesus Christ as your Lord and savior.

"For there is one God, and there is one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus." (1 Tim 2:5) *(See flags — corrected "mankind" to ESV "men".)*

"For by grace you have been saved through faith. And this is not your own doing; it is the gift of God, not a result of works, so that no one may boast." (Eph 2:8-9) *(See flags — corrected from NIV to ESV.)*

"For Christ also suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, that he might bring us to God, being put to death in the flesh but made alive in the spirit." (1 Peter 3:18)

Who did the actions in those verses? Jesus. Jesus died – while we were still sinners! Jesus brought us to God. The Holy Spirit worked in us even before we were saved so that we could comprehend the gospel. *(See flags — "still sinners" corrected from KJV "yet sinners".)*

"In this is love, not that we have loved God but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the propitiation for our sins." (1 John 4:10) Charles Ryrie says "propitiation means placating or satisfying the wrath of God by the atoning sacrifice of Christ" (*Basic Theology*, Moody Publishers). *(See flags — Ryrie quote verified.)*

You see we are all sinners. Romans 3:23.

"For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." (Romans 6:23)

The Good News. "But God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us." (Romans 5:8) *(See flags — corrected from KJV to ESV.)*

"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life." (John 3:16) *(See flags — corrected from KJV/NIV blend to ESV.)*

"Because, if you confess with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." (Romans 10:9) *(See flags — corrected from KJV to ESV.)*

Folks if you've never done that – if you've never confessed with your mouth, and believed in your heart, then you aren't saved. It doesn't matter how good you are, how many times or how recently you've been to church. It doesn't matter if you've taken communion, been confirmed, or helped old ladies across the street. That doesn't matter. Scripture says our righteous deeds are like a polluted garment! *(See flags — corrected "filthy rags" (KJV) to ESV "polluted garment"; ref. Isaiah 64:6.)*

Maybe you're sitting here and you are realizing that perhaps you have been wasting your life. Perhaps you're letting other "things" or people, or circumstances take your focus off Christ, and you realize you need to do better.

Maybe you haven't been in church, haven't been worshipping like you ought. Maybe you've just wandered away from the Lord for a time and now realize you need to come back.

Today is the day, NOW is the time for you to return to Christ, or to accept Christ as your Lord and savior. In a moment the praise team is going to come up here and sing a song, and while they are singing if any of those descriptions sounds like you then I want you to come up here, kneel at the altar (body permitting) and get right with the Lord. If you don't know Jesus, if you've never confessed with your mouth, and believed in your heart come talk to me, and I will help you through that process. Perhaps you have been looking for a church home, and would like to join this one. Come talk to me about that as well. When we have a few people, we will start a new member's class.

### Illustrations Flagged

17. **Vietnam veteran / art auction illustration** ("He who gets the son gets it all"): **This is an apocryphal parable, not a true story.** The earliest known version traces to Ivor Powell, *Bible Windows* (Zondervan, 1954, pp. 27-28). The Vietnam War details, world-renowned art collector, self-portrait, and suicide are later embellishments added as the story was retold across decades. Snopes classifies it as "glurge" (inspirational fiction circulated as true). The core moral — "he who gets the Son gets it all" — is an allegory for 1 John 5:12. If reused, present as a parable/illustration rather than as a true story.
