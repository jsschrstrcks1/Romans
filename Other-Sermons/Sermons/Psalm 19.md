Speaking of, lets read Psalms 19. 

Verses 1-6 are talking about General revelation. That is the idea that God has revealed a bit about who He is in creation. We can see his creativity in every sunset, and mountain vista. His orderliness in the consistency of the son moving across the sky, and the seasons being similar across the generations. We see it in how the stars move orderly across the sky. I learned on the austrailia cruise that the southern cross used to be in the northern hemisphere. You could see from northern Canada this cool arrangement of stars that looks like a cross. That inspired catholics of old to duplicate it on the ground in nova scotia (nolans cross). That is so ingrained in our minds now as being a part of the southern hemisphere that we call it the southern cross. 

In truth, it only looks like a cross from our perspective. If we were to travel a thousand times a thousand years and pulled up along side it in our space ship, it actually is more like a conical spiral of stars. What looks like 4 or 5 stars to us, is bunches. There I an order to the things that God creates, and he uses the things he has created to teach us about himselves. 

I hear people say they have never felt closer to God than when they were out in nature, and to a point I accept and agree with that. There is so much of God’s wonder on display in creation for us to enjoy. I really felt that in Utah, with the massive majestic mountains all around, in the middle of the beautiful desert, topped by the milky way. Its one of the few places you can stand there and see it still with your own eyes. 

Back in a different time, president Theodore Roosevelt who was also reportedly a Christian had a habit of taking the dignitaries that came to visit him outside, and bearing in mind this was before we had light pollution to the extent we do today, and he would stare off into the nights sky. Eventually the visitors would too, and he would take time to enjoy and revel in the majesty of God’s creation. After a time he would start pointing things out:
“That little faint speck of light is the Spiral Galaxy in Andromeda.
“It is as large as our Milky Way.
“It is one of a hundred million galaxies.
“It is 750,000 light-years away.
“It consists of one hundred billion suns, each larger than our sun.”
After a while he would stop talking and they would stand there and stare at the wonders of God’s creation. After a time, he would say “now we are small enough, its time to talk”. 

Which I love. I love learning about the things that God created because of how much it teaches us about God. In fact, philosophy started as a way of learning about God through pure thought. They would ask questions of the world around them and ponder things like what a little nest of ants teaches us about God. Ultimately that progressed into science. Which endeavored to do the same things, but involving more than just thinking. Lets factor in all the other senses as well. What does it mean that with the right equipment we can hear a pulsar making rhythemic sounds in a galaxy far enough away that we could never in our lifetime visit. Our focus on bugs and birds have inspired airplanes and cars that are faster, and more efficient than previous generations and yet still some mysteries exist. Based on what we know right now of physics, bumblebees shouldn’t be able to fly. 

All of that is general revelation, and it’s a beautiful thing. But it’s a thing that should never be divorced from scripture. So often when we hear people say they feel closer to God in their hunting blind what they mean is they just don’t want to go to church, and don’t read their bible. Because if they had read their bible they would know that it tells them to be involved In church, and to study God’s word. 

God’s word is called a special revelation. The things God put in it, are generally things that we coulnd’t figure out with science and philosophy. Some things like the old testament dietary laws helped us to be safe in a time before science. The next six verses (Read them 7-13) are about special revelation. Steven davey once said “creation is what God did, and the bible tells us why”. 

Special revelation and its study has benefits. It revives the soul and brings wisdom to the simple vs 7, vs 8 it makes our heart rejoice, and helps us to better see in all spheres of life. Ever been sad, weary? Depressed? Turn to the Lord, and study his word, and his precepts – his words – his love letter to us can bring us joy and make our hearts rejoice. 

Vs 9 the fear of the lord leads to clean living. Hah. Yesterday was the 281st anniversary of one of the most famous sermons ever written, and it was preached by americans smartest son – Jonathan Edwards who was also a very devout Christian. His influence in Christian thought can still be felt today. Any way, he wrote this:

The God that holds you over the pit of hell, much as one holds a spider or some loathsome insect over the fire, abhors you, and is dreadfully provoked. . . You are 10,000 times more abominable in his eyes than the most hateful venomous serpent is in ours.
And now you have an extraordinary opportunity, a day wherein Christ has thrown the door of mercy wide open and stands in calling and crying with a loud voice to poor sinners."

What mercy. What grace. What Love. That the Lord of all creation, who spoke it into existence in six days would choose to at great cost to himself throw wide the door of mercy for someone like you or I. 

Rejoice indeed. 

Vs 10 God’s word is more valuable than the finest Gold, and sweeter than Honey. I wonder if that’s what inspired the Oswald Chambers quote from earlier “If the average believer truly understood what would happen if he used the Bible more often, he would use it more often”. 

vs 11, they warn us away from danger, and we are rewarded for being obedient and consistent in our persuit of God through them. 

Vs 12, they reveal to us our errors. Tim Keller wrote if you’re God never disagrees with you, than you aren’t worshipping God, you’re worshiping an idealized version of yourself. Any God worth following is going to know better than we do about things. Follow him to your blessing, and ignore him to your peril. 

The passage ends with a verse Pastor Bob always used to quote when he would pray before preaching. “Let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be acceptable in your sight, O Lord, my rock and my redeemer.”. 

In times of trouble, where does our help come from? From God. Yes. And where can we go to read about that kind of help? The bible. Absolutely yes. 

in times of Joy, what keeps us from going off the tracks? God yes, and he frequently uses his word to help keep us going the right direction. 

In times of pain it’s a comfort to know that God deeply and fully knows what it is to see his child die in a terrible way at the hands of people he loves. He can relate. He’s been through it. He’ll get you through it too. 

God is so much better to us than we could ever hope to deserve.
