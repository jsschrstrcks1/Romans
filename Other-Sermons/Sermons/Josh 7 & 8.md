One Hebrew scholar points out that the phrase devoted things – from vs 13, carries with it a lot of meaning. You have heard of double entendre – when someone says one thing but means two? The attractive cloak, may well have been something used in pagan worship. Finally, the specific words used here in this grouping are frequently used to refer to sexual sins.

One quote I love from Baptist history is by a man named Hudson Taylor. Have you ever heard of Hudson Taylor? He was always one of my favorite missionaries. At 21 he felt God leading him to be a missionary to china. So he packed everything up, and left. His first few years there were met with difficulty and disappointment. The field was hard and unfertile, the few other missionaries present were in his words, worldly, and unengaged. Most received a stipend from their sending agency, but also worked as interpreters for business men. 

Hudson Taylor by contrast bought local clothing, dressed like the locals, had a haircut like the locals, and basically tried to be like the locals to the extent that he was able. He eschewed the company of foreign nationals – the English speaking business men his peers spent their time with in favor of actual Chinese people. 

He quickly decided to move inland. He bought up, and imported bibles, and took off for the Chinese inland. About that time, his missionary sending agency ran out of funds, and he decided that if God had him there, God could sustain him there. So he left the Methodists and became an independent missionary. 

That surely required a lot of faith. Many people would have returned home. But he redoubled his efforts. 

He wrote: Can all the Christians … sit still with folded arms while these multitudes [in China] are perishing—perishing for lack of knowledge—for lack of that knowledge which England possesses so richly?

He kept serving. Now married, with a child, the second opium war broke out, and trade was shut down with England. He found himself in pretty dire straits. The Chinese economy went from one of the largest in the world to one greatly diminished. Meanwhile illegal opium flooded in from the west – especially the UK, but we americans were not innocent. The ancestors of FDR, and also John Kerry made much of their family fortunes through state sponsored smuggling of drugs into the heart of china – where Hudson was. 

Now with 4 kids, and a burgeoning missions organization under his command he pressed on. He had as much or more western medical knowledge than did anyone else in the country at the time, his medical practice served at its peak 200 people each day – with only Hudson Taylor to doctor them. 

He began to pray for help. God sent 76 missionaries. He prayed for more help, and God sent 106 more missionaries. By 1900 his organization employed nearly half of all the missionaries in china. Each year more and more missionaries were brought into China even after Taylor had to retire due to his health. His life inspired many to travel to one of the more difficult missions locations in the world, so that they might share the gospel. As a result now, today, there are something like 200,000,000 believers in Jesus Christ in China. 

One of my favorite quotes of Taylor was that “God’s work, done God’s way will never lack God’s supply”. 

The Israelites found this to be true at AI as well, vs 27 of chapter 8 says “Only the livestock and the spoil of that city Israel took as their plunder, according to the word of the lord that he had commanded Joshua. So Joshua burned AI and made it forever a ruin, as it is to this day”. 

When they finally were doing God’s work, God’s way, the victory was complete and total.
