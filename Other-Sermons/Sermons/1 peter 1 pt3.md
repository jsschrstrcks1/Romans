Good morning, and welcome to Lakeview Fellowship Church, were going to continue to work our way through 1 peter if you’ve brought your bibles and I hope you have, if not there should be one in the pew back ahead of you. 

The excellent preacher John MacArthur broke this passage into three parts, and I think they are worth discussing. Not only does this passage talk about bearing up under persecution, but it also talks about our salvation. 

He's writing to people that have been scattered. Remember a few weeks ago our discussion about the elect exiles? 

What does it mean to be elect? Can anyone tell me?

RC Sproul writes: that it is a “privileged and secure position as the objects of God’s sovereign, gracious, eternal choice to be His people in and through Jesus Christ”

Election is an issue that some people get up tight about. It’s a deep and nuanced subject, and were likely not going to be able to cover it thoroughly today, but we will get our feet wet a bit. We’ve talked about it before, but in other terms. 

I like Sproul’s definition of elect better than the dictionary version, which talks about selecting a representative out of the midst of our people for leadership. 

Election is a thing mentioned throughout scripture. In the old testament its talked about scores of times. Any time the old testament talks about the nation of Israel being God’s chosen people, were talking about Election. Or God choosing for himself a people that he then makes priveledged and secure, as objects of God’s Sovereign, gracious, eternal choice to be his people in and through Jesus Christ. 

It’s a thing we are very comfortable with in the old testament. Its an easy Sunday school answer, who are god’s people in the old testament? 

Deutereonmy 7:6 is one of the most clear statements of old testament election: 6 For you are a people holy to the LORD your God. The LORD your God has chosen you out of all the peoples on the face of the earth to be his people, his treasured possession.

The nation of Israel is God’s holy people. His Treasured Posession. Holy by God’s declaration regarding those that followed the law. 

I think its important to point out an old testament precedent too. Did the nation of Israel choose God, or did God choose the nation of Israel?  Acts 13 points out that Israel was chosen not because Israel had chosen God, but because God had chosen Israel. In Deuteronomy 7  it points out that God’s faithfulness to them, and choice of them had to do with his promise to Abraham and not any virtue or value that Abrahams offspring brought to the table. God said he would do it, and even though Abraham was long dead, and many of his descendants – including leaders - were not people that followed God, God honored his promises to Abraham any way. Faith Life Fact Book says it like this: The word(elect) thus indicates God’s prerogative in deciding what shall happen, independently of human choice. 

We accept it so easily in the old testament and to use a horse expression chomp at the bit in the new. Horses that chomp on their bit are uncomfortable. Maybe its because they want the race to start, and they are anxiously excited. But sometimes its because they are misbehaving and have to have a harder bit in their mouth. Not all bits are created equal in terms of comfort. Some apply a little preassure, some apply a lot, Some grip the cheeks, or the roof of the mouth in ways that the horse won’t find comfortable, to encourage them to move more this way or that. Full cheek bits apply a bit of extra preassure to the cheek for a horse that stops well, but doesn’t want to turn obediently for instance. It can pinch their cheek between the bit and their teeth. Makes them want to turn quickly to reduce the pressure. One needs the right bit for the right horse and rider. Even if it sometimes makes the horse uncomfortable when its in training. That part is key. 

We so easily say God is King, and God is sovereign but we balk when we hear things like that means he can do kingly things. 

Back in the days of kings and knights. If the King said go do this thing or that, what right did the knight have to question that? His only real choice was obedience. So on some level in calling God king, we recognize he has the right to make decisions for us that we have to respect. 

In our country our president can’t just executive order us into service (outside of specific conditions, like being employed by the executive branch). So its not as familiar to us as it might be to someone who served in the armed forces. 

For them when the commander speaks, its an order. Some of those orders are going to involve going into places where things are difficult, and some of those orders are going to result in the death of good soldiers. And we accept and expect that from our society. That commanders have that right towards their people. 

And yet when we say the same thing about God People chafe. They fight the bit that is in our mouth. When a horse fights, how does that work out for the horse? Extra training in the round pen, extra time under the saddle, extra measures to ensure they learn what is expected from them. Are we not in the Lord’s Army? Does a general have to explain to the private why he wants this or that done? No, the private just needs to do it. 

I read a post by a marine the other day that said when he was in the marine corps his job was to keep the parking lot swept and dry. When it rained he was out there in the rain mopping up the rain as fast as it came down. When the wind blew leaves on the lot, he was out there sweeping them up as fast as the wind blew them in. He didn’t have to have his orders explained, he just had to follow them.  That’s the prerogative of a king. Of a commander, and of a sovereign God. 

Why makes things easier, and our God has given us quite a bit of his “why” here in his word. We just need to take time to read it. Reading it, as it happens, is also a thing we are commanded to do. 

The Baker encyclopedia of the bible says “The use of the word (elect) emphasizes that membership of God’s people is due to God’s initiative, prior to all human response, made before time began (Eph 1:4; cf. Jn 15:16, 19). It is God who has called men and women to be his people, and those who respond are elect. God’s call does not depend on any virtues or merits of humankind. Indeed, he chooses the foolish things by worldly standards to shame the wise, the weak to confound the strong, and the low and insignificant to bring to nothing those who think that they are something (1 Cor 1:27, 28). The effect of election is to leave no grounds whatever for human boasting in achievement and position. Whatever the elect are, they owe entirely to God, and they cannot boast or compare themselves with other people.”

So you cans see its all throughout the new testament too, not just in our passage today. But mentioned dozens and dozens of times in the old testament, and heaps of times in the new testament as well. 

We who are believers in Jesus Christ are elect of God. To be his treasure. 

With that in mind lets read our chapter, and pick out some of the promises God has left for us there. 

1 peter 1:1-end

The first one comes fairly early on. Right in verse two, Paul writes “in sanctification”. Who knows what sanctification is? It’s the amount of righteousness that’s required for salvation. Another dictionary puts it this way, it’s the holiness required to truly see God. We can progress beyond that initial dose. But it requires the help of God, and working on our spiritual disciplines – things like praying, reading the bible, worshipping, fellowship with other beleivers, and evangelism. 

The next one is in vs 3 – we have been caused to be born again. Our first birth was in sin. Thanks a lot adam. But this rebirth that comes as a result of being elect of God, is to salvation. One day we are going to be in the same state, but even better than what Adam was born into. He was born sinless, and brought sin into the world. We have been reborn and when we one day are in glory with Jesus, we too will be sinless, and held close by God so that we don’t sin again. So were going to be like adam, but even better. 

This is our Living hope still vs 3. In the words of the heheh great theologian Burt Reynolds, he said you can’t keep a good dog down. We know that none of us are good by nature, but because of Jesus, because of his sanctifying work, we have a place in eternity with God. We aren’t Good, but Jesus is, and his righteousness is put in our account. So while we didn’t deserve, it we don’t have to end up in hell. We can spend eternity with God in Glory. We know that Jesus died on that cross, but death couldn’t keep him, and he resurrected. He walked around the earth for a while again being seen by more than 500 recorded witnesses. Jesus is still alive! He is our hope. He keeps our hope alive.

Because of what Jesus did, we have access vs 4 to an inheritance. Imperishable, undefiled, unfading, stored in heaven where theif and rust and fire can’t touch it. Cant be swept away or damaged by a flood. Or even our own damage. If its ours, there is nothing we can do to damage it. People think they can lose or give back their salvation. But let me ask you this. Can you go to heaven to steal it? Can you some how corrode it away when God is protecting it? Can you set it on fire, somehow and lose it if God wants to preserve it. Imperishable undefiled, and unfading, can you mess that up? If you think you can it’s a way of saying you think you are stronger than God. So let me ask you this follow up question, do you think you’re stronger than God? 

Youd have to be to make perishable something God declares is imperishable, you’d have to be to take something that God has protected in heaven. Youd’ have to be to defile something God says is undefileable. 

the enxt one is in vs 4. We are being Guarded by the same all powerful God that sent his sone to die for us. If he wouldn’t spare his son, buy our salvation, what lengths do you think he’d go to, to preserve what was purchased at such a great cost to himself? 

I mean, you know how people are when they buy a new vehicle, most of them are absolutely paranoid they will get a scratch. I knew a guy who drove on old truck, but it was precious to him, and he would park in the parkingspot furthest away from everything and walk a half mile to avoid the possibility that someone would ding his precious truck. Same guy, I covered his car top to bottom in bumper stickers one time, he was /not/ impressed. But the same way, God has spent the blood of his son to buy our freedom from sin. Is he not going to go the extra mile to protect his investment? To protect us. To guard us as his elect?

This is something we can rejoice in, vs 6, The God who keeps his promises, is right there with us, protecting and guarding us. Preserving us for salvation. So we can spend an eternity with him in Glory. 

That should give us cheer, when we go through difficult times. 

Lets pause right there. There is one more thing I want to share, and then we will close. 
 
This is from a pastor that I just love called Paul Washer. He serves In a difficult context, but he words things so well. 

He brought up the story of Esau and Jacob. The bible says “Jacob I loved but esau I hated”. 

How did God’s hatred look in Esau’s life? He got pretty much everything he wanted, he lived the quote good life unquote. Wealth, and prestige, all of it was his. His life was easy. He received every blessing from God due him. 

Jacob on the other hand, the bible says God loved. Jacob was a bit of a liar you may recall. Its almost like God said “Jacob I love you, but we got some work to do”. Washer says Jacob was beat up and beat down pretty much every day of his life, and he wrestled and fought with God until the day he left this earth and entered Glory he did so with a limp. God loved him too much to spare him from hard times. God loved him too much to allow him to go through life like Esau did. God loved him, and so God in his sovereignty sent him difficulty, His brother tried to kill him. He worked for 7 long years for permission to marry the woman he loved, and yet in deception, his father in law made him marry the other sister. We don’t know why she wasn’t desireable, but she should have been able to find a husband in the 7 years it took Jacob to earn Labans blessing to marry Rachel. She was the older sister. Who knows, maybe she had a personality that made a battleaxe seem kind. We just don’t know other than that Jacob, and apparently the other men of the town, didn’t want her. So he worked 7 more years, and earned the right to marry Rachel, finally. Then on his way home six years later, he wrestled with God himself, and his hip was broken by God. 

Bear in mind, all this time, Jacob had not one, but two wives. They say a wife chips the rough edges off a man, I wonder what two wives would do? Two women to keep happy. Two women that had needs he needed to meet. 

13 children later, Jacob comes to believe his favorite son dies in an act of deceipt by his other children. Instead, in truth, he’d just been sold to the slavers in Egypt. 

Then a terrible famine comes across the land. One that threatens to wipe out even his great fortune. He hears rumors of grain in Egypt, so he sends sons to get grain, and  His second son is held prisoner in Egypt accused of stealing. 

And more. God crushed – and if you’ve ever lost a child, you know this is true, God crushed Jacob, to turn him into the father of a great nation. Jacob he loved, but Esau he hated. 

Sometimes we are going to be sent into places that are hard. Sometimes we might be ordered into things that will take our lives. But our lives were bought at a high price by the blood of Jesus. We are treasured possessions of the king. In wasting us, he would be wasting the blood of his son. However he chooses to spend our lives will be well considered and vitally necessary. The hardships we are allowed to go through are out of love for us. A love that loves us too much to leave us as we are. Some of us need a hammer and chisel, some of us need a sledge hammer, and dynamite. But whatever he needs to shape us into what he needs us to be, he will use. Sometimes that’s persecution by the government. Sometimes its obstinate children. Sometimes its health struggles; and we have had our share of those this week going around. Kathy in the hospital, Marie, Dalia and her perpetual struggles. Carol and her health struggles. 

Sometimes its our house being ruined in a storm. 

It is God’s prerogative as sovereign, and in part due to his election of us, to decree that we will go through hard times. Going through hard times means he’s still working on me. Means he still loves us. Means he loves us too much to leave us in the sinful mess we were in when he bought us.
