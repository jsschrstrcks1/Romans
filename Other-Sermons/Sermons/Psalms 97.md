This is whats in view in the opening verses of our passage today. Lets read. Psalms 97

You catch those first couple of verses? Clouds and thick Darkness, fire goes before him vs consuming God’s adversaries. Clearly a reference to the story of Moses. It’s the author of the psalm reminding the audience of all the wonderful things God has done, not the least of which was tolerating a people that didn’t deserve it, and patience beyond measure. 

In verse four it talks about lightning. Lightning is a common component when the judgement of God is in view. Not always. But frequently. It certainly represents the power of God too. 

I mean, even in todays day and age, we have a healthy fear of lightning. There are people that can’t even go outside because lightning terrifies them. Even with all of our knowledge about lightning, how to avoid it, and all of our technology that is designed to keep us safe from lightning, its still a scary phenomenon for many. 

Now imagine back a few thousand years ago, before lightning rods, and car tires, and grounding youre house to protect against lightning, back when people lived in a tent in a field. When there were times and places where people were the tallest thing between the lightning and the ground, there would have been chaos, an death every time a thunderstorm comes through. 

As scary as it can be today, it would have been more so back then. My car was struck by lightning once. A tree near my house another time. It sounded and felt like a freight train had smashed into the ground. Everything shook and trembled. My windows rattled. When its close like that it triggers fight or flight. 

Lightning is a scary symbol of the power of God. Of the judgement of God. The judgement of God should make us tremble. 

If you make me mad, what am I going to do? Yell at you? If its severe enough, maybe it would mean the police get involved, and maybe jail time? If someone were deranged enough, torture, or murder are in view. But the worst man can do to man causes effects that last for a lifetime. 

But what is life in the scope of eternity? God’s righteous judgement determines our eternity. Not just today, or a week, or a month, or a life time. But a billion times a billion years, and youll just be finishing the first sentence of the novel of eternity. 

Don’t fear what man can do to man, fear what God can do. 

Vs 5 says the mountains melt like wax. Back in April I got to stand in the caldera of an inactive volcano. We drove through it for a solid ten minutes. Picture that size of that. How far can you go in a car in ten minutes? We were probably going 20 miles an hour. The caldera was huge. A caldera as you know is when a volcano erupts, and then the top of it kind of collapses back into it self. So it creates a giant depression at the top of the mountain. 

This one was miles across. Can you imagine the kind of volcano that would have left a caldera miles across? I bet the proverbial foundations of the world shook when that thing erupted. This one wasn’t unique either, there were dozens on the same island. Looked like someone had taken an ice cream scoop and scooped up the top of the mountains.

Yet they were simple enough to our God that he could speak a billion of them into existence with a word. That he considers them to be his footstool. 

I love verse 7, it’s a dig at fake worthless idols. Back in old testament times, there were these wooden things they worshiped. Like baal or his consort ashteroth. They believed that worshipping these sticks would bring fertility to the land, and so prosperity to the people. I won’t gross you out with the details, but needless to say the whole mess was inappropriate and adult content. Maybe it’s a reminder of Elijah and the prophets of Ba’al. 

Remember that story? At the start of Elijah’s ministry, he threw open the doors to the throne room of King Ahab, declared God’s judgement against Ahab, and that there would be a drought starting then, and continuing until God said otherwise. He then took off and spent a few years by the Brooke Cherith. 

Needless to say this really made ahab happy with him. You ever notice howthe villians of the storys seldom ever take personal responsibility for what they’ve done? They try and kill the messenger, or act like its somehow elijahs fault for God punishing Ahab, for Ahab’s sins. 

But any way, things build and progress and kind of culminate in a cosmic showdown. Elijah says if Ba’al is real, lets give him a chance to show himself, make an alter, and if he’s real, any God worth his salt can make fire. So make an alter, put a bull on it, and then pray and ask your stick to send fire down. Then I’ll do the same, and whomever sends fire down, that’s the real God. 

Elijah lets the stick follower, also known as the followers of Baal go first, and they did all sorts of crazy stuff, and nothing happened. 

So Elijah taunted him, and it says this in the bible, “maybe he’s busy, or traveling, or using the bathroom, or thinking deeply, go ahead and try again”. 
Ultimately nothing happened, because Baal is a worthless stick, and his followers are foolish as our passage says. But what happened when Elijah called on God? God sent fire down, and the bull, the alter, and all the water was consumed. Even the rocks. You might say they melted like wax. 

At this point the passage changes tone, and the nation of Israel – ie the daughters of Judah, and zion rejoice. Because God is sovereign and in control. Because nothing escapes his notice. 

They praise him for His judgement. You know, that includes judgement on the author? Ever considered thanking God for judging you?
