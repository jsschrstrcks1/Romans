I was listening to music and heard a song that I think goes along with what John was talking about in our passage today. I want to read through some of the lyrics. Never mind the authors, or the meter, or any of that, just listen to the words as I read them. 

This first one says of God that he “Loves like a hurricane, and I am a tree”. After the craziness and violence of this hurricane season, it brought tears to my eyes thinking about the Love of God in those terms. What tree can stand up to the weight of the presence and love of God? 

I think many times we think of God as being safe. A Sunday school rhyme says “see jesus meek and mild, see him as a little child”. We want God to be safe, and put our priorities first.

But God isn’t safe. His love isn’t safe. His love is a hurricane, and I am a tree. Blown this way and that by the weight of his wind and glory. 

We want a tame, calm housecat of a god, and what we get instead is a roaring lion, who in love enforces order. In love rips leaves from our branches in his calm affections, and in the midst of his torrential love cleaves trunk from limb, and uproots entire trees. He tells us he will prune us. He tells us we will be grafted. He tells us he will refine us like Gold in a fire. 

Yet still we are surprised when God’s blessing is a hurricane that takes our home. Our loved one. Our friend. 

Cs Lewis wrote as part of a metaphor he had written about God, That (God) isn’t safe, but he is good. 

So many people follow God hoping for ONLY sonshine and rainbows. Only peace, and warm feelings, and joy. Only cotton candy, and marshmallow fluff. We trust our sinful heart to point towards whats best, but what our sinful heart wants is the exact opposite of what we need. Were like addicts after our next fix. In our natural state we want anything and everything except that which we need the most. This is a hard teaching. 

No one wants to hear that they are going to face hard times. No one gets excited about trials. No one wants to have their faith tested. No one likes to think about such things as loving. 

But its part of love. Its part of your love for your children. Our love for our children is a dim relfection of God’s love for us. God’s love for us is perfect, as it comes from our perfect God, our love is corrupted and tainted by sinful hearts. But think about it,  Its in love that we punish our children. We don’t enjoy punishing them. But we know its for their good that they are punished when they do wrong things. It helps them grow, and mature, and improve. It helps them be productive and ultimately makes their life happier, and healthier by allowing them to experience some austerity, or a smack on the bottom. Punishment should come from a place of love from us to our children, and it does come from a place of love from God towards us. For the Lord disciplines the one he loves, and chastises every son whom he receives. Hebrews 12 says. 

God’s love is more than just punishment too. But lets stop and read our passage. 1 john 4:7-21

I mention the hard parts of God’s love first for a reason, I don’t think people think about it. We think of romantic love that we have towards our wife or ladies for their husband. That warm and gooey place you end up at when you’re first dating? 

I think of a scene in the Disney movie aladin where Aladin is going on about how beautiful he thinks the princess is. I think our culture thinks of love in those terms. It’s a fair weather kind of cotton candy like love, that is sweet and lacking substance. Substance is built slowly and over time. It takes a while for that deep and real love to grow between husband and wife. 

I think we need to think more about the harder aspects of God’s love than we do. People often say, and its basically a cliché at this point that “God loves us no matter what, and he loves us too much to leave us that way”. 

God in his love has to cut things off from us sometimes. 

Like any good doctor. If you went to your doctor this week, and he announced to you that you had a tumor, and its cancerous, but he can cut it out if you want. Who wouldn’t say yeah absolutely cut it out. I don’t want cancer growing inside me. 

our minds are so tainted by sin that we cant even fully comprehend the things we need to have cut off and out of our lives. 

I heard an illustration one time about a compass. A certain ship was leaving London headed for new York, and their compass was off by one half degree. You know where they would end up? Hundreds of miles off course, in that wild and crazy untamed land called miami. Haha. 

But the compass of our heart is more than ½ degree off course, it points the wrong direction, because our vision is clouded by sin. 

God has to cut away at that from our hearts. He has to remove the tumor. He has to degause the compass, he has to spank, and ground, and chastise us where we need it. 

So that we can grow in our relationship with him. 

So that our love for him is more than just cotton candy and fluff. SO that its based on deeper things. 

We know we can love him because he was there for us when this or that happened. Because he saw us through that hard time where we couldn’t see a resolution, and yet he miraculously provided beyond what we could have ever imagined even in our wildest dreams. 

It reminds me of Abraham and issac back in genesis 22. 

God told Abraham to sacrifice his son. His only son. His son whom he loved. 

What we don’t see in that passage is Abraham saying “uhhhmmm but God, I can’t see how that’s going to work out, you promised me descendents like the stars in the sky, and yet all I have is that one son, and we pretty fond of him, are you sure that’s what you want?”. We don’t see anything like that, instead what we see is Abraham goes and packs up what he needs for the sacrifice, and tells his son its time to go. I don’t even think he tells his wife what God’s asked him to do. He just responds immediately in obedience, and out of love for God. Heads up that mountain with his son, some firewood, and a knife. 

We hear about Abrahams faith in this a lot, but how about the faith of Isaac? We don’t hear about Abraham having to wrestly him down, or tie him up. He just goes up there willingly. Like a lamb to the slaughter. Because he loves and trusts God, and he loves and trusts his father. 

And at the 11th hour, God provides a ram in the thicket. The british museum has a gold and silver wireframe ram trapped in the thicket on display that came from the right part of the world, and dates back to when we think Abraham likely lived. 

Oh how he loved us indeed. 

He loves us with a love that didn’t spare his own son from the cross, so that we could spend eternity with Him. 

He loves us too much to leave us where we are. 

He loves us like a hurricane, and like any tree going through any strong cat 5 hurricane, its impossible to encounter that kind of love and leave unchanged. 

He is relentless in his pursuit of us. An unyielding unstoppable force chipping away at our hard hearts of stone. Molding us, and shaping us into something beautiful, and usefull for his service. 

God is so good to us. But if your primary concern is safety, he isn’t safe. He’s safer than the alternative. But God isn’t safe. He might cut that idol you love away from your heart. I knew someone that loved fishing so much, he’d skip church to fish. One day he was getting ready to skip church and go fishing, and out of a blue sky his boat which was already full of gas was struck by lightning, igniting the gas. Guess who got the message, and went to church that Sunday? 

It would be nice if God always acted swiftly, and made it apparent exactly what his will was in the moment. I’d love for God to appear and say, no no, go left this time, instead of right. But often he is more subtle than a lightning strike to a boat. 

Because in the stillness and quiet, in the unsurety and heartache and struggle he strengthens our faith. He expands our patience. 

I hear people say all the time to not pray for patience. But we should still pray for patience, just do so knowing that this means God would give us opportunities to have our patience tested and strengthened. EG that’s the day the people that require extra grace will come out of the wood work, and you’ll be dealing with them all day. But if it makes us better reflect christ, if it draws us closer to christ. If it gives us opportunities for evangelism, than isn’t that better than staying the way we are? 

Love is patient, love is kind. It does not envy, it does not boast, it is not proud. 5 It does not dishonor others, it is not self-seeking, it is not easily angered,it keeps no record of wrongs. 6 Love does not delight in evil but rejoices with the truth. 7 It always protects, always trusts, always hopes, always perseveres.
8 Love never fails.
Wouldn’t then, that be part of abiding in the love of God? 

We shouldn’t try to hold back in loving God. God is after all of us. Not just part. He means to bring all of every part of each of us elect, into alignment with his son. We will be chisseled away at like michaelangelo chizeled away at the raw lump of marble that became David, or how the Egyptians chizeled away at the stone that became the sphynx in Egypt. 

Its not a comfortable process, but it’s a good process. It’s a worthwhile process. 

Remember who you’re doing it for. After all, didn’t we just read that: this is love, not that we have loved God but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the propitiation for our sins.

The fullness and consummation of God’s love for us, is on full display in the death of Christ. God sent what was most precious to him, that we might be redeemed. 

What God cuts away from us with his chisel may hurt, but they are far less precious to us than God’s son was to him. 

He has lead by example, sacrificing Jesus for us. If he then asks us to give up this or that, or to lose your house for a time. Or to endure a year of the leg. We know he has a reason for it, we know his purposes are for our good. We know whatever hes asked of us is less than he asked of his son. We know whatever he asks of us, is easier than an eternity in hell. 

We know he loves us too much to leave us the way we are. 

And all of that begins with the gospel.
