Jeremiah 17:1-18

I had a guy comment that he wished more preachers would preach on politics, really preach on the subject. 

I’ve not been silent on the subject, but I think its important. I’m going to go out on a limb and say the average Christian thinks that politics, and this candidate or that will save our country. If we don’t get this candidate or that then our country will be destroyed. 

Sound familiar? I was reading the story of the life of a missionary named Boniface this week. He served in Germany where at this point in time they were worshipping trees. Perhaps you’ve heard of the tree ygdrasil, or the world tree from norse mythology. 

either way, Boniface was sharing the gospel with germans, and they were receptive, people were becoming Christians, and giving up their pagan ways. But things started to get hard, and some of those germans started going back to the old ways, and worshipping this one particular big tree Which was essentially a great big idol. We know from Bonifaces correspondence that it took a while to figure out what was going on, and when he figured it out, it took some time to figure out what to do. But ultimately the Lord led him to chop down that huge old tree. Boniface writes that it took hours. 

Some of the trees that have survived the centuries are absolutely massive so its no wonder. We don’t really know how old the tree was, nor how big. But we know it took him hours. One tree in Europe that has existed more than 2000 years is sixteen feet in diameter. The circumference of that tree would be about 50 feet. Can you imagine? A tree that grew towards the sky back when Jesus walked the earth. Makes me wonder if he ever saw it. Regardless, we don’t know how big Boniface’s tree was, but it was likely huge. He didn’t have a chainsaw way back in the 8th century, so he picked up his axe and swung and swung and swung. For hours before the great old tree cracked and fell, and made the earth shudder. 

From then on, the people Boniface served had decided that the God of the Bible was the one they should follow. Everyone in the community forsook their tree worship, and worshipped the one true God instead. 

While its sad to lose an old tree, smashing idols is in keeping with a long line of Christians going back clear to the old testament, and included Jesus. Who chased men with a whip that were worshipping money at the temple, instead of God. He over turned their tables, and from the sounds of it, free’d their animals as well. Likewise the protestant reformers were known for their smashing of things that had taken on an innapropriate role in the church. If the created thing was being worshiped instead of God, they smashed them unapologetically. Destroying history with abandon for the purpose of destroying the idols in their midst. Like Boniface would one day do with that big old tree he chopped down. 

As I said before our country worships the idol of politics. Listen, I love our country. I always vote. I’ve voted in every election I was old enough to vote in. Hah. You know when you bring a kid to a voting place, they used to have a way for kids to vote in an inconsequential way. Not live ballots, choosing between George W Bush and George Washington, they let kids vote. Being a little one once, they gave me the chance to do the same, and made the mistake of using a live ballot. I’m certain it wasn’t counted, but it still had to go in the box after being X’d out. But regardless, I have voted in every election I’ve been eligible to vote in, and one that I was’t. I tried to join the army, and even went as far as taking the asvab in an effort to serve our country. There was a time where I ate breathed and slept politics. My opinion regarding how the day was going was largely related to how I felt like political things were going that day. If I was watching something on tv, it was politics, if I was listening to something on the radio, it was politics. If you struck up a conversation with me, I was talking about politics. Politics on the way to work, while working, on lunch break, after lunch, on my way home, and then when I got home, guess what was next? More politics. I think its safe to say my priority in life at that point was politics. 

I’d even argue that for a period of about a year, in hindsight, I was unintentionally worshipping politics. 

I think it’s a true statement that you can tell someones priorities from their check book, and likewise their day planner. If something takes up more time than you spend in prayer, or reading the bible, or worshipping God. Then it might just be an idol for you. Politics was for me – just being really real here, was an idol. 

Lets go to our passage this morning, Jeremiah 17. 

If I preached politics regularly, It would be easy to fill the building. People want to hear your politics, and more than that they want to hear that your politics and theirs are in agreement. 

But this isn’t that kind of message. If you’ve spent more time speaking about Joe Biden, than you have about your savior, then you might have an idol. If you’ve spent more time talking about Donald Trump than you have Jesus Christ, than you might have an idol. 

if you spend more time talking trash about Joe Biden than you are about Jesus Christ than you might have an idol, and are being disrespectful (whether we like biden or not) to the leader God says in his word he has established for us. God says in his word that to be disrespectful of his man is to be disrespectful of God. Full stop. So that means when Donald Trump was president, if you were speaking badly about him you were speaking badly of God’s appointed leader for that period of time, and thus speaking poorly of God. 

But that also means that since Joe Biden is president, if we speak poorly of him, than we are being disobedient to God also. Just like when Trump was president. 

I first read these verses in romans 13 back when a man I strongly vehemently disagreed with was president. I was dead certain that this man was going to ruin the country, and maybe the world. That there would be no America left for my maybe future children to inherit if he won a second term. Sound familiar? At that point in time I was guilty of political idolatry. But just like that tree that Boniface cut down, God chopped that idol out of my hard one swing of the axe at a time. 

The first time I heard this I was angry. Everyone knew that God was the same political party that I was. I certainly believed and still believe that one party reflects the ideals of scripture better. I’d certainly rather a follower of Jesus making decisions for the country, than someone whose priorities were contradictory to scripture. Obviously God wants a leader, I reasoned that agreed with me on politics. But God raises up leaders for our blessing, and also our punishment. God gives us the leader we need, not necessarily the leader we want. 

Part of what makes this idol so hard to deal with, is that it is based and rooted in the idea that we are all inherently capable of rising to any occasion that we might encounter. The idea of pulling ourselves up by our bootstraps. American independence and exceptionalism is a concept that is deeply engrained in us. 

In most domains its not an inaccurate description. We come across a problem, we gather people together, figure it out, and build something incredible. Like the hoover dam. Provides power for much of the electrical needs of three states. Like aircraft carriers. I saw a facebook reel that was kinda funny, but the point of it was that one single aircraft carrier can topple most nations. We have 11 of them, and 18 more in construction. In virginia alone they can construct two at the same time. Two bohemoths of warfare that possess in themselves the capability to topple nations. We see a problem, we figure it out, and we solve it. 

This has trained us to rely on ourselves. To look inside ourselves for solutions to our problems. They call that an internal locus of control. Or that we believe that within ourselves is the ability to answer and solve all of our problems. But what does the bible say?

Thus says the Lord:
“Cursed is the man who trusts in man
    and makes flesh his strength,[a]
    whose heart turns away from the Lord.
6 He is like a shrub in the desert,
    and shall not see any good come.
He shall dwell in the parched places of the wilderness,
    in an uninhabited salt land.

So let me ask you this, in light of that passage, is our hope for the future to be found in our local mayor? Oh no? Well what about our county commissioners? They are pretty powerful. What about the state government? Is our hope in our governor? Our state house of representatives, our state senate? Or are they just men too. 

What about at the federal level. Surely our hope is found in this senator or that senator. This member of congress or that member of congress? Wait you’re telling me our hope isn’t in these guys? 

Well surely then our hope is found in the president? Oh he’s just a man also? 

I’m reminded of the old hymn based on the story of the two men, one who build their home on the shore, and one who build their home on the rock. 

The hymn goes like this “My hope is built on nothing less, than Jesus’ blood and righteousness. I dare not trust the sweetest frame, but wholly trust in Jesus’ Name. On Christ the Solid Rock I stand all other ground is sinking sand”. 

I don’t know about you guys, but I didn’t see politics in there at all, did you? 

Lets look at the bible passage that song is based upon. Turn in your bibles to Mathew chapter 7:24. 

24 “Everyone then who hears these words of mine and does them will be like a wise man who built his house on the rock. 25 And the rain fell, and the floods came, and the winds blew and beat on that house, but it did not fall, because it had been founded on the rock. 26 And everyone who hears these words of mine and does not do them will be like a foolish man who built his house on the sand. 27 And the rain fell, and the floods came, and the winds blew and beat against that house, and it fell, and great was the fall of it.”

In light of that passage, I want to ask you a question. 

Is Joe Biden Omnipotent? Omnipotent means all powerful. God is omnipotent, science still isn’t fully sure how molecules hold together, but the bible says God knows, and created that mechanism. We may think we can solve any problem we encounter but God actually can. God spoke the universe into existence in six days. Can Joe Biden do that? God lead the Israelites as a piller of fire and cloud. Has anyone seen Joe Biden do that? Jesus – who is part of the Triune God commanded the wind and the waves to cease, and they stopped. Can Joe Biden do that?

What about Trump. Did Donald Trump place the stars in the sky, does he know each and everyone by its name? God Does. Can Donald trump tell every lightning bolt where it should go? Surely then Donald trump imagined the sun and gave source to its light? Oh no? Oh right. That was God also. Not man.

Politics is a necessary thing. Please vote. Always vote. 

But at the end of the day our hope is not the vote and whether or not it was fair. Our hope is not in any man or institution of man. Our hope is only in Christ alone. 

Everything else is a worthless idol. Everything else can be killed, ruined or destroyed. Institutions of man can be burned to the ground by the capricious tides of opinion. Only Christ, only God is undefeated, and undefeatable.

Our savior is indescribably beautiful beyond measure. He alone knows the deepest closets of our heart and chooses to love us, and chose to die for us any way. 

Christians, I never again want to hear that our hope is in any politician or political party. Republican and Democrat are pretty similar to the modern day baals. 

The people of old believed that Baal would bring spring. That baal would protect their way of life, and ensure their prosperity. 

People today believe that their favorite politician or party will do the same thing. Bring in a fresh time of spring, and growth, and plenty. Ensuring our prosperity, and the prosperity of those who come after us. Ensuring our way of life. 

But at the end of the day, no human is capable of doing that. 

Only God. Only God holds the heart of the king in his hands like a river of water directing it this way and that proverbs 21 says. Only God raises up leaders of any kind Romans 13. Only God. God is even sovereign over our vote. You want to talk about election interference? Talk to the one who is sovereign over the vote. God ultimately raises up leaders for our blessing and punishment. Still vote. But know the outcome is in Gods hands, and not ours. 

Still vote, but take a deep breath. Be still, scripture says, and know that I am God. God is still doing God things even today. And we know that God is good. Don’t we. We know that God is sovereign and in control. We know that we can trust God to do what is best, even if it doesn’t make sense to us at the time. 

There was a young man who was a dear friend of mine back when I was also young. His grandfather had Pancreatic cancer. My friend didn’t know God. But his grandfather did. His grandfather was the only parent he really knew. Every day, he would wake up and pray “God if you’re real please heal my grandfather”. Every time he’d eat, he’d pray the same “God if you’re real, please heal my grandfather”. Every night before bed often with tears running down his face, he would plead with God to heal his grandfather. 

This went on for about two years, by which point my friend had graduated from college, which in the UK is like graduating highschool here, and with a trade. He was trained to be a plumber specifically for gas lines which run all over the place over there. But any way, shortly after his graduation, his grandfather succumb to the cancer and passed away. 

What you and I know, is that to be absent from the body is to be present with the Lord either in Judgement, or in paradise. Either he’s going to say to us welcome home my good and faithful servant, or depart from me I knew you not. My friends grandfather was a believer in Jesus Christ, so we know that when he took that last breath he would be standing face to face with his savior, and being welcome into eternity in heaven with him. An eternity free of cancer. My friend never really came to a point where he understood that God had answered all of those desperate prayers, and that he could be reunited with his grandfather one day in glory. His grandfathers hopes were built on Nothing less than Jesus. My friends hopes were solidly rooted in this world. He couldn’t see or imagine anything further than this life, and so when his grandfather passed he believed all was lost. 

But we know that this life is just the brief foreword to a book whose pages will be filled for the rest of eternity. 

Where is your hope. 

Our passage todays says 

“Blessed is the man who trusts in the Lord,
    whose trust is the Lord.
8 He is like a tree planted by water,
    that sends out its roots by the stream,
and does not fear when heat comes,
    for its leaves remain green,
and is not anxious in the year of drought,
    for it does not cease to bear fruit.”

His roots are deeper than merely this life. He gets to live for the next one. 

He understands droughts will come, and plenty will come, and droughts will go and plenty will God, but that the word of the Lord lasts for ever. The Sovereignty of God lasts for ever, and he can rest in that warm blanket in the cold night of desert winter. 

But all of that starts with the gospel.
