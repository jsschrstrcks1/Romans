CORRECTIONS LOG (added by Claude Code)
Verified against ESV and primary sources. Do not preach or republish without resolving
flagged items below.

1. JIM ELLIOT QUOTE — MISQUOTED (HIGH PRIORITY)
   Sermon: "he is no fool who gives up what he cannot keep to gain what he cannot earn"
   Actual (Jim Elliot journal, October 28, 1949):
     "He is no fool who gives what he cannot keep to gain that he cannot lose."
   Two errors:
   - "gives up" → "gives"
   - "cannot earn" → "cannot lose"
   "Cannot lose" is theologically significant — it is the point of the sentence. Heaven is not
   something earned (which contradicts the very gospel this sermon preaches); it is something
   that, once received, cannot be lost. Correct before republishing.

2. ISAIAH 57:15 — "BROKEN" IS NOT THE ESV WORD (HIGH PRIORITY)
   Sermon: "to revive the spirit of the broken, and to revive the heart of the contrite"
   ESV Isaiah 57:15: "to revive the spirit of the lowly, and to revive the heart of the contrite"
   The word "broken" does not appear in this verse. ESV says "lowly." While "broken" fits the
   sermon's theme, it must not be presented as a quotation. Either use the ESV word "lowly" or
   paraphrase without quotation marks.

3. DAN MASTRAPA QUOTES — ATTRIBUTION UNVERIFIED (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
   Two quotes are attributed to "Dan Mastrapa - a pastor":
   - "When we say that God is sovereign, we are declaring that He has absolute authority..."
   - "God's sovereignty is the pillow on which we rest our weary heads..."
   These quotes could not be verified against a published source. Before reprinting in a bulletin
   or manuscript, confirm the source (book title, article, sermon date) so the attribution can
   be checked. Do not repeat unverified attributed quotes in print.

4. EPHESIANS 2:4 — OMITS OPENING "BUT" (LOW PRIORITY)
   Sermon begins the quotation: "God, being rich in mercy..."
   ESV Ephesians 2:4 begins: "But God, being rich in mercy..."
   The omission of "But" is minor in oral delivery but should be noted for any printed version.
   The "But God" contrast (dead in sin / but God intervenes) is theologically significant to the
   verse's structure.

5. JOHN 6:44 — "THEM" VS. "HIM"; VERSE TRUNCATED (LOW PRIORITY)
   Sermon: "No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws them"
   ESV John 6:44: "No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws him.
   And I will raise him up on the last day."
   - "them" is gender-inclusive; ESV uses "him"
   - The second half of the verse ("And I will raise him up on the last day") is omitted
   Minor in oral delivery; correct for any printed or published form.
