If you've brought your bibles, and I hope you have, open with me to John 4:7-26 John is in your new testament, which is located kind of towards the end of the bible. Mathew, mark, luke, then John. If you get to the books of acts or romans or Corinthians you've gone too far.

While you're turning there, lets talk about our people group of the week.



I think its important to set the stage a little bit for our passage this morning. Jesus and the disciples were traveling through samaria. That was a no-no back then. Samaritans were second class human beings. They were looked down upon, scorned, and outcast from society. The Jews didn't like them. The Jews at this point in history didn't want them around. Didn't want to visit them, didn't want to pass through their town, didn't want them passing through their own towns. Even though some of them worshiped God, they weren't allowed in the temple.

So in talking to the woman at the well, he was doing a number of nono's all at once. He had entered samaria, stopped in samaria, sent his disciples out for Samarian food, then while his disciples were doing that, he spoke to a Samaritan, a woman. Then horror of horrors he asked for Samaritan water.

I mean, imagine a trump walking into DNC headquarters, or Biden walking into RNC headquarters and saying "woman bring me a drink". This was some bold stuff. Jesus is crossing a lot of barriers to have this conversation. Except in the case of our political system, there is strong dislike, but I like to think there is no hate. Whereas here at this point in time, there is hate between the Jews and Samaritans. Imagine ladies, someone symbolic of the oppression of your people walking up to you and saying "give me a drink". I mean, that sounds like a good way to have someone spit in your drink when you're not looking or worse. The Samaritan woman seems hesitant to give him a drink. It strikes me that at this point In time women were quite low in stature in the city. They were little more than slaves according to the preacher Tim Keller. Any man could pretty much go make demands on the woman, and have a reasonable expectation that she would follow through. These were tough women to be able to survive and thrive in this time period. Any way, you'll notice in vs 8 that the disciples were off securing food, likely for dinner, so this was probably mid day, or mid afternoon. Commentators say that all of the reputable women would go out first thing in the morning. Its only the disreputable ones that got their water at this time of the day, so in all likelihood, she was even lower than the average woman of samaria. She had a lot of disadvantages, she was a member of a hateful, and hated people group, she was marginalized because of her gender, and even then she was lower than most women – an outcast. Maybe it was because of her five husbands vs 18. As far as we know, women couldn't divorce men back then, and its unlikely that 5 men would have divorced her but still possible. Its also possible that she was the widow of 5 husbands. How heart breaking. She was most likely a woman – profoundly touched by tragedy. Then here comes Jesus, give me water.

But its deeper than that, In Samaritan tradition, if a man and woman meet at a well like that, it was generally a thing that led to marriage. This well isn't just any well either, earlier in chapter 4, its mentioned that this is the well where the Jacob met Rachel! Jacob met Rachel at mid day, and now jesus meets this Samaritan woman at mid day also. She maybe thought Jesus was trying to hit on her. He asked her to draw some water, like Jacob was asking when he met Rachel. Its probably why she says what she says – I think shes trying to deflect the conversation. She's not really interested in giving Jesus water, I suspect after losing 5 husbands, and considering shes with a sixth that shes not married to, shes not interested in Jesus in that way. Yet it still seems like shes trying to fill the void in hear heart with the companionship of another human being. It's a common pitfall. Whether she compounded that with sexual sins or not, we don't know. Just simply that she had experienced a lot of loss when it came to her husband, and was with another man. I think the world does this a lot. The band Audio Adrenaline, which was one of my favorites as a teenager had a song called "God shaped hole" that fleshes this idea out a little more. It says: All around the world people are trying. Deserted souls cry to be filled. Spirits left undone their hearts are hollow. I can see, in their eyes, hollow lives. You may have tried to fill that hole inside with an empty hand you reached in vain. Substitutes don't work, bandages only hurt. The only thing that heals is the hand of God. Nobody's meant to be left all alone. Our lives are much too short to have an empty soul. And every heart, and every soul and everybody's got a God-shaped hole.

Or to quote Augustine of Hippo – who you may not be familiar with, but he's the man the city, and fort of St. Augustine were named after, wrote a book that remains in print called confessions in 400ad wrote "You have made us for yourself, O Lord, and our heart is restless until it rests in you.".

Or as C.S. Lewis wrote "If I find in myself a desire which no experience in this world can satisfy, the most probable explanation is that I was made for another world.".

Made for another world indeed. I could keep going – the idea that inside of every human being resides a tangible sense of something missing is well documented inside and outside of scripture. Inside and outside the church.

In fact I was reading an article on this topic in the huffington post, an online news outlet that I'm not over fond of, but I think they represented the worlds view of this void fairly well. It said basically that the only people with voids are those who are hurting, or who were mistreated. It calls that emptiness holes in your heart and soul. It goes on to say "The truth is that anytime you try to distract yourself from feeling what you're feeling, you're avoiding the fact that you're not whole. Something is missing, damaged or broken, and until you face it, no person or thing will ever make you feel complete."

It concludes with the admonition that you are the only one who can make yourself hole once more. Lets think about that argument for a moment. Lets apply it to medicine. You wake up one morning, and your side is in pain. You press gently, and the pain is excruciating, you know this means your appendix is in crisis. The writers of huffington post, are arguing basically, that you should go to the kitchen, and without any medical knowledge or the proper facilities remove your own appendix. Absurd no?

Most of us aren't doctors, but most of us have raised kids. If you were to leave your kids to raise themselves, no adult intervention or interaction, how would that work out for the kids? Could they raise them self properly without some outside interverntion? Again, that would be absurd. There are stories of kids growing up in the wilds apart from civilization, and those children who were raised by wolves so to speak, grow up to be train wrecks in largely the same ways.

If your dog has a hole in her belly, can she fix that herself? Certainly not. Some things require outside intervention, and the same is true of this hole in our heart and soul.

Scripture says:

Acts 17:27-28 that they should seek God, and perhaps feel their way toward him and find him. Yet he is actually not far from each one of us, for 'In him we live and move and have our being'; as even some of your own poets have said, 'For we are indeed his offspring.'
We long for God, for relationship with God. For intimacy with God. But scripture says this remains beyond our reach apart from the help of God.
Our efforts to reach God in our own strength are like the efforts of the child to raise himself, or like the dogs efforts to stitch up its own wounds, or a non-doctors effort to remove ones own appendix successfully. Or to paraphrase ephesians 2, like a dead bodies efforts at self-reanimation. Absolutely impossible.
There is ONLY one way to fill the void. Everything else you try is just a temporary bandage. The woman at the well seemed to use relationships. Relationships are important, but they don't fully fulfill that emptiness. Only Jesus can ever fully satisfy. Only Jesus fills the void. Only Jesus. Do you know him today?

One more thing, then we will close with the gospel. This final point was something I didn't catch the first number of reads. She had five husbands, you know how many groups of Samaritans there were?

In coming to this woman, she ended up believing and being saved, she ended up sharing the gospel with anyone who would listen vs 28. Her water jar was a VERY valuable item, and she just sets it down and takes off. Her mission had previously been to get water, but when she became a beliver in Jesus Christ, her mission changed completely. She was now telling people of Christ, trying to draw people to Christ.

That's our job – Christians. To tell people about Christ. I heard of a story this week. If you follow me on facebook you've likely heard about it too. So they couldn't command a Christian to steal things that aren't theirs, or to blaspheme, or worship an idol. Further the government cant command a Christian not to do something God commands – God commands us to worship him, if the government said no more worshipping then we are well within our rights to rebel against that, and thirdly, the government can not regulate the internal workings of, or worship of the church. So if the government says you can only meet at 15% capacity, that's the government regulating the internal workings of the church. At that point and Pastor Coates made this point in his final sermon before his arrest last week, at that point, if your government is interfering in the Local Church in that way, it is up to believers to call the government to repentance. Peaceful. Either through letters, through calling your government representatives, or through peaceful civil disobedience. One thing I appreciate about Governor Desantis is that any suggestions made for churches were left as suggestions. We suggest you not meet for a few weeks. Ok, we can do online church for a few weeks. We did that early last year, by request, not requirement of our government. I want to spend the rest of our time here today, praying for Pastor Coates – a Baptist – who is the first candian since theyv'e been under English rule to have been arrested for preaching. The slippery slope everyone was worried about, but hasn't been realized yet in America, is being realized in Canada.

I would like to spend the last few minutes praying for the world, and all the pastors out there like Pastor Coates that are facing persecution. I'll open, anyone who wants to pray in the mean time is welcome to, and then when its been quiet for a few minutes we will go ahead and close.
