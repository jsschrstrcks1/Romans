# Romans 13 — Trust God, Respect Authority (Raw Early Draft)

*Raw transcript, likely Obama-era (~2014–2016). Early treatment of Romans 13:1-7 before the polished series versions (`Romans 13.md` and `Romans 13b`). Contains unique illustrations and political specificity absent from later versions. Several factual and Scripture reference errors flagged below.*

Cops killing, being killed. Hundreds of cops killed last year. Average age was 40. Most had kids.

Who does that sound like? Who shed his blood so that we could be saved and secure? Now don't leave here thinking I said every cop is Christ. My point is that cops on a small scale, can identify with Christ, a little bit. They are some of the God given authorities over us — and deserving of our respect even when they are wrong.

Other authorities God has ordained. Ronald Reagan. FDR — his new deal may not have been a great deal, but his foresite helped us to be ready for WW2. What an impact his life has made on the world.

Others we might not like so much: Ruth Ginsberg — legal homosexual marriage, and supported Obamacare. Nancy — just vote for it, and then read it — Pelosi. Harry Reid. Chuck Shumer. Obama himself. I was talking with someone the other day who was surprised Obama hadn't been assassinated.

so don't miss this: God Ordained Nancy Pelosi to be where she is. God ordained Harry Reid to be where he is. God Ordained Chuck Schummer and God even ordained Obama to be our leader.

But we have to abide by them. We have to be respectful of them. We have to obey anything they say that doesn't directly conflict with scripture. If Obama orders you to abort a baby, you can disobey scripturally. If ever you are told by the government to worship some other god, then absolutely resist.

But if Obama asks us to pay for insurance for others, we may not like it, it may not be the best idea, but we have to (as long as that is the law of the land). If he makes it the law of the land that we must all buy our neighbor a different car, or something equally absurd. That we must do. If he orders us to give money to a food pantry, well then so be it.

When we resist for a bad reason we are at fault. Vs 3
Rebellion against authorities, is rebellion against God.
Who wouldn't punish their child for rebelling?

For one thing consider Proverbs 21:1 ⚠️ **Quoted below in NKJV; ESV reads: "The king's heart is a stream of water in the hand of the LORD; he turns it wherever he will."**

No matter what happens a leaders heart is in the hand of the Lord. God directs it like a river. He turns it this way, or that way, and accomplishes his will.
Secondly — and bear with me a minute, because this is deep stuff to consider.
God's knowledge of possible worlds. Different shoes, different results. Late vs early vs on time to work. Win vs lose WW2. String Theory. Called Molinism after Molina ~~the guy that thought about these things in the 1100s~~ ⚠️ **Luis de Molina was a 16th-century Jesuit (1535–1600); not 1100s**. ~~1100s you may remember was when the tower of London was constructed, and also around when the first crusade happened.~~

⚠️ **Theological note**: The label "Molinism" is imprecise. The pastor rejects most of Molinism (particularly middle knowledge as logically prior to God's decree), but affirms that God in His sovereignty knows all possible worlds — not merely the one that ended up existing. This is compatible with Reformed omniscience (God's *natural knowledge* of all possibilities), which Calvin, Owen, and the whole tradition affirm. The error here is the label and the Molina dating, not the underlying conviction that God's knowledge encompasses every possibility. The "String Theory" analogy is a loose illustration, not a theological commitment.

So if God knows everything, and scripturally I believe he does. IF he knows every possible everything, and we know that we can trust God, then we know that the evil of Nero had to happen to create a world wherein the most possible people would be saved and spend eternity in Heaven.

So in the end Trust God even when whats going on around us is beyond our comprehension. Trust God and respect our leader even when we are dealing with nero, or Obama. The long and short of it is trust God, and that starts with having a relationship with him. If you've never asked Jesus to pay the price for your sins, tonight is the night!

## Integrity Log

### Scripture Errors
- **"Romans 12:6"** → Should be **Hebrews 12:6** ("For the Lord disciplines the one he loves, and chastises every son whom he receives." ESV). Romans 12:6 is about spiritual gifts, not discipline.
- **Proverbs 21:1** — Quoted in NKJV, not ESV. ESV: "The king's heart is a stream of water in the hand of the LORD; he turns it wherever he will."

### Historical Errors
- **"in 55 Nero murdered his mother"** — Nero murdered Agrippina in **AD 59**. In AD 55, Nero poisoned his stepbrother Britannicus. Two different events.
- **"Molinism… in the 1100s"** — Luis de Molina lived 1535–1600 (16th century). Off by ~400 years.
- **FDR "shipping American military vehicles and men covertly"** — Lend-Lease Act (March 1941) shipped materiel publicly, not troops. US troops were not deployed before Pearl Harbor (December 1941).

### Theological Notes
- **Molinism label** is imprecise. The pastor rejects most of Molinism but affirms that God in His sovereignty knows all possible worlds, not merely the one that exists. The underlying conviction is sound; the label and the Molina dating are the errors, not the theology itself.
- **"String Theory"** — loose illustration, not a theological commitment; conflates physics multiverse with philosophical possible worlds.

### Factual Claims (Acceptable Range)
- Billy Graham "215 million people" — widely cited figure from BGEA; acceptable as delivered
- Paul writing Romans AD 56-58 from Greece — standard scholarly consensus
- "Hundreds of cops killed" — within range for mid-2010s line-of-duty deaths (all causes)
- Nero biographical details (aside from the AD 55/59 date confusion) are accurate
