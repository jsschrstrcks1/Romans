In the culture of the day two invitations were sent. One in advance and a second on the day of the event to remind people. 

No on initially rejected the invitation. But all of the original guests reject it on the day of the party. Whats worse is they are very transparently liars in their excuses. 

Pretty much everyone here has bought something expensive no? One excuse is that they had just bought a field and needed to go check it out. So think about that for a moment. If you just dropped 100 grand or more on a piece of property – wouldn’t you have checked it out in advance? Wouldn’t you be familiar with every rock and tree? If you were buying a car, wouldn’t you have already taken it for a test drive and maybe had it checked out by a mechanic first! The car is a much smaller purchase. Yet the fellow who purchased the team of oxen – the financial equivalent of buying a new car – seems to have neglected to have done his due diligence. 

The newly weds – in our culture that might be an excuse. But back then it wasn’t even close to a good excuse. They spent much of their first year engaging in these types of social events. Being newly married was pretty much obliged your friends and family to have you over all the time. It helped the newly weds financially. But more than that, it was a learning experience for the newly weds. A friend of mine once said that you don’t really know anyone until you’ve vacationed with them. The more time you spend with people the more likely you are to see their flaws, and with married people you are more likely to see the flaws in their marriage. I think this would likely have a couple of effects on the newly weds – it would show them that no ones happy marriage is perfect. It would show them how people who have been married for a long time can deal with disagreements, and so forth. Lots of wisdom – and other thigns – for better or worse were passed on this way. So this newly married couple would have been pretty much obligated to have gone to the banquet. All three of these excuses are flimsy lies. The host would have been deeply offended.

So if then there are two groups of people, and the party is heaven, what are those two groups of people? 

Lets start with the first group – those that rejected the invitation. In the other examples, they would be the goats, the tares, and the chaff. They would be the ones that found the wide gate that leads to destruction. This first group is any individual that rejects God’s son Jesus’s payment for their sin on the cross. 

You see according to scripture, and I’m summarizing here, things were originally created to be perfect here on earth. Scripture says lions lay down with lambs. There was no sin, no cancer, no sickness. No wheel chairs were necessary, no emphysema or COPD, or alzheimers. It was created to be perfect. There was one command we were given, and that was not to eat from the tree at the center of the garden. Like any kids, what did adam and eve do? They promptly ate from the prohibited tree. That act of sin broke the mold so to speak. The 10$ phrase is actually imputation by corporate headship. But it just means that when Adam and Eve sinned, it shattered the created order in a way that is unrepairable by us. Our best efforts to repair it fall far far short. Further as a result of this shattering, we are all born with the innate ability and capacity for sin. You could even go so far as to say a love for sin. We all persue sin with our lives trying to find wholeness. Trying to repair this shattering of creation by doing things that temporarily make you feel whole again, but that ultimately leave you feeling emptier than you were to start with. 

Scripture talks about this – it says in Romans 3:23 that all have sinned and fallen short of the glory of God. It goes on to say a couple chapters later in 6:23 that the wages, what we deserve for our life persuing sin, instead of God, is death. Eternal separation from God is all that we deserve in life. 

God invited all of us to the party in heaven, a thing that ultimately cost his son his life. Think about that for a second. If your child gave his or her life for something, and your loved ones completely rejected not only what your son had purchased with his life, but also your son. As a parent, how would that make you feel? 

This is just a microcosm of how God feels. He is a perfect, and holy, and just, and infinite God. Who experienced the death of his son in deeper ways that we can ever even imagine. In a more full and complete way than we can ever imagine. That’s not to make light of anyone whose lost a child. It’s a devastating thing. But as a human being, we can only experience a small amount of the things God can experience right? God is infinite. Were not. God knows all and sees all. I grew up thinking my parents were both of those things, but in truth only God knows all, only God sees all. Our best most intense love is a flickering candle compared to the blazing inferno of the Love of God, and so because we are limited, while it is devastating for us, we can’t experience things in the same way God does. I think the hymn has under stated it where it says “behold the pain of searing loss”.  God experienced the loss of Jesus in dozens of ways beyond our ability to comprehend. So when we reject his Son and choose the wide gate, when we reject his son, and his sons invitation to heaven, God is absolutely just to respond with wrath. 

The second group are those that go to the party, they are the sheep, the wheat, and those that find the narrow gate. These are the group of people that at some point before they die, cease rejecting Jesus Christ and his sacrifice, and his invitation to spend eternity in heaven with him.

So I mentioned before that we have all sinned, all fallen short, and the payment we deserve is to be eternally separated from God. But we read in Romans 5:8 while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us. That is to say there was nothing special, or particularly redeeming about us. We were a messed up broken, bunch of people. But God so loved the world that he sent his one and only son Jesus, so that whosoever believes in him, shall not perish, but have eternal life.
God made a cosmic trade. His son, for the wretched busted shambles of people. The only requirement was that they have a relationship with his son Jesus. That relationship is the difference between the two groups. Sheeps and goats, wide gate, narrow gate, wheat and chaff and wheat and tares. If you have a relationship with jesus Christ, you are invited to the party. If you don’t have that relationship – then its not too late to change sides. Scripture says if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus Christ, and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved!

Its that easy. So as the praise team comes up to sing this final song. Take an honest look at your self, and think about which group are you in. 

The one that rejects Jesus Christ and ends up encountering the wrath of God, or the one that accepts the invitation, has a relationship With Jesus Christ, and goes to the party/heaven.
