Background on Ezekiel. For the first 25 chapters, Ezekiel is in exile. The nation of Jerusalem is divided.
In 597 the babalonians exiled the king of Judah along with his people. This included Ezekiel. At the beginning of the book of Ezekiel Jerusalem stands. Israel stands. Smack in the middle of the book Jerusalem is sacked by the Babylonians and the temple destroyed, and then the last section from vs chapter 33 on, including our passage tonight, were written after the temple had fallen. 

The temple was the central point of jewish life. If you wanted a job, you networked with people at the temple to get one. If you wanted news, you hung out at the temple and talked to people, and learned of the machinations of the outside world. If you needed a loan, you looked for one at the temple. You wanted to buy land, you found your seller at the temple. You were ready to worship God or ready to submit to his system of sacrifices to cover sins, you went to the temple. It was smack dab In the middle of their every day life.
