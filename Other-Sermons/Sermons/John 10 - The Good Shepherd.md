The introduction
Some of you know, we picked up a momma sheep and a her baby ram a week or two ago. I'm amazed by how quickly they learn your voice. The first day, she was kind of stand off-ish at feeding time. But the second day, she saw me coming, heard me calling, and came running just like the rest of the sheep. I think we've settled on a name for the momma – were calling her Hope. The baby's name is still up in the air, but were kicking around the name Sir Loin. Now all I have to do for Hope to coming running, is to step into the feed shed, and take the lid off the feed bucket. That simple noise is enough to get her attention, and she starts guarding the feed bucket as though she is not going to let the others eat.

Its funny, the moms have a hard time adjusting to a new sheep around, but the babies, they take to each other so quickly. The moms are pushing each other out of the way to try and get some food. But the babies, they walk up to each other, bump noses a little bit, and then bam, best friends.

Any way, were going to be in Jn 10 tonight, which is one of the major chapters that talks about Believers as sheep. There are three parts to this chapter, Jesus's Illustration, His explanation, and his application.

The Illustration 10:1-6
Come through the proper door. You can't fool anyone. The sheep know their shepherd, and the shepherd their sheep.
A few weeks ago, someone tossed a cat into our yard. They didn't know us, they didn't ask. We didn't want a cat, but they just tossed it over our fence. Dad saw it happen. We instantly knew that we had this extra un-wanted cat.
Likewise we would notice if a strange sheep had been dropped off in an improper way.
God too knows who amoung us are his sheep, and who are trying to fake it.
As a shepherd you get to know each of your sheep. Every sheep has its own personality. Little Anna when you start making noises like you're going to feed her sometimes runs and jumps and spins in the air. Hope if you don't feed her fast enough, she might nip at you. Frazzle is aloof, and distant. Timid until its feeding time. She's probably the most aggressive with the other sheep. Sugar is a sweetie, usually comes when called, tolerates being petted. So too God knows us in ways beyond our comprehension.
People that are faking being Christians are like the thief that hops the fence.
Fake Christians can't help causing problems, if you aren't a child of God you are a child of whom? There is only one other option. Being a servant of the enemy, even in trying to do good, in your heart if no where else you're doing evil.
In Israel, all of the shepherds would keep all of their sheep in a communal pen. Every evening every shepherd would drive their sheep into the pen, and a hired hand, a porter, would sleep at the entrance, and anything that tried to get in our out would have to go through him. Anything that came over the wall was likely to be a human thief. Some times false shepherds would try to call other peoples sheep from the gate, but they wouldn't come to the false shepherd. The sheep would only respond to the real shepherd. The one they know, that protects them, that feeds them, that makes them walk beside the still waters, whose rod and staff were a comfort.
The explanation 10:7-21
Christ is the man sleeping at the door. He protects us from anything that seeks to do us harm.
Likewise any sheep that comes in through the door is safe.
We know Christ, we can hear him calling.
The Holy Spirit tugs at our hearts during the salvation process. We can feel the call of Christ. His work in us began before the foundation of the world Eph 1:4-5: just as He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world, that we would be holy and blameless before Him. In love 5He predestined us to adoption as sons through Jesus Christ to Himself, according to the kind intention of His will.
He makes a distinction here between a hired hand, and the sheeps owner: vs 12-18 (re-read)
Hired hand probably made minimum wage. He doesn't really care what happens beyond his
own safety.
Owner has a vested interest, owner's livelihood is at stake, owner knows the sheep, each sheep
has a unique personality. The shepherds family probably only ate because of the sheep. Because so much depended on the sheep, he was willing to do whatever it takes to keep them safe, even death on the cross.
How can anything compete with that? In order to steal a sheep, you'd have to be more capable, motivated, and intelligent. Our shepherd is Christ – he with God created the entire universe with a command. Nothing else holds a candle to that. Nothing else is strong enough to over power Christ.
Some of you might have noticed Jesus talking about his "other" sheep. Remember Paul said repeatedly in Romans, to the jew first, and then the greek. The other sheep are you and I.
The application 10:22-30
Some of the jews like us are sheep, and no one has ever accused a sheep of being smart.
So when we pick up again in vs 22 it mentions that its winter now. This subject is likely
something they had been discussing for several months by this point. They thought he was mad,
or possessed by a demon.
So Christ broke it down for them into bite size pieces, and what we are left with is a beautiful
picture of Jesus safe guarding our salvation.
So who are Christs sheep?
Those who hear his voice – faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.
Those that know, and are known by Christ.
Those that follow Christ - "If you love me, keep my commands. jn 14
Because they are Christ sheep, they have eternal life, and are secure
If we knew something was coming after an animal we loved, we'd sit outside with our guns and wait for that predator, we'd dispatch that wolf, or coyote, and protect our Sheep. God is no different.

We who are believers in Jesus Christ, are sitting in the hand of God. Anyone who would seek to take away your salvation has to get through Christ to get to you, and if that weren't enough, God the father is there too.
So let me ask you this. Are you stronger than God?
