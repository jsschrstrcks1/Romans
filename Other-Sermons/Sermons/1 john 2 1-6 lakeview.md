In fact, this is an important thing to talk about in this particular passage. There is a verse here that could have been better translated. When reading the Greek, you get the sense that John is talking about his immediate context – the Jews. But also, salvation is offered to the half jewish Samaritans, and even the gentiles. Basically in their economy, there were only these three groups of people. There were those like "us", those who were half like us (Samaritans) and those who were not of us – the gentiles. In declaring that Jesus died for the sins of the whole world, and based on the way John uses the word World in his other writings, its safe to say that John meant the Christian world, or the elect. Basically, if your name is written in the lambs book of life, then you're saved, regardless of whether you're a jew, or an American, or Asian, or Australian, or even arab muslims, and people like the Kurds we were praying for earlier. If God has chosen you, if God has elected you, if you are one of those Given to Christ, whose sins have been paid for then there is nothing more to worry about. You are eternally secure.
