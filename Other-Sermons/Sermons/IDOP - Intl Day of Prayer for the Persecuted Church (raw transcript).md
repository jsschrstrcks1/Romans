**Pastor Han's Story**

This is a picture of someone who kinda resembles Pastor Han. He's remarkable because he was Korean by birth, Chinese by naturalization. He was a pastor serving in a border town. ## Corrections Log

**IMPORTANT: Review before reuse. Items marked ⚠️ need correction or verification.**

### Scripture
1. ⚠️ **2 Cor 11:23b–33 is NIV, not ESV.** The text as read matches the NIV rendering (e.g., "been in prison more frequently, been flogged more severely" vs. ESV "far more imprisonments, with countless beatings"). If reusing, swap to ESV or note the translation.
2. ⚠️ **Matt 5:44 is NIV.** "But I tell you" = NIV; ESV reads "But I say to you." Minor but note for consistency if the church standard is ESV.

### People Group: Bairagi
3. ⚠️ **"52 families" should be "52 gotras (clans)."** A gotra is an exogamous clan lineage, not a family. Population (~3.6 million) is close to "nearly 4 million" — acceptable.
4. ⚠️ **"Many are Muslim" overstates it.** Muslim Bairagi exist but are tracked as a separate, smaller people group. The Hindu Bairagi are the primary group; saying "many are Muslim" may give a wrong impression of the proportions.
5. **"Priestesses" unconfirmed.** Sources confirm Bairagi men serve as priests; women serving as priestesses is not attested in available sources.

### Pastor Han Chung-Ryeol
6. ⚠️ **"Korean by birth, Chinese by naturalization" is inaccurate.** He was ethnic Korean born in China (Korean-Chinese / Joseonjok). He did not immigrate from Korea and naturalize — he was a Chinese citizen of Korean descent from birth.
7. **Lured to the border and killed — substantially accurate.** Sources confirm he was lured from his home after a phone call, went against his usual judgment, and was stabbed to death. His deacon Jang Moon Seok was kidnapped by North Korea in 2014. The exact details (specific man, specific wounds: 1 heart, 1 jugular, 7 head) cannot be independently confirmed from public sources — they may come from the VOM interview or documentary.
8. **"A pastor doesn't abandon his flock" — substantially accurate.** VOM campaign #IWillNotAbandonThem; he insisted on returning to Changbai when urged to stay in Seoul.
9. **Funeral details and peace on his face — cannot verify.** May come from VOM Korea's documentary or private briefings. Not in public sources.
10. **36 martyrs with VOM Korea — confirmed.** Pastor Eric Foley of VOM Korea has confirmed this number.

### North Korea
11. ⚠️ **"Jerusalem of the East" refers to Pyongyang specifically**, not all of Korea. At its peak, 3 in 10 Pyongyang residents were practicing Christians.
12. ⚠️ **"2 of the 10 largest churches started by North Koreans"** — partially accurate. Youngnak Presbyterian Church (Seoul, 60,000 members) was founded in 1945 by refugees from Soviet-occupied North Korea. The second is unclear; Yoido Full Gospel Church (870,000 members) was founded by David Yonggi Cho, who was born in what is now South Korea.
13. ⚠️ **"10 Glorious Principles" — wrong name.** The official name is "Ten Principles for the Establishment of a Monolithic Ideological System" (1974). The word "glorious" does not appear in the title.
14. **Korean trinity, perverted hymns and scripture, weekly worship — all substantially accurate.** Well-documented features of Juche ideology's deliberate mimicry of Christianity.
15. ⚠️ **"160,000 in concentration camps" — within historical range but may be dated.** Estimates have varied widely (80,000–200,000). More recent estimates (post-2020) suggest 53,000–120,000 as some camps closed. The 160,000 figure was common in 2000s–2010s reporting.
16. **Three generations guilt by association — confirmed.** Kim Il Sung stated in 1972: "Factionalists and enemies of class, whoever they are, their seed must be eliminated through three generations."
17. **Neighbor surveillance (inminban system) — substantially accurate.** The "two doors down" phrasing is a simplification of the inminban system where neighborhood units of 25–50 families monitor each other.
18. **"Millions and millions of people just killed off" — imprecise.** Estimates of Christians killed under Kim Il Sung vary widely and are difficult to verify. The claim is directionally true (mass persecution occurred) but "millions and millions" may overstate what can be documented.

### Book Reference
19. ⚠️ **Book about the girl in the concentration camp — unidentified.** The closest match is *These Are the Generations* (VOM Korea), which chronicles a North Korean Christian family through generations of persecution. Other candidates: *The Hard Road Out* (Jihyun Park), *In Order to Live* (Yeonmi Park), or the story of Hea Woo (VOM). The sermon may be drawing from a VOM-published testimony. If reusing, identify the specific book and verify details.
