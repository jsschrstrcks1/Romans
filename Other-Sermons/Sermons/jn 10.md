Thankfulness 

If you’ve brought your bibles and I hope you have, open with me to jn 10. Were going to talk about thanksfulness a little bit this morning, Thankfulness is hard when you’re down. But its appropriate. I heard a story this week about a pastor whose little 2 year old daughter was diagnosed with terminal Leukemia. Of course that reminded me of Bob Clark and his Son Rob, who faced a terminal diagnosis of leukemia in Rob. If you know Bob, you know that God spared Rob, and he went on to get a Masters Degree in counseling, and he’s practicing in Texas. That story could have gone wildly differently but for the Grace and Mercy of God. Likewise this little girl, was diagnosed with terminal cancer but at a much younger age. She was diagnosed with cancer at two years old.
Worse, normally you have your immune system to help fight cancer, but in this case the baby was born with the cancer already growing in her little body. This meant that perhaps his wife had leukemia as well, and just hadn’t discovered it yet. Testing was done the same day, while they were sitting there weeping with their little toddler given this death sentence. Come to find out, the mother had it too. Both were going to be a difficult match for donner marrow, both needed the same type. No one in their family was a match. None of the other four children, nor the brothers or sisters or parents were suitable donors. They dosed both with chemo therapy. They dosed both with radiation, trying to extend their lives long enough to find a match. Even with a match they were told that if both lived another five years, both would most likely make it an additional 15 short of some other tragedy happening. The chemo had limited effect. The radiation seemed to slow it, but not stop it.
Things kept deteriorating. Days turned into weeks, weeks turned into months.
Each day this pastor spent time on his knees pleading with God for his family. Each day they continued to get a little worse. The radiation being shot into their bone marrow made them VERY susceptible to being sick.
Then one day while he was praying, in tears, for his family, a sense of peace washed over him. He got up, and went home, showered for the first time in a couple days, changed his clothes and went back, and in the hour that took, his little girls color was returning. His wife was looking better also. The doctors were perplexed. the radiation hadn’t been stopping the cancer to this point, just slowing it. What happened? the ran tests. They couldn’t find a trace of leukemia in either person. The hospital kept them for several more days, their immune response improved, they were free to go.
It has now been 15 years since they were released from the hospital. Now a chaplain, the pastor takes his family back to the very room they recieved their death sentences in and Give thanks to God.
Normally you don’t think about going back to the place where your worst day happened, normally that wouldn’t happen. But yet we are to be thankful in all things.
Stephen Davey - pointed out that thanksgiving - which is just around the corner for us - has its roots in the civil war. The woman who penned the poem Mary Had a Little lamb prevailed upon president lincoln to institute a day of thankfulness during the darkest days of the civil war. Lincoln wanted it to be a day of national repentance, and thanksgiving to God. It has become so engrained in our nation ever since every president has made the declaration. It strikes me that only once was thanksgiving not celebrated in November, and that was become President Johnson forgot to make the declaration, instead if was celebrated in december that year. Even presidents sometimes forget to be thankful.
Our passage today reminds us, tells us, commands us to be thankful. In fact one of my favorite preachers calls this the first thanksgiving hymn. I hadn’t really thought too much about it, but sometimes we need to be told to be thankful dont we? Its something we do with kids all the time. I have to tell one of my nieces to lose the attitude. These days people hear giving thanks, or thanksgiving and think of shopping, football, and food. But thats neither what the bible means when it talks about being thankful, nor is it what lincoln wanted for the day.
It strikes me that there are several lists in the bible of what it looks like to be a christian, or to be lost, and being thankful, giving thanks is on the list for the christian, and commanded of the christian in several places, meanwhile Paul when he talks about the world, his lists often have thanklessness. In fact in 1 timothy, he put the word Ungrateful in the same sentence as “unholy, brutal, haters of good, treacherous, conceited, lovers of pleasure rather than lovers of God”. I encourage you to read 2 timothy 3:2 and following later for the context of that.
The point is there is a stark difference between the Grateful Christian, and the ungrateful lost person.
Are you grateful? Take a moment to mentally audit your thoughts and words of the last few days. If a stranger were to put you into one of those two categories based on just the thoughts and words of the past week, would they say you are grateful? or ungrateful? What about your actions? grateful? or ungrateful? Do you serve God grudgingly? or with thanksgiving in your heart?
Its easy to fall into the ungrateful category. When someone asks the same question for the 40th time, or when someone needs something trivial to them, done at great cost to you. Its easy to be ungrateful in the teeth of pain and loss. Its easy to be ungrateful, when you’re working for a boss that doesn’t appreciate you. When you are being ungrateful however, you’re elevating yourself, and your desires above the situation.
Who of us with our mouthes would say “I value me, more than I value your need”, and yet how easy is it to say those things with our actions, and lack of action. Just like my nieces, sometimes we need to be told to be thankful. Our attitudes and actions need to be pressed and molded like clay in the masters hands, to better reflect Christ in this.
I want you guys to name some things that Christ went through on the earth. Just shout them out, one person at a time.
Alright, now show me where in scripture Jesus complained? Where did he whine and cry about what God had asked him to do? where did he have a fit, and quit. Quite the opposite - we know that it was hard for him, we know that in the garden that night he wept tears of blood because he KNEW just what was coming, and how hard it would be, and yet its said of Jesus in hebrews 12:2-3 “For the joy set before him he endured the cross, scorning its shame, and sat down at the right hand of the throne of God. 3 Consider him who endured such opposition from sinners, so that you will not grow weary and lose heart.”
Or in Jn 10:11Jesus says: “I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd gives his life for the sheep”
Or in John 10:18
Jesus says: 17 For this reason the Father loves me, because I lay down my life that I may take it up again. 18 No one takes it from me, but I lay it down of my own accord. I have authority to lay it down, and I have authority to take it up again. This charge I have received from my Father.”
No one takes my life from me. I lay it down. I don’t have to, but I have chosen to. Why you ask? A man named Pink wrote this: “When Christ died, He did so of His own voluntary will. Without [Jesus’] own consent, none could have harmed a hair of His head… It was not the nails [that put Jesus on the cross and held Him there unto death] but [it was] the strength of [Jesus’] love to the Father, and to His elect [people, sinners like you and me] which held Him to the Cross.”
John 15:12
“This is my commandment, that you love one another as I have loved you. 13Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends.
I’m as guilty here as anyone. Sometimes if something is going to require me to go out of my way, sometimes I grumble. But what was Christ’s example? To Love people, like Christ loved us. How much did Christ love us? He loved us this much (arms out). He loved us To the cross and back again. He loved us to death and back. And yet there are times we cant love each other enough to cross the street. Sometimes we don’t love each other enough to deal with their noise. Sometimes we don’t love each other enough to tell people the truth that they need to hear. sometimes we don’t love people enough to work through something complicated and tiresome.
Any of you sometimes identify with any of that?
Or you could say those things this way. Sometimes I love my self so much, I don’t want to give up my time. Sometimes I love my self so much that I don’t want to deal with someone elses noise. Sometimes I love my self, and my comfort so much that I won’t tell someone what they need to hear. If you raised your hand, then its also true of you that Sometimes you worship yourself instead of God.
Next time you open your mouth to complain, I want you to remember that. Complaining happens when you worship yourself instead of God. When you elevate your rights, above God’s command to love others as he has loved you. It happens when you worship yourself instead of GOd.
When you complain you are telling other people all of this about yourself. You’re saying with your mouth, and with your actions that you care more about yourself, and your rights, and your own comfort, and security than you do about what God is doing. If you also tell them you worship God, in that moment you complain you make yourself a liar.
Sometimes following God and worshipping God means denying yourself.

Firstly scripture says “deny himself” deny yourself. I looked up the verb Deny in the greek it means “ mainly … denying relationship to a person reject, disown; (2) as choosing to live in a selfless way deny or disregard oneself (

Can we truly be denying ourselves when we are complaining about how badly a thing affects us? Secondly is take up our cross. this was meant to be shocking and offensive. Innocent people weren’t put to death on the cross, only those who were desperately wicked and evil. A death on the cross was to bring shame and guilt on yourself. A path he willingly chose. A path he could have left at any time. But Jesus love for you and me held him their more firmly than the nails through his hands and feet.
So when your dog dies. Give thanks. When your truck breaks - give thanks. When someone makes a noise you don’t like, give thanks. When someone makes life so much more complicated, give thanks. Give thanks not for the truck, or dog’s death, or noise, give thanks for JEsus, and his death. Give thanks for CHrist’s sacrifice.
In the light of Christ’s death, how bad is a noise? In the light of Christ’s death, how bad is a truck problem? or people who make life complicated? or even the death of someone or something you love. God’s been there. he KNOWS what you are going through, and has been through it. I don’t think there is much worse we can go through this side of hell, than losing a child, and yet God knows what thats like. HE watched as we brutally murdered Christ in a way that implied shame Christ didn’t deserve. In so doing he who knew no sin became sin for us. We quite simply deserved to spend eternity in Hell. And yet Jesus, because of his great love, took our punishment, and in so doing wiped away our sin debt so that we could spend eternity in heaven. That’s how much Jesus loved you. In the light of that, what do we have to complain about? Everything else is a triviality compared to the fate we would have shared in Hell apart from Christ. So that bad news, that story about the pastors wife and daughter having cancer, even if they had both died, it’s a reminder of what we deserve, and have been spared from. That’s why they go back to the same room every year. They and we have so much to be thankful for. Don’t in your complaining spit in the eye of Christ. Gospel:
Romans 3:23 – for all have sinned and fallen short of the glory of God. 

Every single one of us has messed up somehow, I doubt any of you would disagree with that, and if you do, come see me later and we will talk. Any way, we’ve all messed up. All missed the mark, all fallen short of God’s standard. If God’s is perfect and holy, his standard is perfect holiness. A standard we are not capable of reaching. Even one sin is enough to make us fall short, and if were being honest most of us have committed more than one sin. Because of that, scripture says we deserve something. 

Romans 6:23a for the wages of Sin is death.

You always hear people say “I don’t deserve this” or “I deserve that”, the only thing we truly deserve is an eternity in hell. What we have earned for our life spent In sin, our wages, is an eternity spent apart from God. There are only two places, heaven and hell. God’s in heaven, so if you’re apart from God you’re where? 

Bad news. But it gets worse before it Gets better. 

Ephesians 2:8 says “for its by grace you’ve been saved, through faith, and this not of yourself it’s the gift of God so no one can boast”. 

So not only did even one sin cause us to be deserving of death, and eternal separation from God, but we can’t do anything about it our self “this is not of yourself”. We can’t earn it, we can’t buy it, can’t steal it. 

The goodnews is that 2000 years ago God sent his son Jesus to pay the price for our sins. Scripture says “John 3:16 – for God so loved the world that he gave his one and only son that whosoever believes in him will not perish but have ever lasting life” We deserved death, but he died for us. But that wasn’t the end. history records over 500 witnesses that saw him alive AFTER one of the most brutal public executions ever recorded. The roman soldiers own lives were on the line, they were motivated to make sure Jesus was dead, if he hadn’t been, then their own lives were forfeit – they would be crucified. Yet after his death, over 500 witnesses saw him alive, talked to him. One even wanted to stick his fingers in the nail holes in Jesus hands and feet. 

Strikes me, if someones been to heaven and come back to talk about it, he would be the guy to ask for directions on how to get back. Fortunately we have scripture, and scripture says 

Romans 6:23 the wages of sin is death…. BUT the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord. 

We deserved death. But were given a chance at life. What a gift. 

Romans 10:9 says that “if you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus Christ, and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead You WILL be saved. That’s all there is to it.
