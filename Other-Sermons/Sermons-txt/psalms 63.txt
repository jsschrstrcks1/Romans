So then are we staying focused on Christ and God, second lets look at what happened to david as a result of his focus on God. 5-7

‌
There seems to be a pattern here. Vs 5 his soul will be satisfied. What had he been doing to find this satisfaction? He had been yearning after God. Probably engaging in the spiritual disciplines. Reading the bible, prayer, worship, and today we would add evangelism. What does he do as a result of finding joy in the Lord, finding this satisfaction in the Lord? He seems to double down. If a little is good more is better. Vs 6 he spends time in meditation RE the Lord in his bed, in the watches. There were three watches at this point in history in the night. When he’s sleepless he focuses on God, he probably prayed, he meditated on scripture, he didn’t focus on his own problem he focused on God, whom he knew would provide the solution. He praises God for having provided him with solutions in the past and for covering David in the shadow of “your wings”. Now that phrase shadow of your wings is something that scholars have talked about at considerable length. Some argue that in our weakest times God is protecting us like a mother bird. When we think of birds we think of doves, or robins, or hummingbirds, or whatever little bird we generally see on a daily basis. But let me tell you some birds are quite fierce. We have guinea hens, I saw a video of thes guinea hens in Africa – momma guinea hens attacking this deadly poisonous snake they circled it, and whichever of the three mommas wasn’t being looked at by the snake would attack the snake. One bite at a time they killed that deadly black mamba snake, and saved their babies. These same birds when threatened by bigger predators will sacrifice their own life to save their babies. They attack with abandon dogs, and cats, and even sometimes the big cats of Africa. This ferocious protectiveness is what comes to my mind when people argue that it’s a momma bird protecting her babies. 
‌


‌
Others argued for a different kind of wing. Ezekiel describes one such type. I recommend you read it later, because it’s a lot of verses and were close to being finished for the night. Any way you’ll find this description in 
Ezekiel 1
and again in 10. Each of the angels had four faces, a lion, an eagle, a man and an ox. They have the stature of a man, with the legs of a cow. They each had four wings, and under the wings they had arms like that of a man. Each wing was completely covered with eyeballs. Surrounding each of these was a cloud, and lightning, and bright light. Other passages of scripture talk about buildings quaking when they speak. These are the angels that were used to guard the garden of eden after Adam and eve were kicked out, and the only other place we really see them in scripture is around the throne of God. IF this is what David meant by the shadow of your wings – and that makes sense in my mind, then david was being protected by God’s personal attendants. It would be not too dissimilar then the president assigning you secret service protection. 
‌


‌
These angels would have been able to see in every direction all around them with perfect clarity. Phones are starting to model this type of thing to a lesser extent. Cameras also! Cameras and phones have pretty much all always had only one single sensor. It took decent pictures, but the pictures always fell flat compared to the memory of what you saw. When we look we see in three dimensions. Pictures can’t show but two. The depth is lost. So they figured that out, and they are making cameras that can capture more information. The new iphones have two sensors and that lets you retain more information when a photo is taken. A camera I read about last year had hundreds of sensors. Each sensor would focus on a different element within the scene, and then on your computer later you can change the focus, zoom in, zoom out, focus on the foreground, the background, or everything all at once. It makes amazing pictures. These angels would have that ability to see and focus their eyes on everything all at once. There would be no blind spots. There would never be a thing that was out of focus or fuzzy. 
‌


‌
In motorcycle school they taught us that the average person can only truly see about three degrees in front of them. Anything out side of that you detect motion and that’s about it. With thousands of eyes you’d have perfect vision from every angle from every side. Not only that but this angel has some characteristics of four different creatures. Sentience, capable of every bit of thought we are and possibly more, parts of a lion, and an ox, and an eagle. Can you imagine something with the ferocity of a lion, the strength of an ox, the cunning and ability to fly of an eagle with the brain of a man (or better). Perfect vision for multiple targets in every direction concurrently? I would be thrilled with a protective lion. But combine everything together and you have a unquestionably the best guardian in existence. And david seems to be saying here that when he’s at his lowest, God sends his best angels to protect him, to cover him with their wings. By the way, with eyes on the inside of their wings they can watch whom they are protecting just as well as anything else. This would allow them to provide comfort as well. 
‌


‌
IN any case, it’s a very loving thing for God to do. When we are at our weakest he sends his best, either personally protecting us like a mother bird, or else sending his personal attendents to shelter us with their wings. It is truly rest that only God can provide. Those wings would shelter, hide, and protect all at once.

You see Christ came and died for us. Scripture says because of the bad things we’ve done, we deserve to be separated from God for eternity, we deserved death. But Jesus took that punishment for us. He said “I am the way, I am the truth, I am the life. No one comes to the father but by me”.
