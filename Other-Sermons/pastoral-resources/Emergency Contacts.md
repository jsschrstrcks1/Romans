**Coordinated Entry System (CES) — How to Get Into the System**

The Coordinated Entry System is the HUD-required process for accessing housing programs in Pasco County. Getting assessed through CES is the **first step** toward Rapid Re-Housing (RRH) or Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH). The church should help members call the appropriate access point and, if possible, go with them.

**Who to Call:**

| Household Type | Organization | Phone |
|---------------|-------------|-------|
| **Families with minor children** | Coalition for the Homeless of Pasco County | **(727) 842-8605, ext. 7** |
| **Families with minor children** | One Community Now | **(727) 849-4724** — 5841 Main St., NPR, M-F 9am-4pm |
| **Single individuals / Adult households** | Metropolitan Ministries BrigAIDe | **(727) 756-1952** |
| **Single individuals / Adult households** | The Sword and the Spoon | **(727) 378-4833** |
| **Veterans** | St. Vincent de Paul CARES SSVF | **(727) 203-3896** |

**What happens when they call:**
1. A diversion screening determines if alternatives to shelter exist.
2. If not, a VI-SPDAT vulnerability assessment is completed (within 3 business days).
3. The household is placed on the By-Name List (BNL), prioritized by need and vulnerability.
4. As housing becomes available, the highest-priority households are matched and referred.
5. **The household must update their contact info and housing status every 90 days** to remain active on the list. If 90 days pass with no contact, they are moved to inactive.

**Who is eligible for CES:**
- Currently homeless (sleeping outside, in a tent, vehicle, shelter, or place not meant for habitation)
- Chronically homeless (homeless 12+ months continuously, or 4+ episodes totaling 12 months in 3 years, with a disabling condition)
- Exiting an institution (hospital, jail, treatment center) where they stayed less than 90 days and were homeless before entry
- Fleeing domestic violence

**What CES leads to:**
- **Rapid Re-Housing (RRH)**: Short-term rental and move-in assistance to exit homelessness quickly.
- **Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH)**: Long-term housing with supportive services for chronically homeless individuals with a disabling condition.
- **Homelessness Prevention**: Financial assistance to keep at-risk households from losing their housing.

**Notes for Leadership**

1. **This list will go stale.** Hours change, organizations close, new ones open. Review and update at least annually. **Call before sending someone.**
2. **The Coordinated Entry System is the most important entry point.** Getting a member assessed and onto the By-Name List is the single most impactful thing the church can do beyond emergency hotel nights.
3. **Go with them.** Many of these organizations require in-person visits, paperwork, and patience. A church member or leader accompanying someone through this process is worth more than a phone number on a sheet of paper.
4. **Keep copies of this directory accessible**
5. **When sources conflict on hours or addresses**, call the organization directly. This directory was compiled from multiple sources (Pasco County government, Coalition for the Homeless, Pasco Sheriff's Office Community Resource Guide, Pasco County Survival Guide, etc) and discrepancies exist.
6. **The Volunteer Way operates multiple locations**: the Congress Street food pantry in Port Richey, and the Moon Lake Mission (soup kitchen, showers, clothing, social services assistance) on Moon Lake Road in NPR. Both use the same phone number: (727) 815-0433.
7. **Father and Son Food Pantry** has been listed at two addresses across sources: 1627 Chesapeake Dr., Odessa and 21418 Carson Dr., Land O' Lakes. Call (813) 846-9993 to confirm the current location.
8. **Metropolitan Ministries operates two Pasco locations**: West Pasco at 3214 US 19, Holiday and East Pasco (Restored Hope) at 13703 17th St., Dade City. Both offer food, clothing, and financial assistance. The West location also provides residential housing and 3 hot meals daily.
9. **For members near the Hernando County line**, Appendix B lists Hernando resources that may be closer. Several organizations (SpringLife Church, St. Vincent de Paul Spring Hill, Jericho Road) serve both counties.
