## II. ATTRIBUTED QUOTES VERIFICATION

### 1. Jonathan Edwards — "Our role in salvation is the sin that made it necessary"

**Attribution**: ⚠️ LIKELY MISATTRIBUTED

The commonly circulated form is: "You contribute nothing to your salvation except the sin that made it necessary." This is widely attributed to Edwards in Reformed and Baptist literature, but no one has located it in Edwards' verified works. The *Works of Jonathan Edwards Online* does not contain this quotation. The Puritan Board forum has investigated extensively and concluded the quote most likely originates with **William Temple** (1881–1944), Archbishop of Canterbury, who wrote: "The only thing of our very own which we contribute to our salvation is the sin which makes it necessary." A similar sentiment also appears in B.B. Warfield.

The polished version quotes it as Edwards. The existing source note in `Sermon - Grace Alone.md` already flagged this: "The specific source text in Edwards' writings has not been confirmed. This may be a secondary attribution or condensed paraphrase of Edwards' theology of total depravity rather than a verbatim quotation."

**Recommendation**: Flag in manuscript. Consider attributing as "commonly attributed to Jonathan Edwards" or researching the Temple connection further. Do not use in print without qualification.

### 2. J.C. Ryle — "Nothing darkens the eyes of the mind so much, and deadens the conscience so surely as an allowed sin"

**Attribution**: ✅ VERIFIED

Source: J.C. Ryle, *Thoughts for Young Men*. The full passage reads: "Nothing darkens the eyes of the mind so much, and deadens the conscience so surely, as an allowed sin. It may be a little one, but it is not the less dangerous for all that." Wording in the sermon matches.

---

### 3. Paul Washer — "The question isn't if you accepted Jesus into your heart or not. The question is if he changed it"

**Attribution**: ✅ CORRECT IN SUBSTANCE — likely a paraphrase

The exact wording has not been located in an indexed Washer transcript, but the sentiment is quintessentially Washer and consistent with numerous verified Washer statements. From "What Is the Gospel?": "Yes, but has your heart changed?" The polished version slightly adjusts the wording: "The question is not whether you prayed a prayer or asked Jesus into your heart. The question is whether he changed it."

**Recommendation**: Acceptable for pulpit use. For print, consider qualifying as "Paul Washer has said, in substance..." or locating the specific sermon.

### 4. Baptist Faith and Message 2000, Article V

**Attribution**: ✅ VERIFIED — verbatim

The quoted text matches the official BFM 2000 Article V ("God's Purpose of Grace") word for word. The sermon combines both sub-sections (Election paragraph and Perseverance paragraph) into a single block, which is a faithful representation — both belong to Article V.

### 5. "A pastor over near Orlando" — air-conditioned hell quote

**Attribution**: ⚠️ NEEDS CLARIFICATION

The existing source note in `Sermon - Grace Alone.md` identifies the "pastor out of Orlando" as **R.C. Sproul** (Saint Andrew's Chapel, Sanford, FL / Ligonier Ministries, Lake Mary, FL — both north of Orlando). Independent research also attributes the "air-conditioned hell" quote to **Steve Lawson** ("We've so elevated the love of God over the wrath of God that we've air conditioned hell") and **Albert Mohler** (2010 article "Air Conditioning Hell: How Liberalism Happens"). All three are in the same Reformed circle.

Given the pastor's known affinity for Sproul, the geographic reference ("near Orlando"), and the earlier self-identification in the Sola Gratia sermon, **Sproul is the most likely intended referent** — though the specific quote may have been said by Lawson or Mohler originally. The sermon's wording ("elevated the Love of God over his holiness and wrath") is a slight paraphrase of the known versions.

**Recommendation**: For print, attribute to the specific speaker if identifiable, or use "as one Reformed pastor put it" with a footnote.
