In our third passage, I want to draw your attention to the Greek. You’ve heard me say the Greek phrase panta ethne? Panta is a word you’re already familiar with even if you don’t know it. Ever heard pantheism? This comes from two words, panta, and theos, panta for everything, and theos for God. Pantheism then means those who believe in that nonsense, means that they believe everything is god. There is a good in the microphone, a god in the podium, a god in the pews. 

Or the word Panorama – the everything view.  Literally you can see everything from here. Panta is a word we are already somewhat familiar with. Panta means Every, or all without exception. 

Ethnos is the other important word. It can be used of big groups or small ones. It can be used of Jews, or Samaritans, or gentiles. It means basically every group of people drawn together by tradition or kinship. With no size limits it’s a pretty amazing word because it means at the end of the day, that every people group, every tribe, and every nation will be present for this opportunity in heaven to make much of God, to worship God.

How does that work? I don’t know, that’s not up to me. That’s up to God. But were all going to be there, and all going to be worshiping God together in glory. What a beautiful day that will be. No divisions over big things or small, no headaches, and drama, and troubles. Just all of God’s people, in every single language gathered together worshipping God. But we have a ways to go from here, to there don’t we?
