This week we are looking at another cult that is locally prominent. They have two large facilities in our county. They are the group of that Ben Franklin claimed would always win in the end. They share some heritage with the southern Baptists. 


Lets look at scripture – open to Romans 1:16-24
You remember we talked about the reformation back in October? Well after the reformation a group of people realized that baptism was for believers, and that it should be administered by a believer. They realized that no living person had been baptized properly. So they drew straws, and the one that drew the short straw baptized all the others present in accordance with scripture. This was the start of the group called annabaptists, or rebaptisers. It also marks the beginning of the radical reformation. 

While there was some good from the radical reformation, there was a lot of weirdness too. Not every radical reformer was all bad, but few were all good in terms of the theology they brought to the table. While some were content with returning us to scripture. Others superimposed weird views on top of the scriptures resulting in a whole bunch of cults.  Initially the Unitarians sole focus was on the unity of God. In other words the three, and yet one nature of God. But in the early 1800s they decided the bible was a mess and not worth following. After that they denied the deity of Christ, and the holy spirit. As time wore on, they stepped further and further away from anything that resembled the word of God. Today, its basically at a point where mostly anything goes. Some of them will tell you that you are a god, that you can realize your best potential now. That right thinking and belief about their idea of God will ultimately save you, if you’re good enough. 

When it comes to the deity of Christ – we have the same old heresy of Arius repackaged. You guys remember arius? He was the one that had the audacity to change the text of scripture. In John 1:1 it says “in the beginning was the word and the word was with God and the word was God. Where the English says “was God” the Greek says homousias, which means the same as, of the same substance as, with the same nature as God. Arius added an extra letter and that changed the meaning. Instead of Jesus is the same as God, it became Jesus is similar to, but not the same as God. Like Jesus is somehow fools gold when compared to the gold of God. 

The earliest Unitarian preached that Jesus was just a good person, and “not very much God at all”. 

So while we share a heritage we clung to scripture – the only revealed word of the one true and divine God. They however clung to French renaissance thinking, and allowed that to twist things so that man became the center, rather than God. 

As you might gather from the name, they sort of became the net that caught all of the heresies. We could talk about pelagianism, or the modern belief that man if he’s good enough can earn salvation, that original sin didn’t taint everyone, and so man is still able to choose good or evil. Which is contrary to scripture Eph 2 says we are dead in our trespasses, and sin, we are followers of the world, followers of satan, the prince of the power of the air, sons of disobedience, slaves to the passions of our flesh and by nature children of wrath. How on earth can one be a slave to the flesh, a follower of satan, and dead nasty stinking corpse, and yet still choose good over evil? Apart from the work of God’s grace in your life, eph 2:8 you are incapable of doing anything about this on your own. This is a nod back to creation I think. Genesis 2:7 – I’ll let you turn there, God makes a man shaped sand castle in the damp dirt, and then breathes life into it. Before God breathed into it, it was just dirt. Afterwards it became a living breathing thing – a man, the first man named adam. 

So it is with us before salvation now. Were basically a dead pile of water and dirt incapable of doing anything for our selves on our own, and God breathed his grace into us, and after that we can believe in him. After that we have free will. Before that we are slaves to sin. Children of wrath and servants of the devil. Afrerwards we are followers of Christ. 

Another heresy contained within universal Unitarianism is called abelardianism. In short they deny the trinity. They believe that God the father is one unique God, God the son is another unique God, and God the holy Spirit is another unique God. 

Those of you paying attention know that this conflicts with what I said earlier about Jesus not being fully God. How can they hold to both? I have no idea. That’s why they are Unitarian universalists. Anything goes. Unity is valued above cogency. They believed it was better to go along and get along, than to believe the true things that God has told us about himself in his word.

Further they believe they can give back their salvation, or be so bad as to have lost it. There are lots of different denominations that are not cults that believe this. However, I am hard pressed to find it in scripture. What we see instead over and over and over is that If you truly believe in God, you are in his hand, and unless you (or something) is stronger than God you can never be removed, nor remove your self. 

The most insidious new belief that they hold to is the idea of universal salvation. This is the unscriptural idea that regardless of the religious path you choose to follow, that ultimately all paths lead to the same heaven. In their view then, you could worship satan, and as long as you do so sincerely, and well, you will ultimately not end up in hell with satan, you’ll go to heaven and be with God instead. 

Crazy no? 

Were going to dig into this, and spend some time, and then we will finish with some tips for reaching them.
So IF all roads lead to rome, or to heaven in this case, then there is no inherent right or wrong. If there is no absolute right or wrong then anything is permissible if you believe it to be. In such a system Adolph hitler was justified in what he did, because he believed he was; in like manner, slavery would have been ok also, or any genocide committed any where. 

Whats more, if universalism is true, Christ would not have needed to die. Why would God send his son to die on the cross for us, if everyone is going to get there in the end anyway. What need was there for him to be born in a sheep food trough – if they were going to heaven any way? What need was there for the scourging? He received 39 lashes -  a death sentence minus one – from a cat of nine tails. His insides would have been outside. For what purpose would Christ have endured the humiliation, the false trials, the nailing of his hands and feet to a cross! Why? What did it matter if everyone gets to heaven in the end any way?
