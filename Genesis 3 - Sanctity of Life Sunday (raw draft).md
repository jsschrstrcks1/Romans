## Verification Flags

### Confirmed Errors — Must Correct Before Preaching

- ❌ **"The only thing he said was VERY GOOD" referring to man**: Inaccurate as stated. Genesis 1:31 (ESV) reads: *"And God saw everything that he had made, and behold, it was very good."* The "very good" declaration is God's omnibus evaluation of the entire completed creation on day six — it covers man, land animals, and everything preceding. Man is rightly called the crown jewel of creation (made in God's image, given dominion), but he is not the *only* thing called "very good." A defensible way to preach this: "Man was the capstone of creation — and when God surveyed everything he had made, his verdict was 'very good.'" Do not say man was the *only* thing called that.

- ❌ **"Rashi writes that Eve gets a few things wrong" — including "in the center of the garden"**: Partially correct, but the "center of the garden" attribution to Rashi is not accurate. Rashi's commentary on Genesis 3:3 addresses one addition only — Eve's addition of "neither shall you touch it." Rashi (citing Genesis Rabbah 19:3 and Proverbs 30:6) writes: "She added to the commandment — thus she was brought to detract from it," and records the Midrashic account of the serpent pushing Eve against the tree to demonstrate she didn't die from touching. **Rashi does not flag "in the center of the garden" as Eve's error.** The "center of the garden" description is not itself wrong — Genesis 2:9 places the tree of life "in the midst of the garden" — and what some general commentators note is that Eve named the tree vaguely rather than precisely, but this is not a Rashi teaching. **Correct to: Rashi specifically notes Eve added the "touch" prohibition; remove or re-source the "center of the garden" claim.**

- ❌ **"Gospel of Philip clearly dates to the middle of the fourth century"**: Misleading — conflates manuscript date with composition date. The surviving Coptic *manuscript* was discovered at Nag Hammadi and dates to approximately 350–400 CE, which is 4th century. But the *composition* of the Gospel of Philip is dated by virtually all scholars to the **late 2nd–3rd century** (c. 180–250 CE). Early Christian Writings places it c. 180–250; Wesley Isenberg's standard translation dates it "perhaps as late as the 2nd half of the 3rd century." The text is Valentinian in theology, and Valentinus was active c. 138–158 CE, giving a firm floor. **The language-based dating argument in the sermon applies to the manuscript copy, not the original text — this needs to be corrected or the argument restructured.**

- ❌ **"Gospel of Philip is the first place in recorded history that this serpent seed has been recorded"**: False. Irenaeus of Lyon's *Against Heresies* (c. 180 CE) documents the serpent seed concept as an *already-existing* Gnostic heresy — he attributes a doctrine of Eve committing adultery via the serpent to the Valentinians and Ophites/Sethians. Since Irenaeus *Against Heresies* predates the Gospel of Philip's *composition*, the GoP cannot be the first recorded instance. The *Apocryphon of John* (Sethian Gnostic, c. 180 CE or earlier) also describes the Demiurge sexually seducing Eve and begetting Cain. **The GoP is an early witness to the serpent seed concept, not the originating source. Do not claim it is the "first in recorded history."**

- ❌ **"God made everything perfect" (Genesis 1 and 2 reference)**: The word "perfect" does not appear in Genesis 1–2. Genesis 1:31 says "very good" (ESV: *tov meod*). "Perfect" is theologically defensible as a description of the pre-Fall state but is not the text's language. Use "very good" and let the text speak, rather than substituting a word that isn't there.

### Needs Clarification Before Preaching

- ⚠️ **Gnostic timeline — "around 100 AD, just a little after the last disciple would have died"**: Partially accurate but oversimplified in a way that could mislead. John the Apostle did die around 98–100 CE in Ephesus — that part is traditionally accepted. But proto-Gnostic ideas were **already circulating during John's lifetime**; his own epistles (c. 90–95 CE) appear to be responding to proto-Gnostic disputes within the churches. Fully formed Gnostic schools flourished in the **2nd century** — Basilides in Alexandria (before 150 CE), Valentinus in Rome (c. 138–158 CE). Saying Gnosticism "sprung up" at 100 AD implies it was a reaction to the apostles' deaths, but the reality is more complex. Consider: "As the eyewitnesses began to die off, a movement that had been lurking at the edges crystallized into recognizable Gnostic Christianity in the second century."

- ⚠️ **Serpent seed lineage — "British Israelists → Ku Klux Klan → Aryan Nations → American Nazi Party"**: Substantially accurate in its broad trajectory, but omits the **Christian Identity movement** as the critical organizing bridge — the movement through which British Israelism was racially radicalized and connected to white supremacist organizations. The pivotal individual is **Wesley A. Swift (1913–1970)**, a former KKK recruiter who fused British Israelite theology with virulent antisemitism and racism and founded the church that Richard Butler later renamed Aryan Nations. Swift is also the most important link to William Branham, who popularized serpent seed doctrine in mainstream charismatic circles from 1958. The lineage is right; Swift and Christian Identity are worth naming for precision and credibility.

- ⚠️ **"Philip who died in the first century"**: Traditional and probable, but uncertain. Eusebius documents a Philip with four prophesying daughters dying at Hierapolis, and this is commonly placed in the late 1st century — but Eusebius himself appears to have conflated **Philip the Evangelist** (the Deacon, one of the Seven) with **Philip the Apostle** (one of the Twelve). Scholars still debate which Philip is associated with the Hierapolis burial tradition. Philip the Deacon's death is simply not recorded in the New Testament; his last biblical appearance is Acts 21 (c. 57–58 CE). Saying he died in the first century is defensible tradition but should not be stated as certain historical fact.

- ⚠️ **Diamond cartel — "big diamond conglomerates got together and set up a price fixing scheme"**: Historically accurate in substance but the structure was primarily a **single-company monopoly** rather than a traditional multi-party cartel agreement. The company was **De Beers**, which at its peak controlled up to 90% of the global rough diamond supply through its Central Selling Organisation. De Beers would stockpile diamonds in weak markets and release supply in strong ones to maintain artificially high prices — exactly the mechanism the sermon describes. In 2004, De Beers pleaded guilty in U.S. federal court to price-fixing (specifically a 1994 industrial diamond case involving De Beers and GE) and paid a $10 million fine. A civil class-action settlement paid out $272–295 million to buyers. The illustration is factually supportable — just attribute it to De Beers rather than implying a broad industry cartel.

### Substantially Accurate

- ✅ **γινώσκω (ginosko) in the LXX**: Valid and defensible. Genesis 2:17 LXX uses **γινώσκειν** (*ginōskein*), the present active infinitive of γινώσκω (Strong's G1097) — so the preacher's identification is accurate for the key prohibition verse. Genesis 2:9 LXX uses the cognate adjective **γνωστόν** (*gnōston*). The point that the LXX chose active verbal forms ("the tree of *the knowing* of good and evil") emphasizing the process of coming-to-know is well supported. The "experiential knowledge" distinction between γινώσκω and οἶδα (*oida*) is a well-established point in Greek lexicography (supported by W.E. Vine and standard lexicons) and is a legitimate theological observation — though scholars note the two words sometimes overlap and the distinction should not be overstated.

- ✅ **Rashi on Eve adding "nor shall you touch it"**: Confirmed. Rashi's commentary on Genesis 3:3 explicitly addresses this as Eve adding to the commandment (citing Genesis Rabbah 19:3 and Proverbs 30:6) and records the Midrashic tradition about the serpent using Eve's addition against her. This is a well-attested Rashi teaching.

- ✅ **Philip: one of the Seven (Acts 6), baptized the Ethiopian eunuch (Acts 8), throughout Acts**: All three confirmed by the text of Acts. Philip the Evangelist is named among the Seven in Acts 6:1–7, baptizes the Ethiopian eunuch in Acts 8:26–40, and appears again in Acts 21:7–10 where Paul stays at his house in Caesarea and his four prophesying daughters are mentioned.

- ✅ **Gospel of Philip makes no internal authorship claim**: Correct. The only mention of Philip in the Gospel of Philip is at 73:8 as a character reference. The title appears only in a colophon (end note) added to the Coptic manuscript. All scholars agree the true author is unknown — a Valentinian Gnostic of the 3rd century.

- ✅ **Gospel of Thomas not written by Thomas**: Scholarly consensus firmly supports this. Dating (early-to-mid 2nd century), literary dependence on canonical Gospels, origin in eastern Syria — all rule out apostolic authorship. The language argument the sermon uses is valid as one of several lines of evidence.

- ✅ **John 1:3 — "All things were made through him"**: ESV confirmed: *"All things were made through him, and without him was not any thing made that was made."*

- ✅ **Romans 5:8 ESV**: Confirmed: *"But God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."* (Note: ESV says "shows" — present tense; "while we were **still** sinners" — not "yet." The sermon's gloss uses "yet," which is KJV. Align to ESV when polishing.)

- ✅ **John 3:16 ESV**: Confirmed: *"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life."*

- ✅ **Genesis 2:15–17 ESV**: Confirmed: *"The LORD God took the man and put him in the garden of Eden to work it and keep it. And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, 'You may surely eat of every tree of the garden, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die.'"*

- ✅ **God never commanded them not to touch the tree**: Confirmed by the text. Genesis 2:17 says only "you shall not eat of it" — the "do not touch" is not in God's original prohibition. Eve's addition is in 3:3.

- ✅ **Serpent seed doctrine condemned as heresy**: Confirmed. It has been declared a heresy in Christian tradition. Beyond the white supremacist channels, it was condemned by the church from Irenaeus onward.
