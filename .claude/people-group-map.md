## Notes

- All data extracted from actual sermon files and the sermon map (`sermon-map.md`). No people group data has been fabricated.
- Population figures are approximate and drawn from the sermon texts, which typically cite Joshua Project data. Figures may be dated — verify against current Joshua Project before repreaching.
- The Mo-Jer-Hai (#35) is not found in Joshua Project or other indexed sources. The pastor should verify before reuse.
- Some groups appear in multiple sermons (Balija, Sanaani Arabs, Zaza-Dimli, Dhanuk, Japanese, etc.), indicating sustained prayer focus.
- The Turkish people (#50) are referenced as a national group rather than a specific ethnolinguistic people group, but the prayer focus is on unreached status.
- The Brahmin of Nepal (#10) has minimal detail in the sermon map — listed as people group in the "Out of the Cistern" sermon but without extended description.
- Several groups (Bhat, Malay, Dhangar, Buryat, Kashmiri Pandits) have limited demographic detail in the sermon texts. Data shown reflects what was actually spoken from the pulpit.
- The infrastructure plan estimated ~79 entries. This registry contains 53 confirmed, distinct people groups. The difference is likely due to: (a) some sermon map entries referencing general missions statistics rather than specific people group prayer segments, (b) some entries being national-level references (Turkey, Japan) rather than ethnolinguistic groups, and (c) some sermons mentioning unreached peoples conceptually without naming a specific group for prayer.
