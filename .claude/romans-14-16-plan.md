# Planning Forward: Romans 14-16

## Romans 14: Christian Liberty & Conscience

**Central Theme**: How the justified community handles disputable matters without destroying one another.

**Key Issues**:
- The "weak" and the "strong" in faith
- Food laws and holy days (matters of conscience, not salvation)
- Not passing judgment on one another
- The kingdom is not eating and drinking but righteousness, peace, joy in the Holy Spirit
- Each will give account to God — so stop judging your brother

**Theological Connection**: Flows directly from Romans 13's "love is the fulfillment of the law." Now Paul applies that love to *intra-church* disagreements over non-essentials.

**Potential Illustrations**:
- Church splits over carpet color, music style, Bible translations
- COVID-era conscience debates (masks, vaccines, gathering)
- Historical Baptist debates (alcohol, dancing, card-playing)

**Cross-References**: 1 Corinthians 8-10, Galatians 4, Colossians 2:16-17

**Pastoral Caution**: This chapter is easily weaponized by both sides. The "strong" use it to dismiss the "weak" as legalists. The "weak" use it to bind consciences. Preach both dangers.

## Romans 15:1-13: Christ the Servant of Jews and Gentiles

**Central Theme**: Christ's example of bearing with the weak; the hope of the nations.

**Key Issues**:
- The strong ought to bear with the failings of the weak
- Christ did not please himself (Psalm 69:9)
- Scriptures written for our instruction — that we might have hope
- Welcome one another as Christ welcomed you
- OT quotations celebrating Gentile inclusion (Psalm 18, Deut 32, Psalm 117, Isaiah 11)

**Theological Connection**: The *Christological grounding* for chapter 14. We don't just tolerate one another — we imitate Christ who bore our weaknesses.

**Potential Structure**:
1. Bear with the weak (vv. 1-3) — Christ's example
2. Hope through Scripture (vv. 4-6) — the Word sustains
3. Welcome as Christ welcomed (vv. 7-13) — Jew and Gentile united in praise

**Cross-References**: Philippians 2:3-11, Isaiah 11:10, Revelation 7:9-10

## Romans 15:14-33: Paul's Ministry & Travel Plans

**Central Theme**: The apostolic mission; Paul's ambition, strategy, and requests.

**Key Issues**:
- Paul's priestly service of the gospel (v. 16)
- Ambition to preach where Christ has not been named (v. 20)
- Spain as the frontier (v. 24, 28)
- Jerusalem collection for the poor saints (vv. 25-27)
- Request for prayer: deliverance, acceptance, refreshment (vv. 30-32)

**Preaching Angle**: Often skipped as "travel logistics," but it's a *theology of mission*:
- Go where the gospel hasn't gone
- Build on no one else's foundation
- The Gentile debt to Jewish believers
- Dependence on prayer

**Cross-References**: Acts 19-21, 2 Corinthians 8-9, Matthew 28:18-20

## Romans 16: Greetings & Final Warning

**Central Theme**: The relational texture of the gospel; watch out for divisive people.

**Key Issues**:
- 26+ named individuals — real people, real church
- Phoebe the deacon/servant (v. 1)
- Prisca and Aquila who risked their necks (v. 4)
- Women in ministry: Junia, Mary, Tryphena, Tryphosa, Persis
- Warning against those who cause divisions (vv. 17-18)
- Doxology: "To the only wise God be glory forevermore" (vv. 25-27)

**Preaching Angles**:
1. *The Community of Grace*: These names remind us faith is never solo. Gospel creates family.
2. *The Women of Romans 16*: Without making it a gender-war sermon, note that Paul honors female co-laborers significantly.
3. *The Divisive Danger*: After 15 chapters of unity, Paul warns about smooth-talking deceivers who serve their own appetites.
4. *The Final Doxology*: The letter that began with wrath ends with worship.

**Potential Structure** (Option A - Single Sermon):
- The Named: Why names matter (vv. 1-16)
- The Warning: Avoid the divisive (vv. 17-20)
- The Doxology: Glory to the only wise God (vv. 25-27)

**Potential Structure** (Option B - Two Sermons):
- Sermon 1: "The Family of God" (vv. 1-16)
- Sermon 2: "The Wisdom of God" (vv. 17-27)

**Cross-References**: Acts 18, 1 Corinthians 1:10-17, Jude

## Closing the Series

Consider how to land the plane:

1. **Final sermon on 16:25-27** could be a capstone summarizing the whole letter:
   - "The mystery kept secret for long ages but now disclosed"
   - "Made known to all nations"
   - "To bring about the obedience of faith"
   - "To the only wise God be glory forevermore through Jesus Christ!"

2. This mirrors the opening (1:1-7) and closes the inclusio:
   - Opened with: "the gospel of God...concerning his Son"
   - Closes with: "the only wise God...through Jesus Christ"

3. The final doxology could review the whole journey: wrath → justification → identity → assurance → ethics → mission → community → glory.
