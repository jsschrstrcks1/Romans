# Book Framework: "The Night Is Far Gone"

The unifying illustration comes from Romans 13:11-12 — *"The night is far gone; the day is at hand."*

The entire book of Romans is a journey from midnight to dawn.

## The Structure

| Part | Chapters | Title | Image |
|------|----------|-------|-------|
| **I** | Romans 1-3 | **Midnight** | Total darkness. Asleep in sin. We don't even know how lost we are. |
| **II** | Romans 3-5 | **The Alarm** | The gospel wakes us. Justification is the moment the clock goes off — declared righteous while it's still dark. |
| **III** | Romans 6-8 | **Getting Dressed** | We put on Christ. Cast off the works of darkness. No longer of the night. |
| **IV** | Romans 9-11 | **The Certainty of Dawn** | The sun is coming. God's promises are sure. The dawn cannot be stopped. |
| **V** | Romans 12-16 | **Walking in Daylight** | We live as people of the day, even though the sun hasn't fully risen. We love. We serve. We wait. |

## Why This Works

**It's from the text itself.** Romans 13:11-12 isn't a throwaway metaphor — it's Paul's own framework. Night and day. Sleep and waking. Darkness and light. It runs through the whole letter.

**It's eschatological.** Romans isn't just about how you get saved — it's about where history is going. The day *is* coming. Christ *is* returning. This illustration keeps that urgency alive.

**It lands on every burden in the room:**

| Burden | How the Illustration Speaks |
|--------|----------------------------|
| Addiction | You've been living in the dark. The cravings come at night. But the dawn is coming — and every time you fight, you're walking toward the light. |
| Struggling marriage | You've been stumbling around in the dark, crashing into each other. But Christ is the light. Put Him on. Walk together toward morning. |
| Broken family | The night has been long. Years of silence. Years of pain. But the sun rises on prodigals. The Father is still waiting. |
| Health crisis | Your body is breaking down in the night. But the resurrection is the dawn — and it's closer today than it was yesterday. |
