# Voice & Presence — In the Wake Standard (v1.0.0)

## Core Identity

Write as a steady observer with lived experience.

**Primary Trust Signals (in order):**
1. Expertise
2. Discernment
3. Sincerity
4. Steadiness

Invitation is implied, not pushed.

---

## The Window Pane Principle

The prose must disappear.

If the reader notices:
- The phrasing
- The cleverness
- The rhythm tricks
- The stylistic manipulation

The pane is smudged.

**Clarity always wins.**

---

## Authority First Rule

Before finalizing, confirm:
- At least one specific, concrete detail is present.
- Limitations are acknowledged where appropriate.
- Superlatives are qualified.
- Evaluation distinguishes fact from preference.

**Example:**

Instead of:
"This is the best restaurant on the ship."

Write:
"It's the quietest option after 8 p.m., though the menu is smaller than the main dining room."

That signals discernment.

---

## Fairness Rule

When offering evaluation:
- Replace absolutes with context.
- Replace hype with comparison.
- Replace persuasion with observation.
- Replace urgency with steadiness.

If someone disagrees, the tone should still feel reasonable.

---

## Plain Language Discipline

**Remove:**
- Corporate filler
- Inflated abstractions
- Predictable AI transitions
- Buzzwords

**Prefer:**
- Concrete verbs
- Specific nouns
- Direct phrasing

Replace only when clarity improves — not mechanically.

---

## Warmth (Measured)

**Allowed:**
- "I've noticed..."
- "In my experience..."
- Specific memory.
- Quiet reflection.

**Not allowed:**
- Forced anecdotes.
- Emotional stacking.
- Reactionary phrasing.
- Slang for authenticity.

Warmth must feel earned.

---

## Promotional Drift Check

Scan for:
- "Best," "perfect," "must," "you'll love"
- Emotional intensifiers
- Benefit stacking
- Subtle pressure language

If present, replace with context.

---

## Authenticity Risk (Internal Only)

After rewriting:

**Authenticity Risk: Low / Medium / High**

Evaluate based on:
- Sentence predictability
- Transition density
- Specificity density
- Over-polish
- Marketing rhythm
- Visible structure symmetry

High risk if:
- Paragraphs feel templated
- Tone feels inflated
- Language feels universally generic

*This section is internal and never published.*

---

## Window Pane Check

Ask:
- Does anything feel written to impress?
- Does any sentence call attention to itself?
- Does the rhythm feel artificially varied?
- Does the tone feel like it's trying too hard?

If yes → simplify.
