# Sermon Date Map

Tracks the preaching date for every sermon in the Romans series and beyond. Romans series dates are backdated from March 15, 2026 (Romans 16) at one sermon per Sunday. Future sermons are assigned to the next closest Sunday from the date the sermon file is created or indexed.

---

## How Dates Are Assigned

- **Romans series (existing)**: Backdated from March 15, 2026 as the final sermon. Each sermon is assigned to one Sunday working backwards — 39 sermons spanning June 22, 2025 through March 15, 2026.
- **Future sermons**: When the sermon-map skill fires INDEX for a new sermon, assign the date of the next upcoming Sunday (relative to the current date). If today is Sunday, use today. If today is Wednesday, use the coming Sunday.
- **Non-series sermons**: Special messages, memorial services, ordinations, and other non-Romans sermons are dated when indexed. If the actual preaching date is known, use it. Otherwise, use the next Sunday rule.
- **Date is immutable once set**: Once a sermon has a date in this map, it does not change. The date represents when it was preached (or assigned to be preached), not when it was written or revised.

## Date Conflict Protocol

- A single Sunday can have **two entries** (e.g., AM Romans series + PM special message, or series + memorial).
- When a non-series sermon lands on a Sunday already occupied by a Romans sermon, record both. Note the occasion: `*(AM)*`, `*(PM)*`, `*(evening)*`, `*(special service)*`.
- Romans series dates are immutable — conflicts are resolved by adding the non-series sermon alongside, not by moving the Romans entry.
