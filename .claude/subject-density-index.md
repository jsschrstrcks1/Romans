## Key Observations

1. **Evangelism dominates.** At 20 entries, Missions/Great Commission is the single most developed category -- nearly triple the median. This reflects a clear pastoral priority.

2. **Doctrine is strong.** Election (14), Providence (14), Atonement (13), Sin/Depravity (17), and Salvation (10) collectively form the theological backbone. Reformed soteriology is well-represented.

3. **Ordinances are the biggest gap.** Lord's Supper has exactly 1 entry. For a Baptist church where communion and baptism are the two ordinances, this is the most structurally significant gap in the index.

4. **Practical/ethical categories are thin.** Stewardship (4), Christian Witness (3), Marriage/Family (5), and Anxiety (4) all fall below the median. These are high-demand pastoral topics where congregants seek guidance most often.

5. **Ecclesiology needs development.** Church Membership (4), Shepherding (3), and Lord's Supper (1) together suggest that the doctrine of the church -- a Baptist distinctive -- has room to grow relative to the doctrine of salvation.
