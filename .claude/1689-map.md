## 1689 in the Confessional Framework

`confessional-framework.md` identifies six doctrinal areas the 1689 supplies:

1. **Doctrine of Scripture** — final authority, sufficient, clear
2. **Doctrine of Sin** — total depravity without despair
3. **Doctrine of Justification** — imputed righteousness, once-for-all
4. **Doctrine of Sanctification** — necessary, progressive, Spirit-wrought
5. **Doctrine of Assurance** — real, grounded in Christ, sometimes shaken
6. **Doctrine of Civil Magistrate** — Romans 13 handled soberly, not triumphalistically

Chapter 24 receives special attention: "almost a systematized Romans 13, with Acts 5 and Daniel folded in." The confession holds together three things the preacher is already teaching: (1) God ordains governing authorities, (2) Christians owe obedience in lawful matters, (3) conscience is bound only by God.

## 1689 in the Baptist History Sermons

`Sermon - The Measuring Stick.md` traces Baptist confessional history:
- 1611 — Thomas Helwys (first English Baptist confession)
- **1689 — Second London Baptist Confession** (named and placed in historical sequence)
- Philadelphia Confession
- New Hampshire Confession
- Abstract of Principles
- BFM (1925, 1963, 2000)

The 1689 is presented as a historical milestone in the development of Baptist confessional identity, not merely a theological reference.
