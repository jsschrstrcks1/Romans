# Series Trajectory: Where We've Been

## The Arc of Romans 1-13

| Phase | Chapters | Theme | Key Question |
|-------|----------|-------|--------------|
| **Diagnosis** | 1-3 | Wrath & Rebellion | Why do I need the gospel? |
| **Remedy** | 3-5 | Justification by Faith | How am I saved? |
| **Identity** | 6-8 | Union with Christ | Who am I now? |
| **Assurance** | 9-11 | Sovereign Mercy | Is my salvation secure? |
| **Application** | 12-13 | Living Sacrifice & Submission | How do I live now? |

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

**Romans 1** — God's revelation in creation; humanity's active suppression of truth; the exchange of glory for idols; guilt and deserved wrath established.

**Romans 2** — Religious hypocrisy exposed; clean outside, filthy inside; judgment by conscience; no favorites with God.

**Romans 3** — All unrighteous; none seeking God; God's wrath as holy hatred of sin (Owen: "slight thoughts of sin = slight thoughts of God"); justification introduced.

**Romans 4** — Abraham justified before Law and circumcision; faith as empty hand receiving; the ledger metaphor (debt cancelled, righteousness credited); David's freedom from guilt.

**Romans 5** — Peace with God through justification; access to grace; love demonstrated while we were still sinners; suffering produces perseverance, character, hope.

**Romans 6** — Dead to sin, alive to God; Bannockburn illustration (choosing your banner); no neutral ground; obedience reveals your true master.

**Romans 7** — The war within; Law reveals sin but cannot save; "O wretched man that I am!"

**Romans 8** — No condemnation; Holy Spirit's intercession; predestined, called, justified, glorified; nothing separates from God's love. (Ordo Salutis framework)

**Romans 9** — Paul's anguish over Israel; Jacob and Esau; Pharaoh hardened; "Who are you, O man, to answer back to God?"; sovereignty and mercy.

**Romans 10** — "The Gospel That Reformed the World"; faith comes by hearing; Israel's zeal without knowledge; Christ is the end of the Law.

**Romans 11** — God has not rejected Israel; the remnant by grace; Gentiles grafted in; "Grafted by Grace"; doxology of God's unsearchable wisdom.

**Romans 12** — "The Mountain of Mercy" (Abraham/Isaac parallel); living sacrifice; transformed mind; body of Christ; love without hypocrisy; weep with those who weep; overcome evil with good.

**Romans 13** — "Least popular chapter"; submission to governing authorities under Nero; "be subject" ≠ "celebrate"; taxes, honor, love fulfills law; civil disobedience only when commanded to disobey God.
