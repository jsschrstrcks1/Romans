# John 11 — Lazarus

Story about Becca. Bridge Church Pastor Nathan. Cancer neuro blastoma. In her breast, in her kidney, in her liver, and in her brain. His baby girl is probably going to die. What is his response?

> Count it all joy, my brothers, when you meet trials of various kinds, for you know that the testing of your faith produces steadfastness. And let steadfastness have its full effect, that you may be perfect and complete, lacking in nothing.
> — James 1:2–4

May we be as faithful when the lives of our loved ones are on the line.

Its easy, well easier to consider your own mortality. I went to Pensylvania a few years back and as part of induction into their missionary corps I went through a week long job interview and personality test. First night they started to put us into boxes. What animal are you most like. Cormorant. Trinity – coming to grips with my own mortality.

The second to last day my team member, my friend was taken hostage. Its one thing to consider your own mortality. Its quite another to have a friend being cut up into pieces and delivered to your door. We seasoned believers are ok with giving our lives to and for the Lord, but what about the lives of our kids? Our grandkids? If their death disturbs you, and it should. What about their salvation? Do you know where they are going to go? If you don't, and you aren't bothered by that, then you have a little bit of a heart problem there. Come talk to me later.

That brings us to our passage tonight. John 11:1-53. Were going to be reading about Lazarus.

## Follow Christ Regardless of What May Come (11:1–16)

Vs 4 This illness does not lead to death. It is for the glory of God, so that the Son of God may be glorified through it.

Jesus knew in advance that Lazarus was going to die, and be resurrected. Notice vs 5 Jesus loved Martha and her sister and Lazarus.

Jesus had been going around doing miracles, challenging the authority of the religious leaders, and some thought even Rome. The more he did to demonstrate that God was real, and he was God's son, the more the leaders reviled him. Last week, we read in 10:39 that "Again they sought to arrest him, but he escaped from their hands."

They were playing a game of whack-a-mole. Have you ever played whack-a-mole? Its one of those games wyley and I almost always play at Stop & Play. Except one of them you have to whack alligators, and for the other you have to hit prarie dogs. Both of us working together usually get a pretty good score. But the Pharisees all of them working together still weren't able to catch and arrest Christ. He was public enemy #1. If the FBI had been around back then he would have been #1 on the 10 most wanted list.

The disciples were afraid, but catch Jesus response – when we walk in the light, we don't have to worry about stumbling. (vs 9). In other words, we are doing what God wants, we are walking in accordance to his will, and so come what may, I still will follow.

Check out Thomas's reaction in vs 16 – they had no hope that Christ would survive this trip, but they were still willing to go with him. Better to die with their Lord, he seems to be saying, than to die apart from him. The disciples were willing to follow Christ regardless of what may come. Just as Jesus was willing to follow his father no matter what may come.

## Jesus Wept (11:35)

I don't want to get too bogged down here. But I think we should definitely look at vs 35 for a minute. Can someone read that for me? ESV says "Jesus wept."

Jesus knew why he was there, we saw that back in Vs 4 – he was going to raise his dear friend from the dead. Yet he wept. I think a lot of people see God as one who doesn't understand what its like to be human. He doesn't know what its like to be tempted or experience loss. God doesn't understand the human condition people have said. That's ridiculous. Christ came to earth to be incarnated into a man so that he could save us, but through that process he felt what it was like to be tempted. Not just tempted, tempted by Satan himself. You and I will likely never experience temptation from the devil himself, but Christ did, and Christ overcame. Here in this passage he experienced deep loss when a man he loved like a brother died. One commentator, I think wrongly, wrote that Jesus was crying happy tears, because he knew they were about to worship Him when he raised lazarus from the dead. That too I think is ridiculous. Emotions can be illogical. I think he was feeling the loss, even for a short time, and in conjunction with the mourning going on around him he had compassion on them and wept. It was a moving moment, and Christ is compassionate and was moved. He wept. Knowing he would see his brother again he wept.

DiPronio's daughters in the wake of their grandfathers death. No tears, happy happy happy. Trying to be "super spiritual". Remember when we go through loss its ok to cry, because Jesus wept. We know we will see them again, and so did Christ, and he wept.

## Lazarus as a Picture of Christ — and of Us

So not only should we follow Christ regardless of what it costs, not only does Christ understand and has shared in our emotions, he too weeps. Not only all that, but this is also somewhat of a picture of Christ.

Both resurrected by the power of God given to Christ. Both were dead for at least part of four days. Lazarus had been in the tomb four days (John 11:17). Christ was buried for three days before his resurrection.

Lazarus was also like us. He had a sickness that no matter what he tries, will end up in his death. So too apart from Christ we have a terminal illness – namely sin – that will ultimately result in our death.

Like us, Lazarus needed someone capable of intervening on his behalf. He wasn't capable of saving himself. We are incapable of reaching God on our own, we needed a mediator. A propitiation.

Like us Jesus Christ fully man and fully God came, finding us fully dead (and stinking) he resurrected us.

Eph 2:1-4 calls us sons of disobedience. Says we are by nature children of wrath. Says we (apart from Christ) are dead in the trespasses and sins.

A couple of years ago I was on my way home from somewhere, and found my road cordoned off with police tape. A bunch of cop cars, a coroner, a crime scene unit, a couple of dogs, and the whole 9 yards. The police force was out in force, and blocking my yard. So I drove on the grass, and pulled into mom and dads driveway. Officer came over, wanted to know who I was, and why I was there. I handed him my drivers liscense to prove I belonged.

Come to find out a girl had been murdered in the woods. Apparently someone downwind noticed the smell and called 911. She had been there for several days. She was totally incapable of life. She was exactly like we were apart from God. Turned out that her boyfriend killed her, they caught him, and he's going to be in jail for a long time. But just like the girl, just like Lazarus, just like that little baby girl facing death at 10 weeks old, apart from Christ we are worse than dead, we are bound for an eternity in hell. An eternity separated from God.

## Gospel Invitation

Maybe you're here tonight and you've come to realize you are one of the walking dead. Maybe you have been to church for a while, maybe you're embarrassed, but there is nothing to be embarrassed about. We love each of you. We want to see you again in heaven. Don't trade eternity for a moment of embarrassment.

Some of you, maybe you've gone back to rolling in the filth of the world. Tonights the night to repent and return to God.

While he's singing if you are sitting there stinking because you are dead like Lazarus, or because you as a Christian have been revisiting the sins of your past, I want you to raise your hand, and after the song, we will all as a group say the sinners prayer together.
