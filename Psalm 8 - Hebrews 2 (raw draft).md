## Verification Flags

### Confirmed Errors — Must Correct Before Preaching

- ❌ **"in the Greek"** (Ps 8:1 Yahweh/Adonai discussion): Psalm 8 is Hebrew, not Greek. Correct to "in the Hebrew."
- ❌ **Deuteronomy 20:9** is wrong reference. ESV Deut 20:9 is about appointing military commanders. The "portion and heritage" verse is **Deuteronomy 32:9**: "But the LORD's portion is his people, Jacob his allotted heritage." Must correct.
- ❌ **Caño Cristales / volcano**: The river is Caño Cristales (Serranía de la Macarena, Meta, Colombia). It is *not* in or near a volcano. The region sits atop the Guiana Shield — ancient non-volcanic geology ~1.2 billion years old. The volcano detail is simply wrong and must be removed or corrected.
- ❌ **"filthy rags"**: ESV Isaiah 64:6 reads "polluted garment." "Filthy rags" is KJV. Use ESV wording or explicitly cite KJV.
- ❌ **"Loue key reef"**: Correct spelling is **Looe Key Reef**.

### Unverifiable — Pastor Must Decide How to Handle

- ⚠️ **David Platt / Muslim cab driver story**: No published or indexed source confirms this specific story (Muslim driver, dreams of a baby, converted). Platt has shared similar Muslim-dream stories publicly (SBC context), but this particular account with these details cannot be sourced. Do not attribute to Platt without a specific citation (book/page or sermon date). Consider telling it as a personal account if that is its actual origin.
- ⚠️ **Edwards quote** ("just the sin that made salvation necessary"): Full form widely circulated as "You contribute nothing to your salvation except the sin that made it necessary." This quote does **not** appear in the Yale critical edition of Edwards' works. Possibly misattributed — may originate with a 2013 devotional compilation or with Melanchthon. Do not present as a confirmed Edwards quote without a primary source.
- ⚠️ **MacArthur quote** ("Christians should serve righteousness exactly like they served sin — as slaves"): Cannot be verified as a direct MacArthur quote from any indexed source. It is an accurate paraphrase of Romans 6:18–19. Either attribute to the biblical text directly, or note it as a paraphrase (e.g., "as MacArthur summarizes Romans 6").

### Substantially Accurate — Minor Wording Notes

- ✅ **J.C. Ryle quote**: Genuine. From *Holiness* (1883), p. 204. Exact wording: "There is a common, worldly kind of Christianity in this day, which many have, and think they have enough—a cheap Christianity which offends nobody, and requires no sacrifice—which costs nothing, and is worth nothing." Preacher's version is slightly paraphrased ("today" for "in this day," "offends no one" for "offends nobody") but the substance and core phrases are accurate.
- ✅ **"How He Loves"**: Written by **John Mark McMillan** (2005), not David Crowder. Crowder's well-known cover (2009, *Church Music*) caused widespread attribution confusion; Crowder himself credited McMillan publicly. Noting McMillan as the writer is courteous and accurate.

### Verified Correct

- ✅ Psalm 8:1 — LORD (Yahweh, all caps) / Lord (Adonai, capital L) distinction: correct
- ✅ Hebrews 2:6–9 ESV: confirmed; quotes Psalm 8:4 and applies it to Christ
- ✅ Psalm 8:4 / Hebrews 2:6 parallel: theologically sound
- ✅ Caño Cristales is in Colombia ✓; "remotest parts of Colombia" is fair ✓; multicolored algae description is accurate ✓ — only the volcano detail is wrong

Majesty of God.

People group of the week.

I was reading an article about the most majestic things in the world this week. I read about and saw pictures of this one particular river which nearly dries up at the end of spring and start of the rainy summer period. While the water is low, it grows multiple colors of algae. Algea can't be majestic pastor, I can see it in some of your faces, but this is really astounding. When I say algea you're thinking the brown stuff that washes up on a sea shore, or the green stuff that grows in your aquarium if you don't take care of it. But the reality is much more beautiful. This little pool, in the middle of the still river, had brown – sure, and green. But it also head algea red like fire, and blue like tourquise, and dark blues like the most beautiful ocean scene, it also had yellows like gold, and green like jade. All of the colors of the rainbow in a perfect circle. Set in the mouth of a dormant volcano in one of the remotest parts of Columbia. The balance of colors, and lighting, and the rocks, and crystal clear perfectly still water, is one of those beautiful things that just takes your breath away. Have you ever seen something so majestic it just took your breath away? Id ask for examples, but I'm sure all the husbands here would nudge their wife, and say their wife is one of those things. In googling most majestic things in the world I saw lists of 10, and 100, and 10,000 of the most majestic things in the world. I didn't make it all the way through 10,000. But its safe to say our God has made some amazing things. The great barrier reef. Clouds of tropical fish, giant dramatic coral heads, things that reminded me of Loue key reef down in south florida, we dive there when we can. And those are the kind of things we have seen there as well. Grouper as big as a man, more brightly colored beautiful tropical fish than a team of people could count in a day. It was beautiful. Or if you've ever been out west, arches national park, beautiful rock formations of multiple colors, streatching towards heaven, and reminding us both just how small we are and how big God is. Others talked about man made things. Monet;s water lilies featured high up the list. So did the mona lisa, and certain architectural things like the big gothic cathedrals of Europe like notre dame that by their very nature were designed to remind us both of our place as small finite beings, and God's grandeur and majesty. Ive actually been to notre dame in paris, and its sheer scope is beyond anything I can describe. I had some friends that were chearing its destruction because today its largely a monument to the death of Christianity in france. Its been taken over by the government, and secular humanism, and the worship of mans tradition over God. And yet by its very design it shouts that Jesus is Lord! Scripture says that even the rocks cry out that Jesus is Lord.

If you think Majesty can be summed up In things that man can make, or even just things God makes your view of the majesty of God is shallow indeed.

I read a testimony written by our former director of missions David Platt, He was getting ready to travel for a conference, and had to leave at 3:30 or 4 in the morning to be at the airport on time. So he had to take an uber. He was pretty sure he was going to get mugged. He really wasn't looking forward to the cab ride, and when he walked outside to get in the cab he was still half asleep. But he got in the cab any way. I don't know if you guys have ever taken a cab ride, but I think cabbies are probably the chattiest people in the world. Maybe second to an adult whose been stuck home alone with little kids for too long, and is lonely. You know what I mean? They have that desperation to talk sometimes.

So any way, he gets in the cab and the cabby starts asking questions. Where are you headed, for what? Oh work? What kind of work do you do? And my friend says "I'm a pastor". The cab driver – a muslim - stops right there, and starts weeping. Muslims have an issue with the idea that God would condescend to come to earth and live among us. They just don't think that a real god would want to do that. So this muslim man, whose weeping at 4 something in the morning tells my friend that he has been having this re-occurring dream that this little tiny baby keeps appearing to him every time his eyes are closed and begins talking to him in clear plain language as though the baby were an adult. Telling him he's gotta go find and talk to someone about Jesus. The cab driver says preacher, can you explain this dream to me.

My friend said the adrenalin was pumping, and he was wide awake and began sharing the gospel with the cab driver, and that night, that muslim cab driver became a believer in Jesus Christ, and has gone on to be baptized and become a member of his church.

That transformation that lost people go through, that you and I have been through from death to life is one of the most majestic things in the world. Who else has command of even death but Jesus? Who else?
