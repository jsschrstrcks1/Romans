A WORD TO THE MAN WHO MAY NOT YET BELONG TO CHRIST

You have followed the argument. You may even be persuaded that the doctrine is correct. But you know in your honest heart that you have not bowed to Christ as Lord and Savior.

Hear this plainly. The Christ whose cross was an actual accomplishment is the Christ who calls you tonight to repent and believe. The gospel is not a doctrine to be analyzed. It is news to be received. He saves sinners now. He is willing to save you tonight. Turn from your sin and trust Him.

If you do not know how, ask the brother next to you.

⸻

THEREFORE — WHAT MEN WHO BELIEVE THIS MUST DO

Pray for people by name. Tonight. Write three names down before you leave this room and pray for them on the way home. Daniel did not stop praying because he had read the prophecy. Paul did not stop praying because he had written Romans 9.

Test the sermons you sit under by Nehemiah 8:8. Was the book read? Was the sense given? Did you understand more of God than when you walked in?

Refuse both caricatures. The caricature that says Calvinism is fatalism that cancels prayer is false. So is the caricature that says Calvinism is theological lecture that requires no labor in the pulpit. Walk the narrow path.

Show up for the people who oppose you. Ira went to see Dr. Bloom in the hospital. He was turned away. He went anyway, and he left kindly. That is the gospel shaping pastoral practice.

REFLECTION QUESTIONS

1. Read Daniel 9:1-3. Daniel knew the prophecy was certain and he prayed anyway. Where in your life have you stopped asking God for something because you decided it was already settled?
2. Christ Himself prayed for Peter (Luke 22:32) rather than relying on a bare decree. What does that tell you about your own prayers for the people you love?
3. Where in the past month has your doctrine quieted rather than deepened your prayer life? Be specific.
4. Read Ezekiel 36:36-37. God promises restoration, then tells His people to ask Him for it. How does this change the way you think about sovereignty and prayer?
5. Read Nehemiah 8:8. Was the last sermon you heard faithful preaching by this standard?
6. Acts 20:26-27: What part of the whole counsel are you most tempted to leave out?

GROUP DISCUSSION

* Read Acts 4:23-31 aloud. Walk through verse 28, then read what they pray for next. What does this say to the man who has used sovereignty as a reason to pray less?
* Read Romans 10:1. How do you hold Romans 9 and this sentence together in your own prayers for the lost?
* Read Ezekiel 36:36-37 together. God promises, then commands asking. Does this settle the question for you, or does tension remain?
* Read Nehemiah 8:8. Of the preaching you have sat under in the past year, how much would pass this test?
* Read 1 Timothy 4:16. Which have you watched more carefully: yourself or the teaching? What would change if you watched both?
* For the man who has been theologically confident for years and has not seen new conversions — is it possible that your doctrine has become a reason to not engage?
* For the man who has never been sure whether he himself has been called by God — what did the gospel sound like in this study? Talk to one of the brothers before you leave.

CLOSING REFLECTION

The doctrines of grace are not tested in theological disputes. They are tested in two specific places.

The first is on the knees. A man who believes in sovereignty and does not pray has not understood sovereignty. Daniel prayed after reading the prophecy. Christ prayed for Peter. The early church prayed after confessing predestination. Paul prayed for the lost after writing Romans 9. Ezekiel gave us the principle: God promises, then commands asking. There is no other Reformed pattern in the Bible.

The second test is in the pulpit. A Calvinist preacher who does not labor at exposition has not been faithful to the doctrine he claims. The doctrine demands a workman.

A man who stands the two tests is a Calvinist worth listening to. A man who stands neither has the doctrine without the practice, which is no Christianity at all.

PRAYER FOCUS

"Father, the doctrine has to walk on two legs. Forgive me for the times I have used Your sovereignty as a reason to pray less, and forgive me for the times I have used my theology as a reason to labor less. Make me a praying man. Make me a careful handler of Scripture. Search me, O God, and know my heart."

⸻

FINAL GROUP PRAYER

"Sovereign Lord, who made the heaven and the earth and the sea and everything in them —
You have decreed our salvation, and You have decreed the means.
You have appointed prayer. You have appointed preaching. You have appointed faithful men.
We have wanted the doctrine without the labor, and the assurance without the asking.
Forgive us for both.
Make us men who pray the way Daniel prayed and the way Paul prayed and the way the early church prayed —
who confess Your sovereignty and ask boldly anyway,
who pray for the lost by name as if it mattered, because it does.
Make us men who handle Your word with care —
reading the book, giving the sense, refusing every shortcut.
And for any man among us who has not yet bowed to Christ —
draw him tonight, by the same gospel that drew us all.
We ask in Jesus' name. Amen."
