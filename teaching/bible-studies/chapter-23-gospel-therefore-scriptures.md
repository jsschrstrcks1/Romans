A WORD TO THE MAN WHO MAY NOT YET BELONG TO CHRIST

Before the imperatives, a word for the man in the room who has been listening to all of this from the outside.

You have followed the argument. You may even be persuaded that the doctrine is correct. But you know in your honest heart that you have not bowed to Christ as Lord and Savior. You have studied Him without surrendering to Him.

Hear this plainly. The God who has been described in this study is the God you must reckon with. The Christ whose cross was an actual accomplishment is the Christ who calls you tonight to repent and believe. The gospel is not a doctrine to be analyzed. It is news to be received. He saved sinners then. He saves sinners now. He is willing to save you tonight. Turn from your sin and trust Him.

If you do not know how, ask the brother next to you. He will gladly walk you to Christ. That is the work this study exists for.

THEREFORE — WHAT MEN WHO BELIEVE THIS MUST DO

The doctrine has to walk on two legs. Here is what that looks like in plain terms.

Pray for people by name. Tonight. If you cannot remember the last time you prayed by name for the lost in your life, write three names down before you leave this room and pray for them on the way home. If your doctrine has cooled your prayer, repent and ask again. Daniel did not stop praying because he had read the prophecy. Paul did not stop praying because he had written Romans 9. You have less reason to stop than they did.

Test the sermons you sit under by Nehemiah 8:8. Was the book read? Was the sense given? Did you understand more of God than when you walked in? If your answer is honestly no, do not blame yourself for being a poor listener until you have checked whether the speaker was actually preaching. Sometimes the problem is not the hearer.

Refuse both caricatures. The caricature that says Calvinism is fatalism that cancels prayer is false. So is the caricature that says Calvinism is theological lecture that requires no labor in the pulpit. Walk the narrow path. Truth and grace together. Sovereignty and means together. Doctrine and labor together.

Show up for the people who oppose you. Ira went to see Dr. Bloom in the hospital. He was turned away. He went anyway, and he left kindly when he was asked to. That is the gospel shaping pastoral practice. Doctrine without that is not the New Testament. Whose hospital room would you not visit because of an old grievance? Visit them. Bring nothing but kindness. Pray for them on the way home, even if they would not let you pray for them at the bedside.
