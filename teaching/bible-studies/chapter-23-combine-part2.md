PART 2 — IS CALVINISM THE CURE OF ALL THE ILLS OF THE CHURCH?

Two preachers in the morning failed. Two preachers in the afternoon were faithful. All four were Calvinists. The difference was not in the theology. The difference was in the obedience to what Scripture requires of a man who handles the word of God.

Theology is a foundation. It is not a method. It is not a personality. It is not a substitute for the work the Bible demands.

NEHEMIAH 8 — THE CENTRAL DIAGNOSTIC TEXT

"And all the people gathered as one man into the square before the Water Gate. And they told Ezra the scribe to bring the Book of the Law of Moses that the LORD had commanded Israel. So Ezra the priest brought the Law before the assembly, both men and women and all who could understand what they heard, on the first day of the seventh month. And he read from it facing the square before the Water Gate from early morning until midday, in the presence of the men and the women and those who could understand. And the ears of all the people were attentive to the Book of the Law... They read from the book, from the Law of God, clearly, and they gave the sense, so that the people understood the reading." (Nehemiah 8:1-3, 8, ESV)

Verse 8 is the job description. "They read from the book." The book itself, read aloud. "From the Law of God." The Bible. "Clearly." Plainly enough that the people can hear it. "And they gave the sense." What the passage actually means. "So that the people understood the reading." That is the goal.

Test every sermon you sit under by Nehemiah 8:8. Was the book read? Was the sense given? Did the people understand the reading?

2 TIMOTHY 4 — THE CHARGE BEFORE PAUL'S DEPARTURE

"I charge you in the presence of God and of Christ Jesus, who is to judge the living and the departed, and by his appearing and his kingdom: preach the word; be ready in season and out of season; reprove, rebuke, and exhort, with complete patience and teaching. For the time is coming when people will not endure sound teaching, but having itching ears they will accumulate for themselves teachers to suit their own passions, and will turn away from listening to the truth and wander off into myths. As for you, always be sober-minded, endure suffering, do the work of an evangelist, fulfill your ministry." (2 Timothy 4:1-5, ESV)

The verb is preach. The object is the word. A preacher who substitutes humor for exposition has begun to scratch the itching ears Paul warned about.

⸻

2 TIMOTHY 2:15 — THE WORKMAN WHO HANDLES THE TEXT

"Do your best to present yourself to God as one approved, a worker who has no need to be ashamed, rightly handling the word of truth." (2 Timothy 2:15, ESV)

The image is a workman. Exegesis is work. There is no shortcut. A man who teaches the Bible at his own kitchen table owes the same workmanship as a man in a pulpit. Not the same training. Not the same length. The same care.

⸻

1 CORINTHIANS 2 — THE POWER IS NOT IN THE RHETORIC

"And I, when I came to you, brothers, did not come proclaiming to you the testimony of God with lofty speech or wisdom. For I decided to know nothing among you except Jesus Christ and him crucified. And I was with you in weakness and in fear and in much trembling, and my speech and my message were not in plausible words of wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power, so that your faith might not rest in the wisdom of men but in the power of God." (1 Corinthians 2:1-5, ESV)

The only safe foundation is Christ crucified, plainly preached.

⸻

1 TIMOTHY 4 — LIFE AND DOCTRINE TOGETHER

"Until I come, devote yourself to the public reading of Scripture, to exhortation, to teaching... Keep a close watch on yourself and on the teaching. Persist in this, for by so doing you will save both yourself and your hearers." (1 Timothy 4:13, 16, ESV)

"Keep a close watch on yourself and on the teaching." Both. Not one or the other.

⸻

TITUS 1:9 — HOLDING FIRM AND REBUKING ERROR

"He must hold firm to the trustworthy word as taught, so that he may be able to give instruction in sound doctrine and also to rebuke those who contradict it." (Titus 1:9, ESV)

Two halves of one calling. Instruct in sound doctrine. Rebuke those who contradict it. Both. Together.

⸻

ACTS 20 — THE WHOLE COUNSEL OF GOD

"Therefore I testify to you this day that I am innocent of the blood of all, for I did not shrink from declaring to you the whole counsel of God." (Acts 20:26-27, ESV)

The whole counsel. Not the favorite parts. Not the comfortable parts. Paul did not shrink. The shrinking is the problem.

⸻

THE MARKS OF FAITHFUL PREACHING

Exposition of the text (Nehemiah 8:8). The whole counsel of God (Acts 20:27). Christ crucified at the center (1 Corinthians 2:2). Sound doctrine paired with the willingness to rebuke contradiction (Titus 1:9). The work of a workman (2 Timothy 2:15). Public reading of Scripture before exhortation and teaching (1 Timothy 4:13). A life and a doctrine watched together (1 Timothy 4:16).

CONSCIENCE APPLICATION — PART 2

The Entertainer Trap. If the laughter carries the room and the text carries nothing, that is a problem. Read Nehemiah 8:8 again.

The Quote-Collector Trap. Puritans are witnesses, not pulpits. A sermon that consists of an hour of quotations has not preached. Read 2 Timothy 4:2 again. Preach the word.

The Lecture Trap. A theological dissertation is not a sermon. Read 1 Corinthians 2:1-2 again. Paul refused the lofty speech.

The Fatalism Trap. Preaching aimed at no one in particular, because "God will do what God will do." Read Acts 20:26-27 again. Paul did not shrink. The whole counsel includes the urgency.
