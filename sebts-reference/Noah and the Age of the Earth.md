Noah


Noah and the Age of the Earth
Dr. Ken Keathly
Student #000210914 
Ken Baker 
Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary, November, 2013
Speakers:
Dr. Gregg Davidson 
	Dr Davidson began his education with a Bachelors of Science from Wheaton College in Geology in 1985. He then proceeded to earn his Masters of Science from the University of Arizona in hydrology in 1989 and concluded his studies with a Ph.D from University of Arizona in Hydrology in 1995. He has taught and done research at University of Arizona, researched with Hargis & Associates; is an environmental firm in Tuscon. Currently he is a professor at University of Mississippi teaching Geology and Geological engineering. He is also holds the chair for the Geology and Geological engineering department. He is currently certified and Registered as a “Registered Professional Geologist” by the Mississippi State Board of Registered Professional Geologists. He is a member of over a dozen scientific and professional societies. Furthermore, he has been published in both books and scholarly journals over a dozen times in the past 5 years.
Outline of His Arguments:
Christians have apriori assumptions regarding the age of the earth
These can be unhelpful
These can be hurtful
These can make people feel deceived
We must set these assumptions aside and look past our existing paradigm to see if there is worth in the paradigms of others who may “know” better than we do. 
We are preachers and missionaries not scientists
Bonafides (Scripture)
Bible Inerrant, infallible, authoritative word of God (as long as its not talking about something science disagrees with)
Deity, bodily resurrection of Christ
Necessity of Christ for salvation
Reality of Miracles
(general) history of biblical narritives.
Everything created from nothing
Historical Adam and Eve
Genuine First Sin
Historical flood
Bonafides (Science)
Science does not and never will trump scripture
Science is useful but not required to understand the central point of a scripture
Science may well be much more helpful in understanding the secondary points of a scripture.
Rightly understood the Bible and Nature are not ever going to be in conflict
Chief aim of science is to understand the rational order that has been imposed upon it by God ~Kepler
Why are we here?
Love for the Bride of Christ
Concern of stumbling blocks to the mission of the church. 
Biblical worldview = Young earth
Humanistic worldview = ancient earth
Biblical worldview evidence = very strong (misconception based upon cherry picking evidence)
Ancient earth view evidence = very weak(misconception based upon cherry picking evidence)
The argument should not be humanism Vs biblical worldviews
Causes young people to stumble
Better to ask: “Is it the intention of scripture to instruct on the nature of nature?
Does the Bible make use of the common understanding of the nature of nature of the time period to reflect and communicate truths RE the nature of God and our relationship with him?
Scripture is not a science text.
Scripture does not intentionally speak on issues of science. 
Where it speaks on science – if it conflicts with what we know from science we can disregard the “science” aspects and retain the “moral” parts of the scripture.
Scripture is wrong on occasion where it speaks on science
Seeds don’t die
Some seeds are smaller than mustard seeds
