# Service — Kim's Funeral
## Psalm 23

---

At the request of the family, we will be reading out of Psalms 23 today. While you are turning there if you've brought your bibles, and I hope you have, I want to talk about the psalm itself. It has a lot of depth, its one we could spend a month of Sundays talking about without fully plumbing its depths. I'm going to try and keep my part short, and still do justice to the psalm.

This passage reminds me a lot of Kim for a lot of reasons. Lets read it:

---

Lets start with **The Lord is my shepherd.** Some of you that know me, know I have a flock of about a dozen sheep. I love having sheep. The little ones are so cute running across the fields in spring. They are just figuring out how to run by then, and their back ends sometimes get away from them, and they end up turned around backwards while they run. It's the cutest thing. But sheep aren't smart when it comes to a lot of things. They need protected from predators. Their instinctual response will get them killed more often than not. Some of them will eat things that can make them sick. Looking at the second verse gives you another insight into sheep. The shepherd makes them lay down by the still waters. Sheep for all their spring time cuteness, don't always know when and where to rest. They have to be guided to safe comfortable places, and made to lay down, sometimes.

That makes me think of Kim a little bit. She was always doing something. Always working. She helped keep the church clean for a while, and on Sunday mornings she would get here about an hour early and help get things started. She was always quick to make coffee for everyone – good coffee too. She'd almost always apologize for it each week also. *Sorry I don't think I got the coffee right this week* she would say. But I never heard anyone complain.

She also would bundle coupons for people. Rolled up and kept together with a rubber band, she kept probably half the church supplied. If Kim liked you, she would do just about anything for you. The one thing I didn't see Kim do much of was rest. Unless it was after 7:30 or so in the evening. Or before about 4 in the morning. Otherwise she was as busy as an ant doing a little of this or a little of that. Getting ready for a garage sale, or doing some house cleaning. She was quick to offer help to anyone she liked.

---

**Vs 3 says that "He restores my soul."** I'm not sure how many people she told about this, but when she was younger she had been a Sunday school teacher. Each week she would prepare a lesson for kids, and would teach them about the Lord. Despite the darker roads she traveled later in life, I believe one day those of us that know Jesus Christ as our Lord and Savior will see Kim again. Coming back to church was a fairly recent thing for Kim. Maybe the last 9 or so months of her life. I know she made a big impact on us here. We will miss her warm smiles, and peanut butter chocolate cake. We will miss her willingness to help, and the love she had for us, and the Lord.

She wasn't one to express her love with words. She would say she loved you through her actions. I doubt there was anyone living she loved more than her son David. She would keep his bed made, and do many of the chores he couldn't do. She'd cook, and clean, and bicker and fight with him. A wise man once told me that love and anger are close to each other. I think that he meant both require passion and strong emotion. That both often go hand in hand, the people you love most, sometimes, you get the most angry with. She loved the Lord also. I remember several times while preaching, I would look out over the crowd, and see Kim mopping the tears from her eyes while we were talking about Jesus. Or the weight of our sin.

---

Kim's story is like the parable that Jesus told.

> "See that you do not despise one of these little ones. For I tell you that in heaven their angels always see the face of my Father who is in heaven. What do you think? If a man has a hundred sheep, and one of them has gone astray, does he not leave the ninety-nine on the mountains and go in search of the one that went astray? And if he finds it, truly, I say to you, he rejoices over it more than over the ninety-nine that never went astray. So it is not the will of my Father who is in heaven that one of these little ones should perish."
> — Matthew 18:10, 12–14 (ESV)

Kim was that one sheep. She came into the fold, and then got lost wandering down some side roads. But God loved her so much, he – like the shepherd in the parable – God pursued her, and brought her back into the fold. Kim couldn't have ever done anything that was so bad that God couldn't save her, there is no path so dark that God can't find you. There is nothing you can do that's so bad, that Jesus can't save you. There is hope for everyone, love for everyone, peace for everyone, security for everyone. Scripture says all who call on the name of the Lord shall be saved.

I've known Kim for probably close to 25 years, and if there is one thing that I KNOW for a fact about Kim its this – that she will want to see all of you in Heaven again one day. Your sister loved you, your aunt loved you, your cousin, or friend, or niece loved you. David, your mom loved you and she wants to see each of you again in heaven one day.

---

**Vs 4 says "even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I shall fear no evil. For you are with me, your rod and staff they comfort me."**

A shepherd's tools are used for a lot of things. The staff – or you may have heard it called a shepherd's crook – is used to pull a sheep back from trouble. If a sheep starts to wander, you grab it with your crook, and pull it back towards you, then you can give it medical attention, or shear the wool off, or take care of whatever the sheep needs. Most sheep tend to run away from help offered. Likewise the rod is used in protection of the sheep. If a hungry predator comes around, you use the rod like a baseball bat, and protect your flock from the predator. The rod and staff are the tools of God's help, and compassion, and care, and security for us. When we are hurting, God uses his staff to pull us close, and comfort us. When we are in danger, God uses his rod to protect us. When we go through these struggles of various kinds, God is there for us, to help us, to care for us, to protect us even from ourselves. To the point he was willing to send his son to take our death on the cross.

---

There are more than two kinds of people in this church today, but there are two kinds that I want to talk about right now.

Firstly, there are those – who like Kim are believers in Jesus Christ, they are part of God's flock of sheep. But they maybe – like Kim – have wandered away. I want you to know today that God is calling you back.

The second kind of person, that I think is here today, is someone who has not yet ever been a part of God's flock. Maybe you don't understand what it all means. Maybe you just know you want to see Kim again one day. God wants you to know this.
