### 9. Strengths (Top 3)
1. **Accurate Scripture reproduction.** The text of 1 John 1:1–7 is quoted precisely and faithfully, including versification.
2. **Accessible weekly pacing.** The seven-day breakdown is well-designed for incremental retention — a genuinely useful discipleship tool.
3. **Covers theologically rich ground.** The selected passage contains the incarnational prologue, fellowship with the Father and Son, and the light/darkness contrast — all worthy of deep memorization and deeper exposition.

### 10. Critical Weaknesses (Top 3)
1. **This is not a sermon.** It contains no thesis, no argument, no exposition, no application, and no exhortation. Submitting it for sermon evaluation is a category error.
2. **The gospel is structurally absent.** Even as a brief devotional note attached to each memory passage, there is no Christological comment, no cross reference, no grace note. The passage being memorized includes fellowship with the Father and Son — that language cries out for gospel comment and receives none.
3. **No differentiation of hearers.** A memorization exercise that never asks "what does this mean?" leaves the person who memorizes it with words but no understanding of why those words matter to them specifically.

### 11. Blind Spots
The preacher may assume that because the Scripture itself is powerful, providing it in a memorization format is sufficient homiletical work. It is not. The Word of God is sharper than any two-edged sword, but it must be wielded — explained, applied, pressed home. Memorization serves preaching; it does not replace it.

### 12. Illusion of Strength
The presence of Scripture text throughout this document may give the impression that it is theologically substantive. It is not. Reproducing God's Word verbatim is not the same as proclaiming it. A parrot can repeat Scripture. A herald must open it.

### 13. Missed Opportunity
1 John 1:1–7 is among the most theologically compressed passages in the New Testament. It contains: (1) the incarnational eyewitness claim that grounds all Christian epistemology; (2) the declaration that fellowship with God is the goal of proclamation; (3) the radical statement that "God is light, and in him is no darkness at all"; and (4) the ethics of walking in the light as the test of genuine fellowship. Every one of these ideas is a sermon in itself. None are touched.

### 14. Surgical Fixes
- **Rebuild this as a devotional or sermon companion, not a standalone submission.** Attach a brief expository paragraph to each week's memory block explaining the theological freight of those verses.
- **Add a gospel note to Week 1.** "The Word of life" in 1:1–2 is the incarnate Christ — the eternal Son who entered time and was handled by human hands. That claim is the scandal of Christianity. Say so.
- **Add an application question to each week.** "If God is light (Week 3), where in your life are you walking in darkness this week?" Turns memory into examination.
- **If a full sermon on this passage is intended, start over with a thesis.** State the main point: "John grounds all Christian fellowship and ethics in the eyewitness proclamation of the incarnate Word of life." Then build exposition, application, and exhortation from that center.
