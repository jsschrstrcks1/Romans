# Be a Seawall
## A Sermon on 2 Timothy 1:3-14

---

## Opening

We talked last week about the state of the fledgling church as Paul was unjustly arrested and faced execution. People all over Asia were turning away, people Paul had known and discipled were turning away. People that Paul had written about in positive terms in other parts of the Bible were having a crisis of faith in this part of the Bible.

This is the last letter that Paul ever wrote, and the things it contains are his last requests of the church. Of Timothy.

It strikes me that some of the language Paul uses here may indicate that even Timothy was wavering. Warning him not to be afraid, or ashamed, and to fan his spark into a flame.

We don't know the exact nature of the letters that Timothy wrote, but it was likely a reflection of the same pressures Paul was facing. Paul went from rock star to parasite overnight. Maybe that made the results Timothy had been experiencing dry up, maybe that made him question his gifts or his calling, maybe because of the lack of success. We're all left to speculate here, but it's reasonable to think that Paul's troubles would be visiting Timothy who Paul loved like a son, too. Maybe Timothy's top lieutenants were jumping ship too. Maybe.

All we know is Timothy gets this response from Paul that sort of feels like a sea wall. You guys know what a sea wall is right? Usually a construction of bricks, concrete and rebar built along the edge of a person's property intended to stop erosion. Because without a sea wall as the tide rises the sea water comes up into people's yards, and kills their most fragile plants, and washes away some of the top soil. Erodes foundations under buildings, and especially the bank of whatever body of water is in question. The sea dune, the river bank, the lake shore, whichever, as the water recedes it washes it away.

One of my neighbors on the creek my family has lived on for the last six decades or so lived on a narrow part of the creek, just downstream of the spring that feeds it. It's narrow, and shallow, and when my sister and I were little we called it the "rapids."

In truth, it's not very fast, but it is faster than anywhere else in the creek system, so it fit. Anyway, my neighbor mentioned a while back that on paper his property line which was drawn in the 50s, is about 15 feet from where the dry land starts now. He had no sea wall, and as the water rose, and fell, and the spring pushed water through the "rapids," it washed away feet upon feet of canal bank. He purchased the land 5 or 10 years ago, and when he realized where his property line had originally been, he immediately had a seawall built, to stop the erosion.

Paul wants to build a seawall not just around Timothy, but around those Timothy is serving, indeed around the whole fledgling church. A reminder to hold fast. The superscription in my Bible says "Guard the deposit entrusted to you." Or — Be a Seawall.

John MacArthur writes in his commentary that Paul is trying to instill in Timothy the kind of holy boldness that will help Timothy advance the gospel. To help him persevere in the face of increasing hostility.

Let's read our passage, and then I want to go back to that thought of increasing hostility. 2 Timothy 1, verse 3.

## Increasing Hostility

Increasing hostility. You guys know that for the last number of years I've tried to be less plugged into world news. I know enough to know who I want to vote for, but I try and stay away from the near constant barrage of awful and twisted untrustworthy news that much of the media puts out. However, even I am aware that hostilities have been increasing the last few years between both sides political. I even had a situation a year ago where someone I love posted things calling Christians hateful bigots, monsters, and hypocrites. Very much in the tone of "down with the Christians." I responded trying to be gentle, and asked her to explain to me how I was being a bigot, and to diffuse some of the things that the article she shared was saying. Naturally, she blocked me. This kind of sentiment is unfortunately all too common in the world today. Someone I was talking to this week has been doing a deep dive into American history and was reading the editorials people were sending into newspapers in the build up to the Civil War, and the mentality, and accusations were largely the same.

In fact in an article written by the Prince of Liechtenstein, he mentions that there is a link between a rise in Marxist thinking, and the decline of church attendance. Where church attendance, and Bible reading, and gospel sharing, and public worship thrive, Marxist thinking is on the decline.

He came to different conclusions. But I agree with a lot of his observations. Including that where much of the tensions between God and government exist, one doesn't have to look far to find a Marxist thinker in the vicinity.

Marxism of course, was the motivation behind such things as Pol Pot's massacre in Cambodia where he murdered about a quarter of the population. If you've ever read or seen the movies about the killing fields of Cambodia you already know that UNICEF estimates there were nearly 3 million people executed and buried in tens of thousands of mass graves. Perhaps as many as 3 million more starved to death. Families broken apart and sent to reeducation camps, from which most never returned.

Things got so bad, that Vietnam stepped in and put an end to the Khmer Rouge.

You've heard of people clubbing baby seals? They were clubbing baby Cambodians in a similar fashion. There are signs of infants having been swung by the feet into trees with the intention of cracking their skulls open.

It's a horrifying, dark, and ugly chapter of world history. Likewise, others in the Marxist maniac tyrant hall of fame would include people like Stalin who planned — planned — he planned — a mass starvation largely in Ukraine, but in other parts of Russia and China, a mass famine. A mass starvation event. He intentionally starved 100 million people to death. I could go on, but the prince cited a statistic that more people have been killed by people who subscribe to Marxist thought than any other thought line across history. One of their consistent actions has been to destroy any who stand against them, especially the church. Mohler in his podcast called The Briefing talks frequently about Marxism, and how Marx says he's trying to bring about communism. And that even those who were eyebrows deep committed communists wrote "utopia seemed never to arrive. Indeed rather than to deliver on human promises, it delivered human misery, it delivered repression, it delivered poverty, it delivered famine and starvation."

When we shift our hope from God to any man made ideology away from Scripture and God, we are on unsteady soil, and should not be surprised when our dependence on man has caused us to run aground.

I don't care if that's Marxism, Communism, Liberalism, Progressivism, Green Party, Independent Party, Democrat, Republican, Conservative, Libertarianism, or Tea Party — every position we hold we ought to be able to prove from Scripture, and should be held in submission to the Word of God. The Word of God should wash our politics, and wash our discourse about politics. The Word of God is our sea wall. The key here is that any philosophy or position, or elephant or donkey put ahead of God will ultimately lead to the same Godless place. Our hope isn't in some elephant or donkey. Not this party or that, not this philosophy or that. Not this person or that person. Our hope, our ONLY HOPE is in God; and the beautiful thing about a seawall written by God — God never changes. His Word never changes. If you're going to build your life around anything, it shouldn't be elephants or donkeys, it should be the unchanging perfect Word of God. A house built on the rock will stand, but a house built on the sand will fall when the rains fall and the floods come.

I heard Al Mohler speaking on a passage in Malachi one time say "the prophet Malachi quotes God as saying all through his book that 'I do not change, therefore you are not destroyed.'"

It was a word of God to his people to say, if I did change, then you would be destroyed because I would be displeased with you to the extent that I would violate my own covenant and have nothing more to do with you. The only reason why you continue as my covenant people is because even as you change in the fleetness of your heart and in the superficiality of your worship, I do not change. Now, theologians refer to this as the immutability of God. That goes back to the Greek language. What it means is God can't change. He doesn't change. And he tells us that that's a part of his perfection. It's a part of the infinity of his perfection that he cannot change.

## Fan Into Flame

So verse 6 — fan into a flame the gift of God which is in you. Every single one of us has strengths and weaknesses. I'll be honest, cleaning is not my strength. Before Carol came it was a chore, and I helped with it, because it needed to be done, and because even though I don't like to clean, I like things being clean. Even though I'm not great at it, I recognize its importance, right? So what a blessing when someone like Carol comes along, and she finds fulfillment and purpose in things like cleaning the church. I'd much rather spend that time in study, or preparation to preach, or counseling, or doing ministry things. God gave Carol that gift among others, and it's a beautiful thing. I think if you asked her it's less about the vacuuming and more about honoring God, and filling a need that the church has, and making sure the building isn't a distraction for those who are lost that come through these doors, than it is specifically enjoying cleaning.

But we have that in other areas. Someone joked one time that they were a jailhouse singer — that is, they were behind a few bars and can't find the key. That's true of me much of the time. It's a good thing it isn't me being amplified and broadcast out there. I'm glad it's those of you who can and are interested in learning more about it. Praise the Lord for it. Where I'm weak, God has raised up others in our midst that are strong. We pull the load together. We share the gospel with people. We see kids being discipled every week. Adults growing in their faith — it's a beautiful thing. This passage is talking about spiritual giftings. Things like — and this is not an exhaustive list — things like: administration, mercy, leadership, giving, exhortation, helps, serving, and teaching. We should encourage each other to use our gifts. Fan those sparks into flames. Help people identify where they are gifted, and get plugged in. Help them develop their gifts.

## The Treasure We've Been Entrusted With

But not only that — fan the flames of the gospel. We have been entrusted with a priceless treasure. The knowledge of the gospel. The knowledge of salvation.

Think about it for a second. Imagine we're in the dark in a long train tunnel. Have you ever been through one of those? Not the quick short ones. But the long dark ones that are so long the horizon bends and you can't see the entrance or the exit from the middle.

Going under the English Channel was like that from London to Paris. We got on the train, and it headed east. Across fields, and under wind turbines, through towns, and through hills, until it went underground. Then we spent over an hour underground before we popped up on the other side, and traveled through French fields and towns. But imagine for a moment that instead of coming out the other side, there was a train wreck. I read about something similar happening, a train passage through a mountain, a train derailed and caught fire. The train was really long, and on fire in multiple places, but one direction you had to travel in it far enough that you'd have died from the smoke and fumes before you found freedom. But if you run the other direction, you can escape to fresh air, and in a mile or two, freedom. Now imagine the confusion, and the smell of smoke. The heat. Imagine the fear. Do I go this way, or that way? Which way leads to freedom, and which way to death? The power is out. It's deep enough underground the only light you see is that from the fires at both ends. Which way do you go?

As Christians we have been told which way to go. Jesus has been there, and came back to tell us about it. Not only the way to safety, but all of the things in the Bible we have been entrusted with. Not just to take into our hearts, but to protect and inform the next generation of. The United States keeps in its strategic gold reserve 8,100 tons of gold, more than 16 thousand pounds. But that is a garbage heap compared to the value of the treasure we have been entrusted with. That is the knowledge of relationship with the King of Kings and Lord of Lords. Relationship with Jesus Christ who died for us to take on our sin debt.

Paul wrote in Philippians 3:8-9 —

> Indeed, I count everything as loss because of the surpassing worth of knowing Christ Jesus my Lord. For his sake I have suffered the loss of all things and count them as rubbish, in order that I may gain Christ and be found in him, not having a righteousness of my own that comes from the law, but that which comes through faith in Christ, the righteousness from God that depends on faith.
> — Philippians 3:8-9 (ESV)

We deserved sin and death and hell and Satan. But what did we get instead? A treasure of all surpassing worth.

## All Is Rubbish

Paul was a powerful man as a Pharisee. He wasn't hungry. People wanted to network with him. If Who's Who had existed back then his picture would have been near the top. Wealth, and position, and authority were his. Respect? He had it! I'm sure all the mothers were trying to marry their daughters off to him.

Yet what does he say? Everything is rubbish. His position with the Pharisees was rubbish. Money? Rubbish! Breeding? Rubbish! Authority and power, all are rubbish. All compared to the surpassing worth of knowing Jesus.

Donkey? Rubbish — and before you say it, I know some of you out there are about to drop an amen — the elephant? He's rubbish too. We're with Jesus or all is lost. Jesus is our all or all we have is nothing.

---

## Closing

Do you know him today? If not you're like the guy from the train story. Lost in the dark, and smoke of this world, and in need of directions.

Those can be found right here in Scripture. Part of our treasure.
